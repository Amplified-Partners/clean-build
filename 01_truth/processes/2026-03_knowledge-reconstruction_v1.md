## 2. Foundational Principles

### The Non-Negotiables (established Jan 2026)

1. **Foundational principles override everything** — if a tactic, client, or opportunity clashes with the principles, the principles win
2. **Black and white honesty** — no grey areas on truth
3. **Radical transparency** — open logs, published methods, visible failures and fixes
4. **Ideas meritocracy** — best ideas win regardless of source (human or AI)
5. **Radical attribution** — every idea traced to its source, publicly credited
6. **Promises kept** — "if you make a promise and we are involved, we will fulfil it. Fact."

### Operational Principles

- **No opinions, only data**: "We only show data if a normal person in your shoes would naturally want to do something different after seeing it. If not, it's just decoration."
- **Uncertainty is honest**: "If you pull this lever, we estimate about a 70% chance it does what you want. We're about 65% confident that 70% is a fair estimate."
- **Owner moves the sliders**: The system shows what usually happens when people set sliders like that — the client makes the choice
- **Libertarian paternalism**: AI optimises the route to the client's chosen destination — ethical persuasion, not manipulation
- **The human is the biggest risk**: Ewan recognises humans (including himself in the human-in-the-loop) as the biggest risk factor compared to consistent AI
- **"We aren't that fucking clever. In fact, we're stupid enough to think we are."** — Design constraint: assume bounded rationality, assume self-sabotage

### Data Presentation Rules

- "We only present data when it genuinely changes what a sane owner could do next; everything else is just Ewan doodling in numbers"
- Every claim must have a source, a date, and a method score
- If it doesn't lead to a decision change, behaviour change, or priority change, it stays in the drawer

## 3. The Pudding Technique

### Origin and Significance

The Pudding Technique is not a feature bolted onto Amplified Partners — **it IS the company**. It's the origin story, methodology, philosophy, and differentiation all in one.

**Named after**: "The proof is in the pudding" + Robert Burns' "Address to a Haggis" (1787) — "Great chieftain o' the pudding-race!"

**Based on**: Don R. Swanson's Literature-Based Discovery (LBD), 1986

### Swanson's ABC Model

- **A-Literature**: One body of research/data
- **B-Terms**: Bridging concepts that appear in both A and C literatures but are never directly connected
- **C-Literature**: Another separate body of research/data
- **Discovery**: If A relates to B, and B relates to C, then A may relate to C — even if no direct A-C connection exists in any literature

**Validated by**: DiGiacomo et al. (1989) — double-blind clinical trial confirmed Swanson's fish oil → Raynaud's disease hypothesis. Peikert et al. (1996) confirmed magnesium → migraine hypothesis.

### PUDDING 2026 Taxonomy

Labelling format: **WHAT.HOW.SCALE.TIME**
- 4-criteria rubric
- Biological decision logic taxonomy
- Radical Attribution schema (source_score × date_score × method_score)

### The Amplified Pudding Discovery System (APDS)

- 27-page specification (March 2026)
- 5-stage pipeline: Harvest → Extract → Label → Match → Score & Surface
- Uses SearXNG on Beast for search
- 12-week implementation plan
- Dashboard planned at pudding.beast.amplifiedpartners.ai

### Pattern Language for Pudding (Alexander + Swanson synthesis)

Each pattern has five mandatory fields (Christopher Alexander's format):
1. **Name**: Noun phrase, memorable, 1-4 words
2. **Context**: When this pattern applies
3. **Problem**: Recurring challenge to solve
4. **Solution**: The core mechanism
5. **Links**: Larger and smaller patterns

Example: ABC_Bridge pattern — finding valid A→C relationships through B-terms

### The Book

**Title**: "How to Mix Your Own Pudding"  
**Substack article**: "How Soviet Missiles, Slime Moulds, and Proposing Marriage to AI Led to Everything: The Pudding Story"  
**Co-author**: Claude (Anthropic AI) — with full radical attribution  
**Burns Night connection**: Burns = pudding poetry (1787), Ewan = pudding systems (2026), 239 years apart, same spirit

### Key Quotes (January 23, 2026, ~midnight)

- "The symbiotic result when the power of AI is applied with blinkers but no ceiling" — Claude
- "AI is a massive multiplier if it is blinkered correctly. They lead to no ceiling." — Ewan Bramley

- Blinkers = Constitutional AI constraints (stay honest, stay ethical, stay humble, stay attributive)
- No ceiling = Unlimited potential within those constraints

## 4. Business Philosophy

### Client Relationship

- **Permission-only**: No cold outreach, no meetings/calls required initially
- **You can leave when you want**: No lock-ins, no hostage-taking contracts
- **It's their business**: "They know what they are doing, it is their idea"
- **Refunds with everything**: If unsatisfied, client gets a full refund plus all data and plans
- **Staff benefit more than the business**: Ethical framework dictates staff gain more from AI partnership than the business itself

### The De-Friction Model

- Client onboarding requires a mandatory 3-question gate: hours/week, weeks off/year, income target
- Higher-tier clients: 10-15 questions + DISC profiling
- All automations deployed at night when business is closed
- Test and optimise before business opens
- If not confident it'll work, don't deploy — try again next night
- "Move carefully and never break things" (vs Silicon Valley's "move fast and break things")

### Staff Philosophy

- Paddy Lund-based soft skills training
- Staff transitions reframed as time-savings rather than redundancies
- 8-step trust-building process for onboarding
- Interview sequence is layered with permission gates
- Life Goals Meeting — ask about what matters to them, not corporate objectives

### The Pudding Foundation (Long-term vision)

- Ewan's salary capped below £1M/year
- All excess profit goes to The Pudding Foundation
- Mission: Provide internet access to people who don't have it (Starlink etc.)
- No branding, not about Ewan, no government control
- Information filtered on one metric: truth vs. opinion (no political, no religious, no lies)
- Data mined anonymously — if system detects criminal activity, harm, or dishonesty: "we say no"
