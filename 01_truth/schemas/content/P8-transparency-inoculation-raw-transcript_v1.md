## VERBATIM QUOTES — P8 RELEVANT

### Quote P8-001: The Creepy Line
**Session:** Session 2, 2026-03-15
**Context:** Ewan articulating the core risk of the system — the point at which support intervention crosses from helpful to surveillance-feeling. This is the defining quote for P8 and the reason the entire transparency architecture exists.
**Verbatim:**
> "If you hit it just at the right time when they're like, 'Have you been listening to me?' you're in trouble."

**Interpretation note:** The critical insight here is the paradox: staff KNOW the system is listening (daily disclosure tells them so), yet if support arrives at the wrong moment, it STILL triggers the surveillance response. This is not a rational reaction — it's emotional. The word "trouble" indicates this is a system-killing event, not a minor inconvenience. A single creepy-line crossing can permanently destroy trust for an entire team.

### Quote P8-002: Daily Disclosure and Full Onboarding
**Session:** Session 2, 2026-03-15
**Context:** Ewan describing the transparency protocol — two distinct mechanisms combined. Daily repetition for normalisation, plus comprehensive upfront disclosure.
**Verbatim:**
> "Every day tell them they're being recorded, so it just becomes normal. At onboarding, we disclose everything."

**Interpretation note:** Two separate instructions in one sentence. First: DAILY disclosure — not weekly, not monthly, not "regularly". Every. Single. Day. The mechanism is inoculation through repetition. Second: onboarding disclosure must be EVERYTHING — every capability, every data point, every process. "Everything" is not an exaggeration — it means the complete system. No surprises. No phased discovery. No "we'll tell you about that later."

### Quote P8-003: AI Demystification
**Session:** Session 2, 2026-03-15
**Context:** Ewan explaining how AI should be presented to staff and customers — through gradual, honest education rather than marketing or mystification.
**Verbatim:**
> "AI needs demystified gradually — you just keep saying stuff like that to demystify what you are... you're not lying, you're educating."

**Interpretation note:** Three key elements. First: "gradually" — not all at once, not in a training session. Over time. Through repetition. Second: "you're not lying" — the demystification is honest. The system genuinely does work like a "suggestion box for tone of voice." The analogies are accurate simplifications, not deceptions. Third: "you're educating" — the framing is educational, not persuasive. Not trying to convince people the system is good — trying to help them understand what it actually does.

### Quote P8-005: The Support-Not-Surveillance Framing
**Session:** Various sessions, 2026
**Context:** The overarching framing that applies to all system interactions with staff, but is enforced specifically by P8's intervention timing and framing rules.
**Verbatim:**

**Interpretation note:** For P8, this manifests in the intervention rules: team-level framing (not individual), circumstantial framing (not observational), temporal delay (not immediate response). **Session:** Session 2, 2026-03-15
**Context:** Ewan explaining the kill switch not as an emergency measure but as a transparency and trust feature — the ultimate demonstration that the business retains control.
**Verbatim:**

**Interpretation note:** For P8, the kill switch is part of the onboarding disclosure: "If anything goes wrong, your boss can turn the entire system off with one button."
