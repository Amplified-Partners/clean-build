# covered.AI Sales Playbook

A complete guide to selling low-friction optimization services to UK SMBs.

---

## Part 1: Discovery Call Framework

### Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes)

Before every discovery call:

1. **Review prospect information**
   - LinkedIn profile (recent posts, company updates)
   - Form submissions (pain points mentioned)
   - Previous interactions (emails opened, links clicked)

2. **Prepare 2-3 custom questions**
   - Reference their specific industry
   - Mention a recent company milestone if visible
   - Connect to pain points they mentioned

3. **Select relevant case study**
   - Same industry if possible
   - Similar revenue range (£500K-£3M)
   - Comparable staff size (3-15)

4. **Set timer** for 30-minute call
   - Have Calendly ready for booking follow-ups
   - Keep proposal template open for customization

---

### Call Structure (30 minutes)

#### 1. Opening (2 minutes)

**Script:**
```
Hi [Name], it's Ewan from covered.AI. Thanks for taking the time to speak 
with me today.

Before we dive in, a quick intro: I spent 30 years running a dental practice 
in Byker, Newcastle. I know what it's like to work 60+ hours a week, chase 
invoices, and feel like you can't step away.

Today, I help UK SMBs like yours get 10-20 hours back per week and collect 
£8K-15K more per month—without changing how you work.

This call is simple: I want to understand your situation, identify any leaks, 
and show you if we can help. Sound good?
```

**Purpose:** Establish credibility, set expectations, build rapport.

---

#### 2. Build Rapport (2 minutes)

**Questions to ask:**
- "How long have you been running [business name]?"
- "What made you go independent/start your own thing?"
- "What's the best thing about running your own business?"

**Listen for:** Passions, frustrations, goals, backstory.

**Sample responses to validate:**
- "It's rewarding but exhausting"
- "I love the work, hate the admin"
- "I feel stuck—I can't step away for a week"

---

#### 3. Pain Discovery (8-10 minutes)

**Core questions (ask one at a time, listen fully):**

**Question 1:** "What's the biggest challenge you've faced in the last 12 months?"

**Listen for:**
- Time poverty (working too many hours)
- Cash flow (late payments)
- Lead generation (inconsistent)
- Scaling (can't let go of tasks)

**Follow-up probes:**
- "How many hours a week would you say you're working right now?"
- "What's the biggest drain on your time?"
- "How much do you estimate is stuck in late payments right now?"
- "What happens if you take a week off?"

**Question 2:** "If you could get back 20 hours this week, what would you do with them?"

**Listen for:**
- Revenue-generating activities they want to focus on
- Personal time they crave
- Strategic work they've been putting off

**Question 3:** "What have you tried before to fix this?"

**Listen for:**
- Previous consultants (what went wrong)
- Software solutions (why they didn't stick)
- Staff changes (what failed)

**Validate without agreeing:**
- "That makes sense why that didn't work"
- "Most people try [X] first—it seems logical"
- "The problem isn't you—those approaches just don't address the real issue"

---

#### 4. Situation Audit (6-8 minutes)

**Process walk-through questions:**

**For cash flow issues:**
- "Walk me through your invoicing process. What happens after you send an invoice?"
- "How do you follow up on unpaid invoices?"
- "What's your average payment time? What's the longest someone's taken?"

**For time issues:**
- "Walk me through your typical Tuesday. What do you actually do?"
- "What tasks do you find yourself doing repeatedly that feel like busywork?"
- "Where do you spend more time than you think you should?"

**For lead issues:**
- "What happens after someone inquires? How long until you follow up?"
- "How many leads would you say you're losing because of follow-up delays?"
- "What does your lead nurture look like right now?"

**Identify 2-3 specific leaks:**
- "So if I'm hearing correctly, you're spending [X] hours/week on [task], 
  and approximately £[Y] is sitting in unpaid invoices over 30 days."

**Quantify the opportunity:**
- "If we could automate that follow-up and get you 15 hours back, plus recover 
  £8K in late payments—that's a significant number. Does that sound about right?"

---

#### 5. Present Solution (4-6 minutes)

**Bridge to solution:**
```
Based on what you've told me, here's what I see:

[Recap 2-3 specific pain points they mentioned]

And here's what we do at covered.AI—we don't transform your business. 
We tighten what's already working.

Our approach is simple:

Week 1: We audit what you're already doing. No changes, no disruption.

Weeks 2-4: We automate the busywork—invoice reminders, lead follow-ups, 
data entry. You keep working the same way.

Weeks 5+: We run in the background. The automation just works. 
You start seeing results.

Typical timeline: 4-6 weeks. Disruption: Zero.

[Insert relevant case study]

The key difference is: we work around your schedule, not the other way around.
```

**Address skepticism proactively:**
```
I know this sounds too good to be true. That's fair—I was skeptical too 
before I implemented this in my own practice.

The difference is: we're not asking you to learn new software, retrain your 
staff, or change how you work. We automate the gaps in your existing process.

If it doesn't work, we're not a good fit—and I'll tell you that honestly.
```

---

#### 6. Handle Objections (2-3 minutes)

**Common objections and responses:**

**"Sounds too good to be true"**
```
That's a reasonable reaction. Here's the thing: we're not selling magic. 
We're selling automation that plugs gaps you already have.

Week 1 is an audit—that's free. You'll see exactly what we'd do before 
you commit to anything.

If the numbers don't add up, I'll tell you. We built this on honesty.
```

**"Worried about disruption"**
```
I understand. Here's exactly what we don't do:

- We don't ask you to learn new software
- We don't require staff retraining
- We don't force you to change your processes
- We don't interrupt your operations

We work in the gaps. If you're sending invoices manually, we automate that. 
If you're chasing follow-ups, we automate that. You keep doing what you're 
doing—we just make the gaps invisible.

My own staff didn't even notice the change. It just started working better.
```

**"I need to think about it"**
```
Of course. This is a business decision. Take the time you need.

I'll send you a proposal with everything we discussed, plus a few options 
at different price points. Review it, sleep on it, and let me know if you 
have any questions.

Fair?
```

**"No budget"**
```
I hear you. Here's how I look at it: if we recover £8K-15K in late payments 
in the first 3 months, that's a 3-5x return on investment. The service 
literally pays for itself.

Our entry point is £2,997—that's designed to be accessible for businesses 
at your stage. And we don't require annual contracts.
```

---

#### 7. Next Steps (2 minutes)

**For interested prospects:**
```
Based on our conversation, I think we can help. Here's what happens next:

I'll send you a proposal within 24 hours—it'll include:

- The specific leaks we identified
- 3 options at different investment levels
- Expected timeline and results
- A simple ROI calculation

You'll have 7 days to review. If you have questions, reply directly to me.

If you're ready to move forward, just reply with "YES" and I'll send the 
invoice and onboarding schedule.

Sound good?
```

**For uncertain prospects:**
```
I understand you need to think about it. That's smart.

I'll send you a case study from [similar client] so you can see real numbers. 
And the proposal with options—that gives you concrete information to work with.

If questions come up, reply anytime. I don't do high-pressure follow-ups.

Fair enough?
```

**For not interested:**
```
I appreciate your time. Just to be straightforward: if you're not a fit, 
I'd rather be honest about it than push something that won't work.

If your situation changes, or you hear from someone in a similar spot, 
I'm here.

Best of luck with [business name].
```

---

### Post-Call Actions (5 minutes)

**Immediate (within 5 minutes):**

1. **Send personalized follow-up email** (see email templates below)
2. **Add to CRM** with:
   - Pain points identified
   - Objections raised
   - Interest level (Hot/Warm/Cold)
   - Follow-up date
3. **Create proposal** if committed
4. **Set reminder** for follow-up based on next steps

**CRM Fields to Capture:**
- Company name
- Contact name
- Industry
- Revenue range
- Staff count
- Primary pain point (Time/Cash/Lead/Scale)
- Estimated hours wasted/week
- Estimated late payments £
- Interest level (1-5)
- Objections mentioned
- Case study shared
- Next action
- Follow-up date
- Lead source (LinkedIn/Website/Referral)

## Part 3: Objection Handling & Closing Scripts

### The SEEDS Framework for Objections

**S** - Sympathize with their concern
**E** - Explore the objection (ask questions)
**E** - Explain your response
**D** - Demonstrate proof (case study, numbers)
**S** - Summarize and move forward

---

### Common Objections & Responses

#### Objection 1: "Sounds too good to be true"

**Script:**
```
I appreciate that skepticism. That's actually a healthy reaction to have.

Here's what I mean: We're not selling magic or overnight transformation.
What we do is identify gaps in your existing processes and automate them.

Let me give you a real example from my dental practice...

[Share case study: £45K additional revenue in 90 days through invoice automation]

The key difference is: we're not asking you to change anything. We work
in the gaps that already exist in your process.

Week 1 is a free audit—you'll see exactly what we'd do before you commit
to anything. If the numbers don't make sense, I'll tell you honestly.

Fair enough?
```

**Why it works:** Validates their skepticism while providing concrete proof.

---

#### Objection 2: "Worried about disruption"

**Script:**
```
I completely understand that concern. Here's exactly what we DON'T do:

✗ We don't ask you to learn new software
✗ We don't require staff retraining
✗ We don't force you to change your processes
✗ We don't interrupt your operations
✗ We don't ask you to work around our schedule

What we DO is:

✓ Work in the gaps of your existing process
✓ Automate tasks you're already doing manually
✓ Run everything in the background
✓ Adapt to your schedule, not the other way around

My own staff didn't even notice the change. It just started working better.

Does that address your concern?
```

**Why it works:** Directly lists what they fear, then provides clear alternatives.

---

#### Objection 3: "No budget right now"

**Script:**
```
I hear you. Business finances are always a consideration.

Here's how I look at it: if we recover £8K-15K in late payments in the
first 3 months, that's a 3-5x return on investment. The service
literally pays for itself.

Our entry point is £2,997—that's designed to be accessible for businesses
at your stage.

And we don't require annual contracts. It's a one-time implementation fee
with optional ongoing support.

The real question is: can you afford to leave £8K-15K on the table every
month?

[Pause for response]

If budget is genuinely an issue, I'd rather be honest with you now than
push something that doesn't fit your situation.
```

**Why it works:** Reframes the conversation from cost to ROI, while maintaining honesty.

---

#### Objection 4: "I need to think about it / Talk to my partner"

**Script:**
```
Of course. This is a business decision worth thinking through.

Here's what I'd suggest: Take the proposal home, discuss it with [partner],
and write down any questions that come up.

I'll send you a follow-up email in a few days with a case study from
[similar client] so you have real numbers to look at.

If questions come up, reply directly to me. I don't do high-pressure
follow-ups—I believe in honest conversations.

What's the best timeline for you to make a decision?
```

**Why it works:** Gives them space while maintaining momentum with a specific follow-up.

---

#### Objection 5: "What if it doesn't work?"

**Script:**
```
That's a fair question. Here's our honest answer:

We measure everything. Before we start, we document your current state—
hours spent, payment times, lead conversion rates.

After 8 weeks, we measure again. If you're not seeing results, we'll
tell you honestly and adjust our approach.

We also offer a 30-day guarantee on our Basic tier—if no measurable
improvement, we'll refund 50%.

But here's what the data shows: 90% of our clients see results within
the first 4 weeks. The ones who don't are usually ones who didn't
implement our recommendations.

We're betting on our process. That's why we're confident.
```

**Why it works:** Addresses the fear directly while providing realistic expectations.

---

#### Objection 6: "Send me some information"

**Script:**
```
Absolutely. What's the best email address?

I'll send you:
1. A one-page summary of our approach
2. A case study from [similar industry]
3. Our pricing options

But I have to be honest with you: information alone doesn't change
anything. The value comes from understanding YOUR specific situation.

That's why I'd love to schedule a 30-minute call where I can audit your
current process and show you exactly where the leaks are.

Would a 15-minute call this week work, or would you prefer to review
the information first?
```

**Why it works:** Provides value while creating urgency for a call.

**Script:**
```
Great. Let me send the invoice so we can get started on Monday.
I'll have the audit scheduled by end of day today.
```

**Use when:** Prospect has shown clear interest and no major objections.

---

```
We have two options here. Option 2 (Standard) is what I'd recommend
for your situation—it covers both the invoice automation and lead
follow-up we discussed.

Would you like to start next Monday, or the following week?
```

**Use when:** Prospect is ready but needs to choose between options.

---

#### Technique 3: Takeaway Close

**Script:**
```
Actually, let me be straight with you. Based on what you've told me,
I'm not sure we're the right fit if you're looking for a quick fix.

We work with businesses ready to implement our recommendations. If
you're not able to commit 5-10 hours over the next 6 weeks, I'd say
wait until you're ready.

I don't want to take your money if we can't deliver results.
```

**Use when:** Prospect is hesitant but you sense they need a push.

---

#### Technique 4: Question Close

**Script:**
```
What's it going to take for you to move forward on this?
```

**Use when:** All objections are addressed and you're waiting for commitment.

---

**Script:**
```
So to recap what we're talking about:

- You spend [X] hours/week on admin that could be automated
- You have £[Y]K in late payments that could be recovered
- We can have this running in 4-6 weeks with zero disruption
- Investment is £2,997-4,997 with 3-5x ROI in 90 days

Does this solve the problem you came to me with?
```

**Use when:** Prospect needs a final reminder of the value.
