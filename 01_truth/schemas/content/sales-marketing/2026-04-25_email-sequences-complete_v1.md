1. "The £15K leak (and how to plug it)"
2. "Quick question: How much money are you losing?"
3. "Your biggest money drain (and it's fixable)"

### Email Body

---

**Subject:** The £15K leak (and how to plug it)

Hi {{first_name}},

Quick question.

When was the last time you calculated how much money is sitting in unpaid invoices?

For most UK service businesses I work with, the answer is £8K-15K.

Every. Single. Month.

Here's why:

**The invoice problem isn't about bad clients.** It's about:
- Invoices getting lost in email
- You feel awkward sending reminders
- You're too busy to chase
- No system exists—just hoping

The result? You do the work, then wait 30, 60, 90 days to get paid. Meanwhile, you're working for free.

**Real example: Sarah's Salon, Newcastle**

Sarah runs a hair salon in Newcastle. Last year, she had £8,500 sitting in invoices over 60 days old.

She hated chasing. Felt awkward. So she didn't.

We set up automated payment reminders that:
- Send 7 days before due date
- Send on the due date (polite but firm)
- Send 3 days late
- Send 7 days late
- Escalate at 14 days

**Result: £8,500 collected in 60 days. No awkward conversations. Just automation.**

The key insight: Most clients aren't avoiding payment. They're busy, forgetful, or assumed you didn't need the money yet.

A polite, automatic reminder solves this.

**If late payments are draining your cash flow, reply "INVOICE"** and I'll send you a simple guide to setting this up.

Even if you don't work with us, this alone could recover thousands.

Talk soon,

Ewan
Founder, covered.AI

P.S. This works for any service business—dental, vet, trades, consulting. The mechanics are the same.

## EMAIL 3: CASE STUDY EMAIL (DENTAL)
**Trigger:** 5 days after problem email
**Send Day:** Day 5
**Goal:** Show detailed case study, build trust, introduce offer

### Subject Line Options
1. "How Mark got 20 hours/week back (without changing anything)"
2. "Real results from a dental practice (like yours?)"
3. "This is what happened when we stopped chasing invoices"

### Email Body

---

**Subject:** How Mark got 20 hours/week back (without changing anything)

Hi {{first_name}},

I want to show you exactly what covered.AI does.

Meet Mark. He runs a dental practice in Durham. 12 staff. £800K annual revenue. Sound familiar?

**His situation before we started:**

Mark was working 55 hours/week. Not because he wanted to—but because he had to.

His weeks looked like this:
- 10 hours sending invoices and chasing payments
- 8 hours manually following up on leads
- 5 hours updating spreadsheets and databases
- 4 hours doing admin his team couldn't handle
- Plus actually running the practice

He hadn't taken a real holiday in 5 years. His team kept asking questions he felt he couldn't leave to answer. And he was exhausted.

Sound familiar?

**What we did (4-week process):**

Week 1: We audited everything. No changes. Just watching where time was going.

We found the leaks:
- £12K/month sitting in unpaid invoices
- 40% of new leads going cold (no automated follow-up)
- 15 hours/week on tasks that could be automated

Week 2-4: We built the automation. Invoicing, reminders, lead follow-ups, data entry.

The key constraint: Mark and his team kept working exactly as before. No new software. No retraining. No process changes.

We just... automated what they were already doing.

**The results (90 days later):**

- 20 hours/week back (automated the busywork)
- £45K more revenue collected (late payment automation)
- His team runs the marketing now (documented processes)
- Mark works 4 days/week (first holiday last month)

**What Mark said:**

"I was skeptical. It sounded like every other 'transformation' pitch. But they set it up and left. Now it just runs. My team didn't even notice. I just have 20 extra hours a week."

**The key point:** We didn't change how Mark's team worked. We just removed the busywork they hated.

No pitch. No pressure. Just honest analysis.

| `booking_status` | Single select | NOT_BOOKED / AUDIT_BOOKED / AUDIT_COMPLETED / PROPOSAL_SENT / CLIENT |
| `calendly_link` | URL | Personalized booking link |

### Trigger Rules
| Email | Trigger | Delay | Conditions |
|-------|---------|-------|------------|
| 1. Welcome | Form submit / List join | Immediate | New contact |
| 2. Problem | Email 1 opened | 3 days | Not booked, not replied |
| 3. Case Study | Email 2 opened | 2 days | Not booked, not replied |
| 4. Lead Magnet | Email 3 opened | 2 days | Not booked, not replied |
| 5. Objection | Email 4 not clicked | 3 days | Not booked, not replied |
| 6. Scarcity | Email 5 not clicked | 4 days | Not booked, not replied |
| 7. Breakaway | Email 6 not clicked | 7 days | Not booked, not replied |

### Conditional Logic
- **IF** contact books audit → Stop sequence, trigger audit reminder emails
- **IF** contact replies "LATE" → Skip to invoice automation guide series
- **IF** contact replies "INVOICE" → Skip to setup guide series
- **IF** contact doesn't open email → Resend with different subject line (2x)
- **IF** contact clicks Calendly → Trigger post-click nurture sequence
- **IF** contact unsubscribes → Stop all sequences, tag for blog-only

### Metrics to Track
- Open rate target: 40%+
- Click rate target: 15%+
- Reply rate target: 5%+
- Booking rate target: 2-3% of total sequence starts
- Audit-to-client conversion: 30%
