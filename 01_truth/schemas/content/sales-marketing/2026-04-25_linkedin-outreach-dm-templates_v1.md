# LinkedIn Outreach DM Templates

## covered.AI Direct Message Framework

This document contains all LinkedIn direct message templates for covered.AI outreach campaigns. All templates follow the brand voice guidelines: straightforward, human, non-corporate, specific numbers, honest about limitations.

---

## 1. Initial Outreach Template (Primary)

**Use Case:** First message to ideal prospect (Founder, Owner, MD of UK SMB £500K-£3M revenue, 3-15 staff)

**Subject Line:** (Optional - LinkedIn shows this as message preview)

**Message Body:**

```
Hi {{first_name}},

I noticed {{company_name}} is scaling - congratulations on the growth.

Most business owners at your stage ({{staff}} staff, £{{revenue}} revenue) are drowning in admin. 60+ hours a week, chasing invoices, handling leads manually. Sound familiar?

I help UK SMBs automate the busywork so they get 10-20 hours back per week and collect £8K-15K more per month. No disruption to how they work - we just tighten what's already there.

I worked 30 years running a dental practice in Newcastle. I know what it's like to be the bottleneck.

If you're doing everything yourself and want 20 hours back, let's chat. 30-min call, no pitch, I'll just show you where your money is leaking.

Worth 10 minutes?

Best,
Ewan

covered.AI | Newcastle
```

**Why This Works:**
- Personalised opening showing research
- Specific numbers (10-20 hours, £8K-15K) build credibility
- Founder story creates connection
- Low-commitment CTA ("worth 10 minutes?")
- No corporate jargon, direct language

## 2. Follow-Up Template (7 Days No Reply)

**Use Case:** After initial message with no response

**Message Body:**

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Didn't hear back - probably inbox overload, I get it.

Just wanted to say: if you're feeling like you're doing everything yourself and want to figure out where your money's going, the offer still stands.

30 minutes. I'll show you your leaks. No pressure, no sales pitch.

[Link to Calendly]

If not right now, fair enough. Best of luck with {{company_name}}.

Ewan
```

**Why This Works:**
- Acknowledges silence without being pushy
- Keeps CTA low-commitment
- Offers exit ("fair enough")
- Calendly link makes booking easy
- Short and respectful of time

## HubSpot Integration Notes

### Automation Workflow

1. **Trigger:** New prospect identified via LinkedIn Sales Navigator
2. **Action:** Send initial DM template (Template 1)
3. **Wait:** 7 days
4. **Condition:** No reply?
   - **Yes:** Send Template 2
   - **No:** Move to engaged workflow
5. **Wait:** 7 more days
6. **Condition:** No reply?
   - **Yes:** Send Template 6
   - **No:** Continue nurture sequence

### Tracking Fields in HubSpot

- `linkedin_dm_sent_date`: Date of first DM
- `linkedin_dm_template_used`: Which template was sent
- `linkedin_dm_response_date`: Date of first reply
- `linkedin_response_type`: Positive/Negative/No response
- `calendly_booking_source`: LinkedIn DM
- `calendly_booking_date`: When call was booked

### Response Handling

- **Positive Response:** Move to "Discovery Call" pipeline, book call within 48 hours
- **Question:** Respond personally within 2 hours during business hours
- **Negative Response:** Add to nurture sequence, respect preferences
- **No Response:** Continue follow-up sequence per schedule

## Best Practices for LinkedIn Outreach

### Do:
- Personalize each message with specific details
- Keep messages under 300 words
- Lead with value, not features
- Use specific numbers and results
- Make CTA easy and low-commitment
- Follow up consistently but respectfully
- Track everything in HubSpot

### Don't:
- Use corporate jargon or buzzwords
- Send generic copy-paste messages
- Be pushy or aggressive
- Overpromise results
- Ignore replies (even negative ones)
- Spam prospects with multiple DMs per day
- Forget to follow up
