# covered.AI Brand Identity System

## Document Overview

This document contains the complete brand identity system for covered.AI, including logo concepts, visual design specifications, brand guidelines, and implementation rules. The system is designed to support the low-friction positioning and UK SMB focus of the covered.AI service.

---

## Part 1: Logo Concepts (3 Options)

### Concept 1: Background Layer

**Visual Description:**
A modern, layered visual concept where three translucent horizontal bands overlap to form a subtle "A" shape. The bands suggest processes flowing and tightening without disruption. The top band is navy (#0A2540), middle is teal (#00D9C0), bottom is navy with 50% opacity. The overlap areas create a darker teal where the bands intersect, symbolizing where optimization happens. The overall effect is of a stable foundation with active improvement happening transparently.

**Icon Style:**
- Flat, modern design with subtle transparency effects
- No gradients or shadows (keeps it simple and scalable)
- Soft rounded corners on all bands (2px radius)
- The "A" shape is implied, not literal—subtle recognition over forced branding

**Typography Pairing:**
- Primary: Satoshi Bold for "covered"
- Secondary: Inter Regular for ".AI"
- The ".AI" is smaller (60% of "covered" size), positioned to the right and slightly lower

**Color Application:**
- Primary logo: Navy bands, Teal accent, transparent overlap
- White version: All-white bands for dark backgrounds
- Reversed: White bands, transparent overlap areas
- Icon-only: Stacked layers without text for favicon/app icon use

**Use Cases:**
- Website header and footer
- Email signature and letterhead
- Business cards and stationery
- Social media profile pictures
- Favicon (32x32px)

**Why It Works:**
- The layering visual reinforces "background" positioning—we work behind the scenes
- The "tightening" metaphor of overlapping bands aligns with process optimization
- Simple enough to render at small sizes (favicon) while interesting at large sizes
- The teal accent provides a single point of focus without being overwhelming
- No other brand in this space uses this visual language—memorable differentiation

---

### Concept 2: Safety Net

**Visual Description:**
A stylized shield shape formed by two curved lines that wrap around a central point. The top curve is navy (#0A2540), bottom curve is teal (#00D9C0), and they meet at points on the left and right. The curves don't fully close—they leave a subtle opening at the top, suggesting protection that doesn't trap. Inside the shield is a simple, modern "c" lowercase letter in teal. The overall effect is of protective embrace, not fortress walls.

**Icon Style:**
- Minimalist, geometric curves
- 2px stroke weight throughout
- Smooth, organic curves (not rigid geometric shapes)
- The "c" is a geometric circle with a horizontal cut on the right

**Typography Pairing:**
- Primary: Satoshi Bold for "covered"
- Secondary: Inter Regular for ".AI"
- ".AI" positioned to the right of the main wordmark at 60% size

**Color Application:**
- Primary logo: Navy curves, Teal "c" accent
- White version: All-white curves and "c" for dark backgrounds
- Reversed: White curves, Navy "c" for light backgrounds
- Icon-only: Shield with "c" for small applications

**Use Cases:**
- Website header and footer
- Business cards
- Social media profiles
- Favicon and app icon
- Presentation slides
- Client deliverables (reports, audits)

**Why It Works:**
- Shield imagery suggests protection and optimization—covering gaps without being aggressive
- The "c" inside the shield makes it immediately legible as "covered"
- The gap at the top reinforces "low friction"—we cover without constraining
- Curves feel modern and approachable, not corporate or fortress-like
- Scalable from favicon to billboard while remaining recognizable

---

### Concept 3: Tightening Mechanism

**Visual Description:**
A stylized gear or cog with six teeth, but instead of solid teeth, each tooth is a separate element being "tightened" toward the center by three horizontal lines that pass through them. The gear body is Navy (#0A2540), the tightening lines are Teal (#00D9C0), and the teeth are navy with teal outlines. The visual suggests active optimization—things coming together, not being replaced. The center of the gear is empty, creating negative space that draws the eye inward.

**Icon Style:**
- Technical, precise aesthetic
- 1.5px stroke weight for tightening lines
- Solid fill for gear body with stroke outline
- Clean geometric forms balanced with organic tightening lines

**Typography Pairing:**
- Primary: Satoshi Bold for "covered"
- Secondary: Inter Regular for ".AI"
- ".AI" positioned superscript to the right of "covered"

**Color Application:**
- Primary logo: Navy gear, Teal tightening lines
- White version: All-white elements for dark backgrounds
- Reversed: White gear, Navy tightening lines for light backgrounds
- Icon-only: Simplified gear without text

**Use Cases:**
- Website footer
- Invoice and document headers
- Technical documentation
- Process diagrams
- Favicon (simplified version)

**Why It Works:**
- Gear imagery immediately communicates "process" and "system"—we optimize how things work
- The "tightening" lines reinforce our core message of optimization over transformation
- Technical aesthetic appeals to practical, results-oriented business owners
- The negative space center creates visual interest without complexity
- Distinctive enough to be memorable but not so abstract it becomes meaningless

### Logo Concept Comparison

| Element | Concept 1: Background Layer | Concept 2: Safety Net | Concept 3: Tightening |
|---------|---------------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Primary Metaphor** | Layering, transparency | Protection, embrace | System, optimization |
| **Visual Complexity** | Low (simple bands) | Low (shield + "c") | Medium (gear + lines) |
| **Scalability** | Excellent (favicon to billboard) | Excellent (all use cases) | Good (complex at small sizes) |
| **Tech/System Feel** | Moderate | Low | High |
| **Protection/Safety Feel** | Low | High | Moderate |
| **UK SMB Appeal** | High (subtle, not pushy) | High (trustworthy) | High (practical) |
| **Memorability** | High | High | Medium-High |
| **Differentiation** | High (unique visual) | High (shield + "c" is ownable) | Medium (common gear imagery) |

---

### Recommended Concept

**Recommendation: Concept 2 (Safety Net)**

For a non-coder founder targeting UK SMBs with a low-friction positioning, Concept 2 provides the best balance of:

1. **Trust Signals:** The shield imagery immediately communicates protection and coverage—core to the covered.AI value proposition
2. **Simplicity:** The clean lines and minimal elements make it easy to reproduce consistently across all applications
3. **Memorability:** The "c" inside the shield is immediately legible and distinctive
4. **Flexibility:** Works at any size, on any background, in any application
5. **Approachability:** Curves feel welcoming, not corporate or technical

The gear imagery in Concept 3 is more common and may feel too "techy" for service business owners. The layering in Concept 1 is distinctive but may be too abstract for quick recognition.

### Clear Space Rules

Clear space ensures the logo is always visible and not crowded by other elements.

**Minimum Clear Space:**
- Horizontal lockup: 1x height of "c" character (approximately 50px)
- Stacked lockup: 1x height of "c" character (approximately 40px)
- Icon only: 0.5x icon height (approximately 20px)

**Clear Space Definition:**
Clear space is measured in "x" units where x = height of the "c" character in the icon.

```
[              x              ]  [covered.AI]
[     x        ]              ]
[              ]              ]
```

### Never-Do List

**NEVER do the following with the logo:**

1. **Stretched or distorted:** Never change the aspect ratio
2. **Rotated:** Never rotate the logo
3. **Outlined:** Never convert fills to outlines (except for single-color)
4. **Shadow or effects:** Never add drop shadows, glows, or filters
5. **Background elements:** Never place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
6. **Color changes:** Never use colors not in the brand palette
7. **Element separation:** Never separate the icon from the text (except icon-only version)
8. **Font substitution:** Never use fonts other than Satoshi and Inter
9. **Reconstruction:** Never recreate the logo from scratch
10. **Clutter:** Never add elements (borders, boxes, frames) around the logo

### Color Usage Rules

**Primary Color Hierarchy:**
1. Navy (#0A2540) - Primary brand color, use for main headings, primary text, footers, main backgrounds
2. Teal (#00D9C0) - Accent color, use for CTAs, buttons, icons, highlights, links
3. Charcoal (#2D3748) - Body text color for all long-form content
4. Light Gray (#F7FAFC) - Background color for page backgrounds, card backgrounds

**Contrast Requirements:**
- Text on Navy: White text only
- Text on Teal: Navy text only
- Text on Charcoal: White text
- Text on Light Gray: Navy or Charcoal text
- All text combinations must meet WCAG AA (4.5:1 minimum)

**CTA Button Colors:**
- Primary CTA: Teal background (#00D9C0), Navy text (#0A2540)
- Secondary CTA: Navy outline (#0A2540), Navy text (#0A2540)
- Hover state: Light Teal background (#33E8D0), Navy text

**Link Colors:**
- Default link: Teal (#00D9C0)
- Visited link: Teal Light (#33E8D0)
- Hover link: Navy Light (#1A3A5C)
- No underline (use color to indicate link)

---

### Accessibility (WCAG AA Compliance)

| Combination | Contrast Ratio | Pass/Fail | Notes |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|-------|
| Navy (#0A2540) on White (#FFFFFF) | 14.5:1 | ✅ Pass | Headings, main text |
| Charcoal (#2D3748) on White (#FFFFFF) | 9.5:1 | ✅ Pass | Body text |
| Teal (#00D9C0) on White (#FFFFFF) | 2.8:1 | ❌ Fail | Never use Teal text on white |
| Navy (#0A2540) on Teal (#00D9C0) | 5.4:1 | ✅ Pass | Primary CTAs |

**Critical Rule:** Never use Teal text on white or light backgrounds. Use Navy or Charcoal for text on light backgrounds. Use White text on Navy or Teal backgrounds.

## Part 5: Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice Principles

**1. Straightforward, Not Simplistic**
- Use clear, direct language
- Avoid jargon and buzzwords
- Be specific with numbers and outcomes
- Don't dumb down—treat readers as intelligent adults

**2. Human, Not Corporate**
- Write like a person, not a brand
- Use first person ("I", "we") sparingly
- Be conversational without being casual
- Show personality and humanity

**3. Honest, Not Brutal**
- Acknowledge limitations openly
- Be realistic about outcomes
- Admit when something isn't a fit
- Build trust through transparency

**4. Compelling, Not Salesy**
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Use social proof over hype
- Make claims you can back up
- Let results speak for themselves

---

### Voice Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use specific numbers (£8K, 10-20 hours) | Use vague adjectives (amazing, incredible) |
| Say "we automate the boring stuff" | Say "we leverage AI to optimize workflows" |
| Be conversational ("Here's the thing") | Be corporate ("In today's landscape") |
| Admit limitations ("This won't work if...") | Overpromise ("You'll double your revenue") |
| Use active voice | Use passive voice |
| Ask questions to engage | Talk at the reader |
| Keep sentences under 20 words | Write long, complex sentences |
| Use plain English | Use jargon (synergize, leverage, transform) |

---

### Vocabulary Guidelines

**Approved Terms (Use These):**
- Automate, automate, automate
- Tighten, optimize, improve
- Save (time), collect (money), get back (hours)
- Background, invisible, seamless
- Process, system, workflow
- Busywork, admin, invoicing
- Results, outcomes, impact
- Honest, straightforward, transparent
- £8K-£15K, 10-20 hours, 30%

**Forbidden Terms (Never Use):**
- Transform, transformation
- Revolutionary, groundbreaking, game-changing
- Synergy, synergies
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge
- Disrupt, disruption
- Unlock, unlock your potential
- Next-level, next generation
- Pain points (use "challenges" or "issues")
- Low-hanging fruit (use "quick wins")

---

### Tone Examples

**Wrong Tone:**
> "We leverage cutting-edge AI technology to synergize your operational efficiency and transform your business into a world-class operation. Our revolutionary background automation will disrupt your industry and unlock your true potential."

**Right Tone:**
> "We automate the boring stuff so you can focus on what actually makes money. No transformation. No retraining. No new software to learn. We just tighten your processes in the background while you keep running your business."

**Wrong Tone:**
> "Our game-changing platform delivers amazing results for UK SMBs. We're revolutionizing how businesses optimize their workflows with our best-in-class solution."

**Right Tone:**
> "Most UK SMBs we work with get 10-20 hours back per week and collect £8K-15K more monthly. That's not magic—just process tightening that works in the background."

## Part 6: Visual Elements

### Imagery Style

**Photography Treatment:**
- Real photos of UK businesses and business owners
- Natural lighting (avoid overly staged stock photos)
- In-action shots (working, not posed)
- Diverse representation (age, gender, industry)
- Newcastle/UK context (local landmarks subtly included)

**Image Subjects:**
- Business owners at work (not looking at camera)
- Teams collaborating (small groups, 3-15 staff)
- Process documentation (whiteboards, notes, screens)
- Results visualization (charts, before/after)
- Everyday office environments (not polished studios)

**Image Mood:**
- Professional but approachable
- Optimistic without being fake
- Practical, not aspirational
- Trustworthy, not corporate
- Real, not staged

**Image Treatment:**
- No heavy filters or effects
- Subtle shadow or border (optional)
- Rounded corners (4px) on contained images
- Consistent aspect ratios (16:9 for hero, 4:3 for portraits)

---

### Icon Library

**Icon Style:**
- Line icons (1.5px stroke)
- Rounded corners (2px radius)
- Consistent 24px grid
- Single color (Navy #0A2540 or Teal #00D9C0)
- Simple, recognizable symbols

**Icon Categories:**
- Process (workflow, steps, timeline)
- Automation (gears, clock, refresh)
- Money (pound, invoice, chart)
- Time (clock, calendar, hourglass)
- Results (check, growth, arrow)
- Communication (email, phone, message)

**Icon Use:**
- Use icons sparingly (one per section maximum)
- Always pair with text label
- Size: 24px for inline, 32px for section headers
- Color: Navy for neutral, Teal for emphasis

---

### Photography Guidelines

**Hero Images:**
- 1920px wide minimum
- Show business environment or owner in action
- Allow space for text overlay (center or left-aligned)
- Natural, warm lighting
- Avoid busy backgrounds

**Team/Person Images:**
- 800px wide minimum
- Headshot or three-quarter shot
- Neutral or business-appropriate background
- Natural expression (smiling, not forced)
- Consistent style across all team photos

**Process/Action Images:**
- 1200px wide minimum
- Show actual work in progress
- Authentic, not staged
- Include hands/tools/technology
- Blur background for focus

**Image Sources:**
- Original photography (preferred)
- Unsplash (commercial use, no attribution required)
- Pexels (commercial use, no attribution required)
- Custom illustrations (for abstract concepts)
