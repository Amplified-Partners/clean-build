```
Background: Navy Blue (#0A2540)
Center Text: "covered.AI" in white Satoshi Bold
Subtitle: "Low-Friction Optimization for UK SMBs" in teal (#00D9C0)
Accent: Subtle geometric pattern in bottom right (teal)

Design Principles:
- Clean, professional, modern
- No stock photos of handshakes or meeting rooms
- Text must be readable at small sizes
- Mobile-friendly (most LinkedIn mobile users see cropped version)

Avoid:
- Cluttered backgrounds
- Overly "tech startup" aesthetic
- Generic business imagery
- White text on light backgrounds

Canva Template Search: "LinkedIn Company Page Banner - Professional Blue"
```

**Mobile Crop Warning:**
LinkedIn crops company banners differently on mobile. Keep all critical text in the center 800px width.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Don't:**
- Post only promotional content
- Ignore comments or messages
- Use stock photos of handshakes
- Post inconsistently
- Use buzzwords like "revolutionary" or "game-changing"
- Tag irrelevant people/companies
- Delete negative comments (respond instead)
- Buy followers (violates LinkedIn terms, hurts engagement)

**Do:**
- Lead with value in every post
- Mix educational content with case studies
- Use specific numbers (£8K, 10-20 hours, 30%)
- Personalize responses to comments
- Keep visual design consistent
- Use your founder's story throughout
- Be honest about limitations

---

## Best Practices Summary

1. **Consistency wins.** Post 3-5x per week minimum.

2. **Story builds trust.** Use Ewan's 30-year background repeatedly.

3. **Numbers convince.** Always include specific results.

4. **Low friction sells.** Emphasize "zero disruption" constantly.

5. **Honesty differentiates.** Admit what you don't do.

6. **Engagement compounds.** Respond to every comment.

7. **Test and iterate.** A/B test CTAs and content types.

8. **Mobile first.** Most LinkedIn traffic is mobile.

9. **Links kill reach.** Use link posts sparingly (LinkedIn limits reach).

10. **Images matter.** Posts with images get 2x engagement.
