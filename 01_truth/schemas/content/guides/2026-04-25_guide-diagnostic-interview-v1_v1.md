# DIAGNOSTIC INTERVIEW QUESTIONS
## Master Guide for 30-Minute Profitability Calls

**This is your core deliverable.** These questions are designed to uncover profit leaks fast while building rapport and trust.

---

## PART 1: CONTEXT & RAPPORT (7 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their business at 30,000 feet. Build comfort.

### Opening (1 min)
```
"Thanks for taking 30 minutes. Quick logistics:
- I'll ask questions about your business (about 20 minutes)
- Then I'll share what I'm seeing (about 7 minutes)
- Then we'll talk next steps. Sound good?

To start: Tell me about your business. What do you do, roughly how long, and 
what's your revenue range?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Passion (do they light up talking about it?)
- Pain (any sighs or frustration when discussing revenue?)
- Sophistication (are they tracking numbers?)

### Business Overview (3 min)
```
"OK, so £X revenue, [X] employees.

A few quick ones:
1. How's the business grown over last 2-3 years? Up, flat, down?
2. Is profit growing as fast as revenue? (Or shrinking?)
3. What keeps you up at night most? What's the biggest frustration?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Growth pattern (stalled growth = pain point)
- Margin concern (if profit didn't grow as fast = classic issue)
- Specific pain point (this is your entry angle)

### Recent Changes (3 min)
```
"A lot's changed in [Industry] the last 18 months.

For you:
1. What's changed most in how you operate?
2. What's changed in what your customers want?
3. Have you made any big operational changes recently?"
```

**Listen for:**
- New market pressures
- Technology adoption (or lack of)
- Customer behavior shifts
- Any changes they made that backfired

## PART 2: DEEP DIVE (15 minutes)

**Goal:** Gather enough data to identify 2-3 specific profit leaks.

### Financial Data (6 min)

**Opening:**
```
"I want to understand your financials better. 
Have you got your last 12 months P&L handy? Or in your head?"

(If yes, ask for share. If no, ask them to describe roughly.)
```

**Key Questions (pick 3-4 based on business type):**

**For most businesses:**
```
1. Rough breakdown: What % of revenue goes to:
   - Payroll / labor costs?
   - Cost of goods sold (if applicable)?
   - Operating expenses (rent, utilities, etc.)?
   - Marketing / sales?
   - Other costs?

2. Have these percentages changed in last 12 months? How?

3. What's your gross margin? (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
   - For service: Should be 60-80%
   - For product: Should be 40-60%
   - For SaaS: Should be 70%+
   - If below benchmark = leak

4. Net profit margin? (Profit / Revenue)
   - Target: 10-20% for small business
   - Less than 10% = something's wrong
```

**For companies with variable costs:**
```
5. Do you track profitability by:
   - Customer?
   - Project?
   - Product?
   
   (If not: "We often find your most time-consuming work is 
   your least profitable. Worth exploring?")
```

**For labor-heavy businesses:**
```
6. How much of your time do you personally spend on:
   - Billable work (client delivery)?
   - Admin / overhead?
   - Business development / sales?
   
   And per employee, roughly how many billable hours per week?
   (Full capacity = 32-36 billable hours. Most are 24-28.)
```

### Customer Data (5 min)

**Opening:**
```
"Let's talk about customers. How many do you have roughly? 
And what % of revenue comes from your top 3?"
```

**Questions:**

```
1. Revenue concentration:
   - What % of revenue is your top customer?
   - Top 3 customers?
   - Top 10 customers?
   
   (If top customer > 20%, top 3 > 50%: This is a risk and opportunity)

2. Customer acquisition:
   - How do you get new customers? (referral, cold outreach, inbound?)
   - How much do you spend on sales/marketing per year?
   - How many new customers per month? Per year?
   
   (Calculate: Marketing spend / # new customers = Customer Acquisition Cost)

3. Customer profitability:
   - Do you know which customers are most profitable?
   - Do you have customers you'd love to lose?
   - Any customers that are a lot of work but low profit?
   
   (Most businesses have this. Insight = opportunity.)

4. Churn / retention:
   - What % of customers come back next year?
   - How many customers have you lost in last 12 months?
   - Why do they leave?
   
   (High churn = customer quality issue OR delivery issue)
```

### Operational Data (3 min)

**Opening:**
```
"Last thing: How's the day-to-day going? 
What's the biggest operational frustration?"
```

**Questions:**

```
1. Time allocation (from owner perspective):
   - How many hours per week do you work?
   - Which tasks take up the most time?
   - Any processes that feel broken or inefficient?
   
   (If owner working 50+ hours: Time management leak)

2. Staffing:
   - How many employees? Contractors?
   - Have you hired/lost people recently?
   - Any hiring challenges?
   
   (High turnover or hiring struggles = cost leak)

3. Technology:
   - What software are you using? (Accounting, CRM, project management?)
   - Do they talk to each other? (Or are you doing manual data entry?)
   - Any tool you hate or constantly work around?
   
   (Manual processes = labor waste)

4. Pricing:
   - How do you price? (Fixed rate, hourly, retainer, value-based?)
   - Have you raised prices in last 12 months?
   - Do you ever feel your prices are too low?
   
   (Most SMBs are underpriced by 20-40%)
```

## PART 3: QUICK ANALYSIS (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Show ONE insight that proves your diagnostic works. Build confidence.

### Analysis Approach

During the conversation, you're mentally noting:
- Margin gaps vs industry benchmarks
- Unprofitable customer segments
- Time management issues (if owner-heavy)
- Pricing below market
- Technology waste
- High concentration risk

### Pick Your #1 Insight

Choose the clearest, most impactful finding:

**Example 1: Customer Profitability**
```
You: "So you've got 50 customers, top 5 are 60% of revenue. 
     But when I listen to you describe them, 3 of them sound like 
     they take the most time for lowest margins. Have you ever done 
     the math on per-customer profitability?"

Client: "No, I've never broken it down like that."

You: "That's exactly what we do. I'd bet £500/month you're making 
     on 5 customers what you could make on 2. That's a £X/year 
     opportunity just in customer mix. That's what the diagnostic finds."
```

**Example 2: Margin Compression**
```
You: "You said gross margin is about 65%, but it used to be 70%. 
     That 5% drop... on £X revenue... is £X/year. We typically 
     find that's from either pricing or cost creep. Want to explore?"

Client: "Yeah, labor costs went up."

You: "Exactly. But the diagnostic shows whether it's billable rate, 
     utilization, or overhead. Knowing which changes everything 
     about the fix."
```

**Example 3: Time Management**
```
You: "You're doing 50% billable work and 50% admin. Most service 
     companies at your stage are 70/30. That extra 20% of your time 
     is worth roughly £X/year. We find that's usually systems—
     bad CRM, manual data entry, poor scheduling. Ring a bell?"

Client: "Oh absolutely, our CRM is a mess."

You: "That's what we'd dig into. You probably have £X/year of 
     'hidden billable time' sitting in bad processes."
```

### Present It (2 min)

```
"So here's what I'm seeing:
[INSIGHT #1 - specific, with £ impact]

And I think [INSIGHT #2 - connected insight]

That's the kind of stuff the diagnostic uncovers. 
Does that feel directionally right to you?"
```

**Listen for:**
- "Yes, absolutely"
- "Hmm, we've never thought about it like that"
- "Yeah but we've tried to fix that before and couldn't"

All are good signs. The third one is PERFECT (you've found something they've struggled with).

## PART 4: NEXT STEPS (3 minutes)

### If They Seem Interested

```
"So, here's what I'm thinking:

The diagnostic I do takes 2 weeks. 
In that time, I'll:
1. Pull all your data (financials, customer data, operations)
2. Build models on what's workable
3. Identify the top 3-5 leaks with £ impact
4. Propose fixes for the biggest 1-2
5. Give you a working solution you can test in 48 hours

Cost is £[1,500-2,500], and you only pay once you see the findings.

Sound interesting?"
```

**If "Yes":**
```
"Perfect. What I need from you:
1. Last 12 months P&L + balance sheet (QuickBooks/Xero export, or Excel)
2. Customer list (names, revenue, if possible)
3. 1 follow-up call in 1 week to review preliminary findings
4. Your email to confirm

I'll send a proposal today."
```

**If "Maybe" or "I need to think about it":**
```
"That's fair. Here's what I'll do:
I'll send you the '5 Profit Leaks' guide I mentioned. 
Read it, see if any resonate. 

If they do, reach out. 
No pressure, but it's 10 minutes of reading."
```

### If They're Not Interested (But Took the Call)

```
"I get it. This might not be the right time. 
No worries.

But—one ask: Do you know 1-2 other business owners 
who might be struggling with this? 
I'd love an intro.

And if this ever becomes relevant, you have my number."
```

**Why this works:**
- You're removing pressure (makes them more likely to refer)
- You're staying in touch (they have your number)
- You're positioning as low-pressure expert (builds credibility for future)

## RED FLAGS & PIVOTS

### Red Flag: "We already use a consultant / accountant for this"

**Pivot:**
```
"That's great. Who do they use for [specific area like customer profitability 
or pricing strategy]?"

If they say no one: "That's exactly what I do. 
I work alongside your accountant to find things they're not set up to find."

If they say yes: "Perfect. I actually work with [company name] consultants 
all the time. We usually complement each other—they do strategy, 
I do the operational diagnostics. Worth a chat?"
```

### Red Flag: "Can you just tell me the answer now?"

**Response:**
```
"I could guess, but guessing costs you money. 
The diagnostic is me doing the work—not telling, showing. 
It's the difference between me saying 'your pricing might be low' 
vs me showing you 'you're underpriced by £X on these customers, 
and here's exactly how to fix it.' 
The proof is worth more than the guess."
```

### Red Flag: "How much will this cost to implement?"

**Response:**
```
"Great question. That depends on what we find. 
Some fixes are free (just reallocation or a process change).
Some are £X to implement.

But here's the thing: The diagnostic tells you 
exactly which ones are worth implementing 
and which ones aren't. That's the whole point.

Let's find out what we're working with first."
```

### Red Flag: "I'm too busy to do this right now"

**Pivot:**
```
"I hear that. But here's the irony: 
The diagnostic usually finds why you're too busy.

Could be 3 hours a week of wasted time on something fixable.
Could be the wrong customers taking all your time.

What if we found £X/year of efficiency?
That's time back. Worth 2 hours to explore?"
```

## BEST PRACTICES

### During the Call

✅ **DO:**
- Listen more than talk (70/30 rule)
- Take notes while they talk
- Pause before answering (shows you're thinking)
- Confirm understanding ("So what I'm hearing is...")
- Share ONE insight (not 5)
- Make it specific (£ amount, not vague)
- Ask permission before proposing ("Worth exploring?")

❌ **DON'T:**
- Interrupt
- Sound like you're reading from a script
- Ask questions you don't care about (ask genuine questions)
- Bad-mouth competitors
- Over-complicate your explanation
- Promise miracles
- Act desperate for their business

### Tone

- **Consultant, not salesman.** You're exploring with them, not selling to them.
- **Confident but not cocky.** You know what you know. You don't know what you don't.
- **Problem-focused, not solution-focused.** Agree on the problem first.
- **Real.** Use conversational language. Say "I'm not sure" if you're not.

## SCRIPTS YOU'LL USE REPEATEDLY

### On Price Objection
```
"I get it, it's an investment. But think about it this way:
We usually find £X+ in hidden opportunity. 
The diagnostic costs £X.
If we find even half of that, it's self-funding in 6 months.
And that's just from sitting on what we find—not even acting on it.
Worth exploring?"
```

### On "It's Not Me, It's Timing"
```
"Timing's tricky. But here's the thing:
Most business owners don't know if this is the right time.
That's exactly what the diagnostic tells you.
Is this a 'not now' or an 'I need to think about it'?"
```

### On "How Different Are You From [Competitor]?"
```
"Fair question. Most consultants do strategy and theory.
I do diagnostics—I look at YOUR data and tell you what's broken in YOUR business.
It's less 'here's best practices' and more 'here's what you're leaving on the table.'
Does that distinction matter?"
```

## CALL RECORDING & IMPROVEMENT

**After first 3 calls:**
1. Record calls (with permission: "Mind if I record? Helps me take better notes.")
2. Listen back once
3. Note 1-2 things to improve
4. Iterate

**Things to improve:**
- Are you pausing enough? (Or filling silences?)
- Are you confirming understanding? (Or assuming?)
- Are you sharing insight clearly? (Or burying it?)
- Are your questions open-ended? (Or yes/no?)

The diagnostic is a skill. It improves with practice.

**Remember: The goal isn't to sell. The goal is to help them understand their business better. If you do that, the sale follows.**
