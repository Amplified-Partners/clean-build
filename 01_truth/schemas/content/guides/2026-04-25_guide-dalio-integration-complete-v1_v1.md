## LAYER 1: YOUR BUSINESS OPERATIONS (Principles-Based)

### Your Personal Operating Principles

**Instead of:** "I'll figure it out as I go"
**Now:** "I have written principles for every major decision"

### The Core Principles You Write Down

**1. CLIENT ACCEPTANCE PRINCIPLE**
```
I will only accept clients who meet ALL of these criteria:
├─ Profit margin potential >40% (based on their willingness to implement)
├─ Owner engagement: Attends Monday meetings, responds <24 hours
├─ Team size: 2-10 people (sweet spot for our systems)
├─ Revenue: £500K-3M (can afford us, will see ROI)
├─ Industry: Service-based SMB (our expertise)

Why this principle:
- Protects my time (no low-margin clients)
- Ensures success (engaged owners = better results)
- Focuses expertise (service SMBs = we know their problems)

When to revisit:
- Quarterly: Are these criteria still accurate?
- After every bad client experience: What criterion would have prevented this?

Last updated: January 2026
Success rate: TBD (tracking from Month 1)
```

**2. PRICING PRINCIPLE**
```
I will raise prices when:
├─ Sales conversion rate >60% (demand exceeds supply)
├─ Client satisfaction (NPS) >60 (they're getting value)
├─ Delivery capacity <20% remaining (I'm near full)

Why this principle:
- Prevents underpricing (if closing too easily, price too low)
- Ensures quality (only raise when delivering well)
- Protects capacity (don't get overloaded)

Historical data:
- Month 6: Conversion 68%, NPS 62, capacity 15% → RAISED from £1,200 to £1,500
- Result: Lost 10% of clients, profit up 25% → Principle worked ✓

Last updated: Month 6
Next review: Month 12
```

**3. HIRING PRINCIPLE**
```
I will hire when:
├─ Revenue per employee >£80K (can afford them)
├─ Team working >45 hours/week for 4+ weeks (unsustainable load)
├─ New hire can generate £100K+ revenue within ramp period
├─ I have documented systems for their role (they can succeed)

Why this principle:
- Prevents premature hiring (must have revenue to support)
- Prevents burnout hiring (catch it early)
- Ensures ROI (hire must pay for themselves)
- Ensures success (systems exist for them to execute)

Ramp periods by role:
├─ Sales: 3 months to productivity
├─ Delivery: 6 months to productivity
├─ Marketing: 9 months to productivity

Last updated: January 2026
Success rate: TBD
```

**4. FIRING PRINCIPLE (For Clients)**
```
I will fire a client when:
├─ 3 missed Monday meetings without valid reason (not engaged)
├─ 2 late payments (cash flow risk)
├─ Profit margin <20% for 2 consecutive months (not worth it)
├─ 5+ support issues per month for 3 months (consuming disproportionate time)

Why this principle:
- Protects time (bad clients consume disproportionate resources)
- Protects cash (late payers are risk)
- Protects margin (low-margin clients prevent taking good ones)

Process:
1. First violation: Warning conversation (document it)
2. Second violation: Final warning (30 days to fix)
3. Third violation: Fire them (give 30-day notice, transition gracefully)

Last updated: January 2026
```

**5. TIME ALLOCATION PRINCIPLE**
```
I will allocate my week as:
├─ Sales (new client acquisition): 30% (12 hours)
├─ Delivery (Tier 3 clients): 40% (16 hours)
├─ Strategic work (building systems/coaching): 20% (8 hours)
├─ Admin/operations: 10% (4 hours)

Why this principle:
- 30% sales = pipeline stays full (need 8-10 Tier 1/month)
- 40% delivery = can handle 10-12 Tier 3 clients well
- 20% strategic = building AI coaching platform (future revenue)
- 10% admin = necessary evil, keep minimal

When to revisit:
- If revenue drops 2 months: Increase sales to 40%, decrease delivery to 30%
- If pipeline full (15+ Tier 1 prospects): Decrease sales to 20%, increase delivery to 50%
- Once AI coaching launched: Shift 10% from delivery to coaching (scale product)

Last updated: January 2026
Tracked: Weekly (did I stick to allocation? Why or why not?)
```

### How You Use These Principles

**Every Monday Morning (Weekly Review):**
```
1. Did I follow my principles this week?
   ✓ Client acceptance: Rejected 2 prospects that didn't meet criteria
   ✗ Time allocation: Spent 50% on delivery (should be 40%) - Why? Client X issue.
   ✓ Pricing: Didn't drop price despite pressure from prospect

2. Did any principle get violated? Why?
   - Time allocation violated because Client X had urgent issue
   - Learning: Should have flagged this as "5+ support issues" risk earlier
   - Action: Add to client's record, watch for pattern

3. Do any principles need updating?
   - Not yet, only Month 1

4. What decisions do I need to make this week?
   - Should I take Client Y? Check acceptance criteria... YES (meets all 5)
   - Should I hire? Revenue/employee £75K (below £80K threshold)... NO (wait)
```

**Result:** Your decisions aren't emotional. They're systematic.
