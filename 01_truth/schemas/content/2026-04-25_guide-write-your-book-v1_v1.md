## WHICH AI TO USE (And How)

### Option 1: CLAUDE (Anthropic) - RECOMMENDED

**Why Claude:**
- Best at complex, nuanced writing
- Understands your business deeply (you'll brief it)
- Can hold 100K+ words of context
- Strongest at: Narrative flow, practical guidance, examples

**How to use it:**
1. Upload your complete business system doc (the one we just created)
2. Give it this prompt:

```
You are a professional editor and ghostwriter helping me write a business book 
called "Principles-Based Scaling: How to Build a £1M+ SMB Coaching Business."

I've provided you with my complete business system documentation.

Your job:
1. Organize it into 17 chapters (see structure provided)
2. Add narrative flow (connecting the pieces)
3. Add my personal story (why I'm qualified)
4. Add real examples and case studies
5. Add actionable worksheets readers can use
6. Write in my voice (direct, practical, no fluff)

Write Chapter 1 first. Then we'll iterate on the rest.

Focus on: Clarity, actionability, proof.
Avoid: Jargon, theory without examples, generic advice.
```

3. Iterate chapter by chapter
4. Refine based on your feedback

**Time commitment:** 4-6 weeks (3-4 hours/week managing the AI)

**Cost:** ~£50-100/month (Claude Max subscription)

## THE WRITING PROCESS (Step-by-Step)

### Week 1: Chapters 1-3 (Foundation - 12,000 words)

**Monday:**
- Brief Claude with context doc + prompt
- Review Chapter 1 draft
- Give feedback

**Tuesday:**
- Claude revises Chapter 1
- Claude starts Chapter 2
- You review in evening

**Wednesday:**
- Claude revises + completes Chapter 2
- Starts Chapter 3
- You provide examples

**Thursday:**
- Claude completes Chapter 3 draft
- You read all 3 chapters
- Mark up changes needed

**Friday:**
- Claude does revision pass
- You approve or request changes
- Lock in Chapters 1-3

### Week 2-4: Chapters 4-13 (Same Process)

- 3 chapters per week
- Daily review + feedback
- Evening refinement
- Friday lock-in

### Week 5-6: Chapters 14-17 + Worksheets

- Implementation chapters
- Worksheets (decision log templates, etc.)
- Full book assembly

### Week 7: Final Edit

- Read entire book (one sitting if possible)
- Mark flow issues
- Ask Claude to fix transitions
- Add case studies if needed
- Fact-check numbers

### Week 8: Publish

- Beta readers (5-10 people)
- Final feedback
- Small tweaks
- Publish via KDP or agent

## PUBLISHING OPTIONS AFTER WRITING

### Option 1: Amazon KDP (Self-Publishing)

**Pros:**
- Fastest (1 week from manuscript to published)
- Most control (pricing, cover, etc.)
- Highest royalties (35-70%)

**Cons:**
- You handle marketing
- No traditional prestige

**Timeline:** 1 week

**Cost:** £0 (free to publish, but you pay for editor + cover designer)

**Revenue potential:** £1-5K in first year (if marketed well)

---

### Option 2: Traditional Publisher (Agent)

**Pros:**
- Publisher handles marketing
- Traditional prestige
- Advance payment possible

**Cons:**
- Takes 12-18 months
- Less control
- Lower royalties (10-15%)

**Timeline:** 6 months pitching + 12 months publishing

**Cost:** Agent takes 15% commission (worth it)

**Revenue potential:** £5-50K advance (if good agent)

---

### Option 3: Hybrid (Recommended for You)

**What it means:**
1. Publish on KDP first (get proof of concept)
2. Use KDP numbers to pitch to agents
3. Traditional publisher picks it up for paperback/audio
4. You keep ebook and digital rights

**Timeline:** Week 1 (KDP) + 3 months (agent pitching) + 12 months (publisher)

**Cost:** £1-3K (editor, cover designer)

**Revenue potential:** £3-10K Year 1 (KDP) + Advance from traditional deal
