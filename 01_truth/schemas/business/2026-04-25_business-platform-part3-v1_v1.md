### C. Customer Pain Points (Pre-Transformation)

#### 1. Inconsistent Experience
**Current State:**
- Service depends on which staff member answers
- One person delivers great service, another delivers poor service
- No standard operating procedures
- No consistency guarantees
- Customer frustrated ("Why was I treated differently?")

#### 2. Slow Response Times
**Current State:**
- Response time 24+ hours (or longer)
- Customer has already found alternatives
- Expectation of fast response unmet

**Research Benchmarks (2025 data):**
- **Customer satisfaction peaks at 84.7% when first response is 5-10 seconds** (Fit Small Business)
- **Strategic customers expect <5 min response** (Harvard Business Review: 21x more likely to convert if <5 min)
- **Most customers expect <2 hours** for critical issues, **<24 hours** for non-urgent

**Current Impact:**
- Slow response = customer already considering competitors
- Satisfaction drops sharply beyond 24 hours
- Lead conversion rate plummets

#### 3. No Personalization
**Current State:**
- All customers treated the same (transaction-based)
- No memory of previous interactions
- No preference tracking
- Feels impersonal and robotic

**Customer Impact:**
- Feel like a number, not a person
- No differentiation between you and competitors
- Low loyalty (easy to switch)

#### 4. High Churn Rate
**Current State:**
- **Typical churn: 15-25% annually**
- **Research: 42% of customers will switch after one bad experience** (Salesforce)
- Customer acquisition expensive; losing customers doubles CAC payback

**Industry Benchmarks (2025):**
- **Acceptable churn: 10-15%**
- **Good churn: 5-8%**
- **Best-in-class churn: 3-5%**

**Financial Impact (£500K revenue example):**
- 100 customers at £5K each
- 20% churn = 20 customers lost = £100K revenue gone
- 80% retention = £100K saved annually (vs current state)

#### 5. Low Net Promoter Score (NPS)
**Current State:**
- **Typical NPS: 20-30** (poor)
- **Detractors > Promoters** (customers actively recommend against you)
- No referral growth (negative word-of-mouth)

**Research Benchmarks:**
- **Poor NPS: <0** (more detractors than promoters)
- **Average NPS: 0-30**
- **Good NPS: 30-50**
- **Excellent NPS: 50-70** (customers are advocates)

**Business Impact:**
- NPS <30 → minimal referral growth
- NPS 30-50 → some referral growth
- NPS 50+ → referral growth is primary growth channel
