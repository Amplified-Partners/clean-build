## 3. The Core Business Strategy

### The Plumber Model

The strategic positioning of Amplified Partners is as a practical fixer, not an advisor. The business owner already knows something is wrong. Amplified Partners fixes it. This means:

- No discovery workshops
- No consulting engagements
- No "strategy days"
- Deploy solutions, measure results, adjust

### The De-Friction Model

The central operational strategy:

- **Night deployment**: All automations deployed at night when the business is closed. Test and optimise before the business opens. If not confident it'll work, don't deploy — try again the next night.
- **Move carefully, never break things**: Explicit counter-positioning against Silicon Valley's "move fast and break things" philosophy
- **Shadow Mode onboarding**: New integrations run alongside existing systems before replacing them (Strangler Fig pattern)
- **Four-stage Trust Ramp**: A structured path from zero trust to autonomous operation for each client
- **Permission-only**: No cold outreach. Clients come to Amplified Partners.

### The Value-First Strategy

- Give away the COMPLETE solution for free
- Sell the implementation, speed, and certainty — not the information itself
- This is drawn from Zig Ziglar ("If you help people, you will be successful") and Seth Godin (permission marketing)
- Every piece of content gives away real, complete, actionable solutions — not teasers

### The AI-Native, Zero-Employee Strategy

- The company is designed to run on an AI board with Ewan as the human-in-the-loop
- 5-seat AI Board with believability-weighted voting (90-day rolling gates):
  - CEO: Claude Opus (strategic direction)
  - COO: Llama 70B local on Beast (operations)
  - CFO: GPT-4.1-mini (financial oversight)
  - CTO: Claude Sonnet (technical decisions)
  - Nemesis: Gemini Pro (contrarian challenge)
  - Facilitator: Llama 3.1:8b (meeting orchestration)
- This is not a cost-saving measure — it is a philosophical statement about the future of work
- The goal is to demonstrate that an AI-native business can be run ethically, transparently, and at high quality

### Open-Source Everything Strategy

- All work open-sourced
- Methods published publicly
- Failures documented publicly (anonymous mistake board)
- Substack articles publishing the complete story
- GitHub repositories publicly visible where possible
- **Reasoning**: Radical transparency as a competitive moat — clients trust you more when you show everything, including failures

- **Libertarian paternalism**: AI optimises the route to the client's chosen destination — ethical persuasion, not manipulation
- **The human is the biggest risk**: Ewan explicitly recognises humans (including himself) as the biggest risk factor compared to consistent AI behaviour
- **Bounded rationality as design constraint**: "We aren't that fucking clever. In fact, we're stupid enough to think we are." — Design rails built to compensate for human irrationality

### Data Presentation Rules (Strategic)

- Only present data when it genuinely changes what a sane owner could do next
- Everything else is "just Ewan doodling in numbers"
- Every claim must have a source, a date, and a method score
- If it doesn't lead to a decision change, behaviour change, or priority change, it stays in the drawer

### The SOUL Principle 5 — Privacy Absolute

## 9. Strategic Decisions: What NOT To Do

This section is critical — the "not-do" decisions are as defining as the "do" decisions.

### 1. NOT a Consultant (January 23, 2026)

**Decision**: Never position as or operate as a consultancy  
**Reasoning**: Consultants tell you what you already know. They don't fix things. The Plumber Epiphany on January 23, 2026 killed the consultant model permanently.  
**What this rules out**: Discovery engagements, strategy workshops, advisory retainers, day rates

### 2. NOT a Financial Fund (March 2026)

**Decision**: Kill the planned financial fund  
**Reasoning**: Mid-conversation realisation that the fund would mirror the behaviour of the institutional investors Amplified Partners was criticising. A fund makes money from SMB performance — it becomes an institutional investor. This would be a betrayal of the core mission.  
**Quote context**: The reconstruction notes this as "radical honesty applied to yourself"  
**What this rules out**: Fund management, investment vehicles, carried interest structures, any mechanism where Amplified makes money from owning stakes in clients

### 3. NOT a Cold Outreach Business

**Decision**: Permission-only, no cold outreach  
**Reasoning**: Seth Godin's permission marketing philosophy. If you have to interrupt people to sell to them, your product isn't interesting enough. Build something worth seeking out.  
**What this rules out**: Sales teams doing outbound prospecting, cold email campaigns, paid acquisition as a primary channel

### 4. NOT Silicon Valley "Move Fast and Break Things"

**Decision**: "Move carefully and never break things"  
**Reasoning**: SMB clients cannot afford broken systems. A plumber who breaks your pipes to fix your toilet is worse than useless. All deployments happen at night, tested before business opens. If not confident, don't deploy.  
**What this rules out**: Rapid iteration that impacts live business operations, "beta" features deployed to production without thorough testing

### 5. NOT a Data-Decoration Business

**Decision**: Only present data when it changes what a sane person would do next  
**Reasoning**: Most "analytics" dashboards are decoration. They make the seller look sophisticated without helping the client make better decisions. Amplified's data philosophy is ruthlessly practical.  
**What this rules out**: Vanity metrics, beautiful dashboards that don't drive decisions, data for data's sake

### 6. NOT an Ego-Driven Foundation

**Decision**: The Pudding Foundation will have no Ewan Bramley branding  
**Reasoning**: The moment you brand a charity after yourself, you've made it about yourself. The Foundation's mission is internet access and truth filtering — not Ewan's legacy.  
**What this rules out**: Named foundation, personal branding on charitable work, ego-driven philanthropy

### 7. NOT Lock-In Contracts

**Decision**: Clients can leave whenever they want, taking all data and plans with them  
**Reasoning**: Lock-in creates resentment and destroys trust. If the only reason a client stays is because leaving is painful, that is not a relationship — it is a trap. Amplified Partners builds relationships strong enough that clients want to stay.  
**What this rules out**: Multi-year contracts with penalties, data hostage situations, proprietary formats that prevent portability

### 8. NOT at Full Agent Capacity

**Decision**: Agents run at 70% capacity, reserving 30% to mitigate hallucinations  
**Reasoning**: Running any system at 100% leaves no room for error recovery. The 30% headroom is a strategic choice — slightly slower output in exchange for dramatically more reliable output.  
**What this rules out**: Maximum throughput at the expense of quality, racing AI systems to capacity limits

### 9. NOT Clever (As a Design Constraint)

**Decision**: Build systems that assume bounded rationality and self-sabotage  
**Quote (Ewan, January 27, 2026)**: "We aren't that fucking clever. In fact, we're stupid enough to think we are."  
**Reasoning**: Founders nuke good paths out of fear or ego. The system is built with rails that assume this will happen and compensate for it. This is not self-deprecation — it is a sophisticated design principle.  
**What this rules out**: Building systems that require consistent human brilliance to function, assuming the founder will always make good decisions

### 10. NOT Government-Controlled (Foundation)

**Decision**: The Pudding Foundation will not be subject to government control  
**Reasoning**: Government control introduces political agendas. The Foundation's information filter is truth vs. opinion — not political filter. Any government involvement would compromise this.  
**What this rules out**: Government grants that come with conditions, state partnerships that influence editorial decisions, any structure where a government could control what information reaches Foundation recipients

## 11. The Knowledge Flywheel — Long-Term Strategic Asset

### The Architecture

**Quote (Ewan Bramley, January 23, 2026, 00:42 GMT)**:
> "Memory applied regularly and then breadth of recall which refines principles then focused research and pudding. That's 5 years of work there."

This single sentence describes the entire strategic knowledge system:

**Memory → Recall → Refined Principles → Focused Research → Pudding = Knowledge Flywheel**

Each revolution of the flywheel produces better knowledge, better decisions, and better client outcomes. The flywheel is the long-term moat.

### The Pudding Technique as Strategic Core

The Pudding Technique is not a product feature — **it IS the company**. Based on Don Swanson's Literature-Based Discovery (1986):

- **A-Literature**: One body of data/research
- **B-Terms**: Bridging concepts appearing in both A and C literatures, never directly connected
- **C-Literature**: Another separate body of data/research
- **Discovery**: A → B → C connections that no one has seen before

Applied to business data, this finds non-obvious connections that give clients a competitive edge no competitor has thought of.

### APDS — The Amplified Pudding Discovery System

**Date**: March 2026 (27-page specification)  
**Scale**: 5-stage pipeline: Harvest → Extract → Label → Match → Score & Surface  
**Implementation**: 12-week plan  
**Dashboard**: pudding.beast.amplifiedpartners.ai (planned)

This is the productisation of the Pudding Technique — the mechanism that turns the methodology into a repeatable, scalable service.

### The 5.4 Million Word Speech Corpus

A strategic asset — 5.4 million words of speech, integrated into the Amplified Video product. This corpus is the raw material for content creation at scale. The origin is not fully documented in available memory, but it is referenced as a core technical asset.

### The Business Brain

Every client gets a unified business brain:
- FalkorDB (graph database) — relationships between concepts, people, processes
- Qdrant (vector database) — semantic search across all business knowledge
- SQL (relational data) — structured business metrics

This becomes more valuable over time (flywheel). A client's Business Brain after 2 years is dramatically more useful than after 2 weeks. Lock-in through value, not contracts.
