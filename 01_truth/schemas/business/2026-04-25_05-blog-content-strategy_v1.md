# Introduction (150-200 words)

## The Hook (2-3 sentences)
- Start with a relatable situation or statistic
- Name the specific problem immediately
- Promise a specific outcome

## What You'll Learn (1-2 sentences)
- Preview the article's value
- Set expectations for the solution

## Why This Matters (2-3 sentences)
- Connect to the reader's desire (time, money, freedom)
- Establish credibility through experience

---

# The Problem Section (300-400 words)

## The Situation Most SMBs Face (3-4 paragraphs)
- Describe the common problem in specific terms
- Use data and statistics where relevant
- Name the gap (invoices, leads, processes, cash)

## Why It Happens (2-3 paragraphs)
- Root causes of the problem
- Why business owners accept it as "normal"
- The real cost of staying the same

## What Happens If You Don't Fix It (2-3 paragraphs)
- Consequences of inaction
- Progressive nature of the problem
- Personal and business impact

---

# Why Most Approaches Fail (250-350 words)

## Common "Solutions" That Don't Work (3-4 paragraphs)
- New software (why it fails)
- Hiring admin (why it often makes it worse)
- "Transformation" projects (why they disrupt)
- Waiting (why time makes it worse)

## The Real Reason They Fail (2-3 paragraphs)
- Process problems, not tool problems
- Disruption creates resistance
- Complexity kills adoption

---

# The Low-Friction Solution (400-500 words)

## The Core Principle (2-3 paragraphs)
- Optimize, don't transform
- Work with existing methods
- Automate gaps, not everything

## The Step-by-Step Approach (4-5 paragraphs)
- Step 1: Audit (what you're already doing)
- Step 2: Identify gaps (where money leaks)
- Step 3: Automate (the busywork)
- Step 4: Document (so team can replicate)
- Step 5: Run background (no disruption)

## Why This Works (2-3 paragraphs)
- No retraining required
- No new software needed
- Changes happen invisibly
- Results compound over time

## Real Example (3-4 paragraphs)
- Brief case study (name, industry, situation)
- What we did (specific actions)
- Results (specific numbers)
- Client quote

---

# How to Implement (350-450 words)

## Quick Wins to Start Today (3-4 paragraphs)
- Immediate actions anyone can take
- Tools and resources needed
- Time investment required

## The 4-Week Framework (4-5 paragraphs)
- Week 1: Audit your current process
- Week 2: Identify your top 3 gaps
- Week 3: Automate one gap
- Week 4: Document and repeat

## What to Expect (2-3 paragraphs)
- Timeline for results
- Common challenges and solutions
- When to seek help

---

# Results Expected (250-350 words)

## Typical Results (3-4 paragraphs)
- 10-20 hours/week saved
- £8K-15K more collected
- 30% more leads captured
- Zero disruption to work

## Timeline (2-3 paragraphs)
- 30-day milestones
- 90-day expectations
- Long-term benefits

## Case Study Recap (2-3 paragraphs)
- Brief before/after comparison
- Specific metrics achieved
- Client testimonial

---

# Conclusion (150-200 words)

## Recap (2-3 sentences)
- Summarize the key points
- Restate the core principle

## Next Steps (2-3 sentences)
- Clear call to action
- Value proposition for booking

## Final Thought (1 sentence)
- Memorable closing statement

## 3. Blog SEO Optimization Checklist

### On-Page SEO Requirements

**Title Tag:**
- Length: 50-60 characters
- Includes primary keyword
- Compelling and clickable
- Example: "How to Collect £8K More Per Month | covered.AI"

**Meta Description:**
- Length: 150-160 characters
- Includes primary keyword
- Compelling summary with CTA
- Example: "Automated invoice reminders can collect £8K more per month. Here's the system UK SMBs use. Book your free audit."

**URL Slug:**
- SEO-friendly format
- Includes primary keyword
- Short and readable
- Example: /blog/collect-8000-more-automatically

**Heading Structure:**
- H1: Main title (includes primary keyword)
- H2: Major sections
- H3: Sub-sections
- Keyword variations in subheadings

**Keyword Usage:**
- Primary keyword: First 100 words, 2-3 times throughout
- Secondary keywords: 2-3 occurrences each
- LSI keywords: Naturally throughout
- No keyword stuffing

**Internal Linking:**
- 3-4 internal links per post
- Links to /how-it-works, /case-studies, /free-audit, relevant blog posts
- Anchor text varies naturally

**External Linking:**
- 2-3 external links to authoritative sources
- Relevant and helpful
- Opens in new tab

**Image Alt Text:**
- Descriptive, includes keyword
- File name: seo-friendly (e.g., "automated-invoice-reminder-system.jpg")
- Compressed for performance

### Readability Requirements

**Flesch Reading Ease:**
- Target: 60+
- Aim for accessible, not academic
- Use Hemingway Editor to check

**Sentence Structure:**
- Average: 15-18 words
- Mix of short and medium sentences
- Avoid long, complex sentences

**Paragraph Structure:**
- 2-3 sentences per paragraph
- White space for scannability
- Bullet points where helpful

**Formatting:**
- Bold for emphasis on key points
- Italics sparingly
- Lists limited to 5 items max

### Technical SEO Requirements

**Page Load Speed:**
- Target: Under 3 seconds
- Optimise images (WebP format)
- Minimise scripts
- Use lazy loading

**Mobile Responsive:**
- Test on mobile devices
- Readable without zooming
- Touch targets 44x44px minimum
- No horizontal scrolling

**Structured Data:**
- Article schema
- FAQ schema where applicable
- LocalBusiness schema

### Conversion Optimization

**CTA Visibility:**
- Above the fold when possible
- Sticky header/footer
- Multiple CTAs throughout
- Contrast colour for buttons

**Lead Magnet Integration:**
- Contextual offers within content
- Exit-intent pop-up
- Related content suggestions

**Engagement Elements:**
- Reading time indicator
- Author bio
- Share buttons
- Comments section (optional)
