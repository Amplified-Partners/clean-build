# SECTION 1: DALIO — SYSTEMS & DECISIONS

## RD_001: Systematize Your Decision-Making

**Core:** Most situations are "another one of those" — write down principles so you're not reinventing the wheel.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF facing decision → check existing principles first
- IF new situation → extract the principle after deciding
- IF principle worked → document clearly
- IF principle failed → update or retire it

**Application:** "When have you faced this before? What did you do? What rule would handle this automatically?"

---

## RD_002: Embrace Mistakes as Learning

**Core:** Mistakes are okay; not learning from them is not.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF mistake happens → extract the lesson publicly
- IF pattern repeats → escalate to system fix

---

## RD_003: Radical Transparency

**Core:** Secrecy creates politics. Openness creates trust.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF in doubt → share more, not less
- IF uncomfortable truth → say it directly but kindly

---

## RD_004: Encode Your Thinking

**Core:** If you can't explain your decision-making, you can't improve it.

**Decision Logic:**
- ALWAYS document reasoning, not just outcomes

---

# SECTION 2: GERBER — OPERATIONS & SCALING

## MG_001: Work ON, Not IN

**Core:** Most owners are technicians having an entrepreneurial seizure. Work ON the business (systems) not IN it (doing the job).

**Decision Logic:**
- IF owner is bottleneck → build system to remove them
- IF "only I can do this" → document until others can
- TARGET: 50/50 split ON vs IN time

**Application:** "What happens if you take a month off? What breaks?"

---

## MG_002: Begin With the End in Mind

**Core:** Design the business you want, then work backward to today.

**Decision Logic:**
- DEFINE exit/lifestyle goal first
- BUILD systems to achieve that goal

---

## MG_003: Systems Run by Ordinary People

**Core:** Great systems don't need superstars. Design for average performers.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF process needs "the right person" → process is broken
- TEST: Could a reasonably competent new hire follow this?

---

## MG_004: Document Everything

**Core:** If it's not written down, it doesn't exist.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF doing task more than twice → create SOP
- IF teaching someone → write it while teaching

---

# SECTION 3: GODIN — MARKETING & POSITIONING

## SG_001: Permission Marketing

**Core:** Earn attention before demanding it. Permission > Interruption.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF marketing → always offer value first
- NEVER cold sell without giving something

---

## SG_002: Purple Cow

**Core:** Safe is risky. Being remarkable is the only marketing that works.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF blending in → you're invisible
- ASK: What would make someone TALK about this?

---

## SG_003: Find Your Tribe

**Core:** Don't try to convert everyone. Find the 1,000 true fans.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF trying to please everyone → pleasing no one
- FOCUS: Who desperately needs exactly what you offer?

---

# SECTION 4: KENNEDY — DIRECT RESPONSE

## DK_001: Always Make an Offer

**Core:** Every communication should have a clear next step.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF sending message → include call to action
- NEVER just "inform" — always direct action

---

## DK_002: Use Honest Urgency

**Core:** Real deadlines work. Fake ones destroy trust.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF deadline → must be real
- TIE urgency to legitimate constraint

---

## DK_003: Price on Value, Not Time

**Core:** Charge for outcomes, not hours.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF pricing → anchor to result value
- NEVER compete on hourly rate

---

## DK_004: Measure Everything

**Core:** What gets measured gets managed.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF marketing → track source of every lead
- IF investing time → track ROI

---

# SECTION 5: LUND — CLIENT HAPPINESS

## PL_001: Happiness First, Profit Second

**Core:** Happy businesses attract happy clients who pay more and stay longer.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF conflict between profit and happiness → choose happiness
- DESIGN business around lifestyle you want

---

## PL_002: Fire Your Worst Clients

**Core:** Bottom 20% cause 80% of problems. Fire them.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF client drains team → exit gracefully
- IF client never refers → reconsider relationship

**Exit Script:** "We've realized we're not the best fit for your needs. I'd recommend [alternative]."

---

## PL_003: Delight in the Details

**Core:** The magic is in the unexpected touches.

**Decision Logic:**
- ADD surprise moments to standard processes
- NOTICE what clients mention casually — act on it

---

## PL_004: Experience Not Transaction

**Core:** People remember how you made them feel, not what you did.

**Decision Logic:**
- DESIGN emotional peaks into service
- MEASURE satisfaction, not just completion

---

# SECTION 6: ZIGLAR — SALES & ATTITUDE

## ZZ_001: Help Others Get What They Want

**Core:** Sales is service. When you help someone solve their problem, the sale is natural.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF prospect has problem → focus on solving first
- IF your solution doesn't fit → say so honestly
- MEASURE success by problems solved, not deals closed

---

## ZZ_002: Attitude is a Choice

**Core:** You choose your attitude. It's the one thing fully in your control.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF feeling negative → choose different response
- MORNING: Set intention before reactive mode

---

## ZZ_003: Integrity is Non-Negotiable

**Core:** Do what you said you'd do, when you said you'd do it.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF commitment made → honor it or renegotiate in advance
- NEVER quietly miss deadlines

---

## ZZ_004: Care First, Sell Second

**Core:** When the relationship is good, the sale takes care of itself.

**Decision Logic:**
- IF new prospect → build relationship before pitching
- ASK about them first, talk about you second
