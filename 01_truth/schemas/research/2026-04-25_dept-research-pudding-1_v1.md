## 1. The Pudding Technique

### What It Is

The Pudding Technique is **the intellectual core of Amplified Partners** — not a feature, not a methodology bolted on, but the origin story, philosophy, differentiation, and product pipeline all in one. As stated in the reconstruction document: "The Pudding Technique is not a feature bolted onto Amplified Partners — **it IS the company**."

### Name Origin — Two Sources

**Source 1: The idiom**
"The proof is in the pudding" — meaning you don't know if something works until you test it. Applied here: don't theorise about connections between literatures, **test** the hypotheses they generate.

**Source 2: Robert Burns, 1787**
Robert Burns' "Address to a Haggis" — "Great chieftain o' the pudding-race!" Burns called the haggis the chief of the pudding-race. Ewan appropriated this: the Pudding Technique is the chief of the discovery-race. The 239-year gap between Burns' pudding poetry (1787) and Ewan's pudding systems (2026) became a deliberate cultural anchor.

**The Burns Night Connection**: The midnight session of January 23, 2026 was close to Burns Night (January 25). This is not coincidence — it became the founding mythology of the technique.

### Core Principle

The Pudding Technique takes Don Swanson's academic Literature-Based Discovery (LBD) and applies it **outside biomedical research** — to business intelligence, market research, competitive analysis, client onboarding, and content discovery.

**The fundamental insight**: Vast amounts of knowledge already exist in published form. Most of the world's most valuable ideas are not new — they are **undiscovered connections between existing ideas**. Swanson proved this in 1986. Pudding systematises this for commercial application in 2026.

### The Two-Layer Definition

**Layer 1 — The Method**: Find relationships between two bodies of knowledge (A and C) that are never directly connected, using bridging concepts (B-terms) that appear in both. If A↔B and B↔C, then A↔C may be a novel and valid discovery.

**Layer 2 — The Philosophy**: "Memory applied regularly and then breadth of recall which refines principles then focused research and pudding. That's 5 years of work there." — Ewan Bramley, 00:42 GMT, January 23, 2026.

The full system is: **Memory → Recall → Refined Principles → Focused Research → Pudding** = a knowledge flywheel.

## 2. Don Swanson's Literature-Based Discovery — Academic Foundation

### Who Is Don R. Swanson?

Don R. Swanson (1924–2012) was an American information scientist and Dean of the Graduate Library School at the University of Chicago. His 1986 paper in *Science* introduced the concept of Literature-Based Discovery (LBD). Neil Smalheiser's 2017 retrospective in the *Journal of Data and Information Science*, "Rediscovering Don Swanson: The Past, Present and Future of Literature-based Discovery," describes Swanson's method as "scientific arbitrage" — finding ideas undervalued in one scientific arena and gaining value by applying them to another.

### The ABC Model (1986)

The core mechanism:

```
A-Literature  →  B-Terms  →  C-Literature
      ↓              ↓              ↓
  One body       Bridging       Another body
 of research    concepts that   of research
               appear in both
               but never jointly
                   cited
                    ↓
            A↔C = New discovery
```

**Formal definition**:
- **A-Literature**: A body of research focused on topic A
- **C-Literature**: A body of research focused on topic C
- **B-Terms**: Concepts that appear in both A and C literature but have never been studied in the context of their relationship to each other
- **Condition for discovery**: A and C literatures must be "non-interactive" — no shared authors, no shared citations, no articles in common
- **Discovery**: If A→B and B→C are both supported in the literature, then A→C is a valid, untested hypothesis

### Swanson's Two Famous Discoveries

**Discovery 1: Fish Oil → Raynaud's Disease (1986)**
- A-Literature: Fish oil and its effects on blood viscosity, platelet aggregation, vasodilation
- C-Literature: Raynaud's disease (intermittent blood flow restriction in extremities)
- B-Terms: Blood viscosity, platelet aggregation, vasodilation (appeared in both bodies of research, never connected)
- Hypothesis: Fish oil may benefit Raynaud's patients
- Validation: DiGiacomo et al. (1989) — double-blind clinical trial confirmed the hypothesis. The two literatures had no articles in common and had never been cited together before Swanson's analysis.

**Discovery 2: Magnesium Deficiency → Migraine (1988)**
- Based on eleven indirect connections
- Led to inference that magnesium deficiency is a causal factor in migraine headache
- Confirmed: Peikert et al. (1996) confirmed the magnesium → migraine hypothesis

Both discoveries were made **without a laboratory, without new experiments**, purely through synthesis of existing published research.

### Open vs. Closed Discovery

Two modes of LBD (relevant to how Pudding operates):

**Open Discovery**: Start with A, find unknown C via B-terms. "What does this domain connect to that we don't know yet?" — The prospective mode. Used for finding novel opportunities.

**Closed Discovery**: Start with A and C (a known hypothesis or relationship), find the B-term pathway explaining the mechanism. "We know A and C are connected — why?" — The retrospective mode. Used for validation.

### Key Academic Validators

| Researcher | Contribution |
|-----------|--------------|
| Don R. Swanson | Original ABC model (1986) |
| Neil Smalheiser | Extended LBD to neuroscience, developed ARROWSMITH tool; wrote retrospective (2017) |
| Dimitar Hristovski | BITOLA system — applied LBD to disease-gene discovery; used association rules on MeSH terms (2005) |
| Weeber et al. (2001) | Computationally simulated both Swanson discoveries using semantic filtering |
| DiGiacomo et al. (1989) | Clinical confirmation of fish oil → Raynaud's hypothesis |
| Peikert et al. (1996) | Clinical confirmation of magnesium → migraine hypothesis |
| Cameron et al. (2013) | Graph-based recovery and decomposition of Swanson's hypothesis using semantic predications |
| Pu, Beck, Verspoor (2025) | Most recent (2025) LBD paper — enriched knowledge representation, showed only 20% of discoveries perfectly represented with pairwise relationships alone |

### Swanson's "Scientific Arbitrage" Concept

From Smalheiser's retrospective: "Don often referred to literature-based discovery as an exercise in 'scientific arbitrage', in which certain ideas or findings are under-valued in one scientific arena, and gain in value by applying them to another field." This concept — **information arbitrage** — is the intellectual ancestor of the Pudding Technique's commercial application.

### Expansion Beyond Biomedicine

The original LBD was biomedical. Modern expansions (Hahn-Powell et al., 2017; Taleb et al., 2024) use LLMs and knowledge graphs to extend this to other domains. Amplified Partners (2026) extends it further to business intelligence, competitive analysis, client knowledge extraction, and content discovery.

## 3. The PUDDING 2026 Taxonomy and Labelling System

### Core Labelling Format

**WHAT.HOW.SCALE.TIME**

Four-criteria rubric applied to every pudding discovery or knowledge item:
- **WHAT**: What is the domain/topic being addressed?
- **HOW**: What is the mechanism or method of connection?
- **SCALE**: At what scale does this apply? (individual, SMB, enterprise, sector, global)
- **TIME**: What is the temporal relevance? (immediate, short-term, long-term, evergreen)

### Biological Decision Logic Taxonomy

The taxonomy uses biological metaphors for classification — drawing on how organisms process and categorise information at different levels of specificity.

### Radical Attribution Schema

Every piece of knowledge in the system is scored on three dimensions:
- **source_score**: How reliable and primary is the source?
- **date_score**: How recent is the information?
- **method_score**: How rigorous was the method of discovery?

These three scores combine into an overall attribution score. This implements the broader Amplified Partners principle: "Every claim must have a source, a date, and a method score."

### Integration with Data Presentation Rules

The taxonomy feeds directly into the operational principle: "We only present data when it genuinely changes what a sane owner could do next; everything else is just Ewan doodling in numbers." A low method_score or old date_score means the item stays in the drawer.

### The Codebook System

Related to the Curator Gate Specification (March 17, 2026): a compressed '_c' code system with CURATOR-CODEBOOK.md lookup table. Two-character metadata codes are assigned by the Curator agent. All code passes five deterministic gates before labelling: Structure, Codebook, Hierarchy, Content, Reconciliation.

The principle: **label-once-propagate-everywhere**. Once an item is labelled in the PUDDING taxonomy, that label propagates across all five data stores (Document Store, Knowledge Graph, Vector Index, Process Database, Backup Store).

## 5. Christopher Alexander Pattern Language for Pudding

### Christopher Alexander — Who He Is

Christopher Alexander (1936–2022) was an architect, mathematician, and design theorist. His 1977 book *A Pattern Language: Towns, Buildings, Construction* introduced the concept of documenting recurring design problems and their solutions as "patterns." The book contains 253 patterns and is written as problems with documented solutions — intended to give ordinary people, not only professionals, tools to work with their environments.

Alexander's Pattern Language was later adopted by software engineering (design patterns, GoF patterns) and is now the conceptual framework Amplified Partners uses to document the Pudding Technique itself.

### The Five Mandatory Fields (Alexander's Format)

Every Pudding pattern must contain:

1. **Name**: Noun phrase, memorable, 1-4 words
2. **Context**: When this pattern applies
3. **Problem**: The recurring challenge to solve
4. **Solution**: The core mechanism
5. **Links**: Larger and smaller patterns that this connects to (the network structure)

### Why Alexander + Swanson?

Alexander's insight was that patterns exist in networks — "A pattern language has the structure of a network." This maps perfectly onto Swanson's ABC model, where B-terms are the network nodes connecting otherwise isolated literatures.

Amplified Partners synthesised these two frameworks: Swanson provides the **discovery engine** (find new A↔C connections), Alexander provides the **documentation engine** (record those connections as reusable patterns).

### Example Pattern: ABC_Bridge

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Name** | ABC_Bridge |
| **Context** | When two bodies of knowledge exist that have never been connected |
| **Problem** | The A↔C relationship is valuable but invisible because no literature links them directly |
| **Solution** | Identify B-terms that appear in both A and C literature; verify non-interaction; document the discovered relationship |
| **Links** | Larger: Semantic Prior Exploitation, Knowledge Flywheel; Smaller: Source Scoring, Date Scoring, Method Scoring |

### Pattern Network Architecture

The full Pudding pattern library is designed as a network, not a list. Each pattern links to:
- **Larger patterns** (the macro-level framework it serves)
- **Smaller patterns** (the micro-level mechanisms it uses)

This ensures the system is self-documenting and navigable without requiring expert knowledge — Alexander's original democratic design intent.

## 8. The 3-Gate Formula for Validation

### Context

Discovered within the Amplified Methodology Framework and the PUDDING validation spec. The 3-Gate Formula is the quality control mechanism for accepting or rejecting a putative pudding discovery before it enters the output streams.

### The Three Gates

**Gate 1: Non-Interaction Check**
- Have these two literatures/domains (A and C) ever been directly cited together?
- Do they share authors?
- Do they share articles?
- If YES to any → not a Swanson-valid discovery. Downgrade or reject.
- This is the original Swanson condition, formalised.

**Gate 2: B-Term Strength**
- How many B-terms bridge A and C?
- Are the B-terms genuinely present in both A-literature and C-literature?
- Are the B-terms statistically significant (not noise)?
- Minimum B-term strength threshold to pass
- Weak bridges → weak discoveries → reject

**Gate 3: Actionability**
- Amplified Partners data principle: "We only show data if a normal person in your shoes would naturally want to do something different after seeing it. If not, it's just decoration."
- Does this A↔C discovery change a decision, behaviour, or priority?
- If it doesn't → it stays in the drawer, regardless of academic validity

### Connection to the Build Quality Framework

The Build Quality Framework v1.0 (March 16, 2026) has its own quality gates:
- Ship threshold: PRS ≥ 7.0
- Gold standard: ≥ 9.0
- 6-stage pipeline: Decompose → Score 0-10 → Research → Test → Reassemble → Attribute

The 3-Gate Formula for Pudding validation maps onto this broader quality framework. A pudding discovery must pass its three gates before it can be attributed a quality score and routed to output streams.

### Validation Database

The AMF v1.0 references a 705-case golden dataset for canary deployment and validation. This is the test corpus against which the Pudding pipeline is calibrated.

## 11. Semantic Prior Exploitation and Standing on Giants

### The Concept

"Semantic prior exploitation" describes the practice of using what is already known (the semantic prior — all existing recorded knowledge) as raw material for new discoveries. It is the epistemological foundation of the Pudding Technique.

**Key insight from Swanson** (Smalheiser, 2017): "Don often referred to literature-based discovery as an exercise in 'scientific arbitrage', in which certain ideas or findings are under-valued in one scientific arena, and gain in value by applying them to another field."

**Commercial translation**: Every piece of published research, every industry report, every case study, every expert interview is undervalued somewhere and overvalued somewhere else. The Pudding Technique is a system for finding and exploiting this differential.

### "Standing on Giants" — Ewan's Formulation

Ewan's phrase for this practice (adapted from Newton's "standing on the shoulders of giants"):

> "We don't need to be smart. We need to be good at standing on giants."

The insight: you don't need to generate new knowledge if you can systematically discover the undiscovered connections in existing knowledge. This is more efficient and more reliable than original research.

**Practical implication for Amplified Partners**: The company doesn't need to conduct original research. It needs to:
1. Harvest existing knowledge (from clients, from public sources, from research)
2. Label it correctly (PUDDING taxonomy)
3. Match it systematically (Swanson ABC)
4. Surface the non-obvious connections (3-Gate Formula)
5. Deliver the actionable findings

### The Knowledge Flywheel

The full semantic prior exploitation loop:


## 12. The Hound Dog Convergence Tool

### What It Is

Hound Dog is the convergence tool — the first stage of the Extraction Department pipeline before DocBench processes documents. It is responsible for semantic convergence of disparate material.

### Function

**Input**: Multiple raw sources — PLAUD voice recordings, session transcripts, vault documents, emails, imported business docs, web research
**Process**: Identifies thematic clusters across all inputs before any formal extraction runs
**Output**: A unified raw corpus with preliminary theme identification, ready for DocBench

**Named after**: Elvis Presley's "Hound Dog" — the tool "sniffs out" connections the way a hound dog tracks a scent across terrain. Consistent with Amplified Partners' preference for memorable, plain-English naming.

### Why It Exists

The problem it solves: raw inputs arrive in many formats and contexts. A voice recording from a morning walk contains the same insight as a formal vault document — but DocBench would process them as entirely separate items. Hound Dog clusters semantically similar material before DocBench runs, so the extraction step operates on coherent thematic groups rather than isolated documents.

This is semantic prior exploitation in practice: Hound Dog identifies the existing B-terms in the raw corpus before the formal Swanson matching step.

## 14. Academic Validation — Hristovski and Beyond

### Dimitar Hristovski — The BITOLA System

**Reference**: Hristovski, D., Peterlin, B., Mitchell, J.A., Humphrey, S.M. (2005). "Using literature-based discovery to identify disease candidate genes." *International Journal of Medical Informatics*.

BITOLA (Biomedical Text Mining Tool) is an interactive LBD support system that:
- Uses MeSH (Medical Subject Headings) terms as B-terms
- Uses UMLS (Unified Medical Language System) for semantic typing
- Applies association rules (support and confidence metrics) to select significant B-terms
- Focuses on open discovery: start with disease X, find related disease characteristics Y, find genes Z related to Y

**Connection to Pudding**: BITOLA's architecture closely parallels the APDS:
- MeSH terms ≈ PUDDING labels (controlled vocabulary for semantic matching)
- UMLS semantic types ≈ PUDDING taxonomy (biological decision logic)
- Association rules ≈ 3-Gate Formula (statistical validation of B-term strength)

### Key 2024-2025 Academic Developments

**Taleb, Navaz, Serhani (2024)**: "Leveraging Large Language Models for Enhancing Literature-Based Discovery" — integrates LLMs with LBD for biomedical discovery. Validates the APDS approach of using LLMs in the matching stage.

**Pu, Beck, Verspoor (2025)**: "Enriched knowledge representation in biological fields: a case study of LBD in Alzheimer's disease":
- Only 20% of discoveries are perfectly represented with pairwise relationships alone
- 73% require pairwise + nested relationships
- 7% require pairwise + nested + hypergraphs
- **Implication for APDS**: The FalkorDB graph database (which supports complex graph relationships) is the right architectural choice. Simple vector matching is insufficient for 80% of discoveries.

**Launer-Wachs et al. (2023)**: "From centralized to ad-hoc knowledge base construction for hypotheses generation":
- Demonstrates that individual researchers / small teams can create ad-hoc, lightweight knowledge bases tailored to specialized interests
- **Implication for Amplified Partners**: Every SMB client can have their own pudding discovery engine — their industry knowledge base + the broader knowledge base = personalised discovery.

### The "Scientific Arbitrage" Legacy

Swanson's framing of LBD as scientific arbitrage is the academic foundation for Amplified Partners' commercial positioning:
- Biomedical research arbitrage: Swanson (1986–2012)
- Business intelligence arbitrage: Amplified Partners (2026)
- The method is identical; only the domain has changed
