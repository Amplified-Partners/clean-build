Executive Summary
We conducted an exhaustive scan of the AI-for-SME market across employee advocacy platforms,
employee experience tools, nudge technology providers, AI implementation consultancies, and managed IT
services. The conclusion is unambiguous: nobody is combining all of our core commitments.
No competitor in any adjacent category offers the combination of: no-redundancy contractual
guarantees, forensic onboarding, staff-first trust ladder, disclosed communication techniques, life
goals integration, AI-as-partner model, three-way SLA, and training privacy guarantees.
Individual elements exist in isolation — primarily at enterprise scale. Perceptyx offers behavioural nudges;
Microsoft Viva embeds productivity insights in Teams; employee advocacy platforms use AI for social
content. But nobody packages these capabilities for SMEs, nobody starts with staff trust rather than
employer productivity, and nobody makes the contractual commitments we make.1
The market is characterised by a fundamental tension: 60% of companies are cutting jobs in anticipation of
AI while only 2% are doing so because AI is actually performing the work.2 Staff fear is the dominant barrier
to adoption, yet every existing solution addresses productivity first and fear as an afterthought — if at all.
Amplified Partners addresses fear first. Productivity follows. This is not a market-entry opportunity. It is a
category-creation opportunity.

The Ten Differentiators Nobody Has
Each differentiator exists somewhere in isolation. No competitor combines even three of these.
Amplified Partners delivers all ten as standard.
1. No-Redundancy Contractual Guarantee
HBR found 60% of companies cut jobs in anticipation of AI, while only 2% did so because AI was actually performing the work.1
Staff are being made redundant for fear, not fact. Amplified contractually prevents this: the AI we deploy will never be used as
justification to reduce headcount. This commitment is unique in the market.
2. Forensic Onboarding with 24–48hr Silent Observation
Before any AI touches a workflow, we spend 24–48 hours silently observing actual work patterns, communication flows, and
hidden bottlenecks. No competitor does this. Enterprise platforms deploy top-down with IT configuration. We deploy bottom-up
with human observation.
3. Staff-First Trust Ladder
Our onboarding follows a deliberate sequence: acknowledge staff concerns → demonstrate capability → guarantee privacy →
show value → integrate life goals. Every competitor starts at 'demonstrate capability' and wonders why adoption stalls.
4. Disclosed Communication Techniques Manifesto
We transparently tell staff: 'We use behavioural nudges. Here is exactly how they work.' Every technique is documented in our
manifesto. No dark patterns, no hidden manipulation. Trust requires transparency about how trust is built.
5. Voice/Tone Capture — 'Their Voice, Their Way'
Skail.ai does voice cloning for marketing content.2 Nobody does it for operational AI partnership. Our AI learns each person's
communication style and mirrors it — the AI sounds like them, not like a generic assistant.
6. Life Goals Integration
No competitor asks staff about personal life goals and aligns AI assistance accordingly. Perceptyx's nudges are work-focused
only.3 Our approach: if someone wants to leave at 4pm to coach their kid's football team, the AI helps them get there.
7. AI as Partner — Egalitarian Trusted Advisor
Every competitor positions AI as a tool that staff use. Amplified positions AI as an egalitarian trusted advisor that earns the right
to joke with you over time. The relationship is the product, not the technology.
8. Three-Way SLA
A formal trilateral service level agreement: Amplified → Client (what we deliver), Client → Amplified (what they commit to), and
Staff ↔ AI (the rules of engagement). No competitor formalises the staff-AI relationship in contract.
9. Training Privacy Guarantee
'Boss never told, we can prove it.' Staff training interactions with AI are private by default and by contract. Managers see
outcomes, not learning curves. No competitor offers provable training privacy at this level.
10. Metric Reframing
'I'm 2 away from my goal' not 'I hit 98%.' We reframe performance metrics around personal achievement rather than deficit
reporting. The psychology matters: the same data, framed differently, changes how people feel about their work.

AI adoption is not a technology problem. It is a trust problem. The data is overwhelming: staff fear is the
dominant barrier, and existing solutions ignore it entirely.
22%
of individual contributors view AI as "anti-worker" — double the 11% of managers who think the
same.1
4 in 10
employees fear what AI means for their job security.2
80%
of workplaces are experiencing hidden AI resistance — staff quietly refusing, delaying, or
sabotaging adoption.3
55%
of employers regret AI-driven layoffs — the cost of getting adoption wrong is already being
measured.4
30%
of the workforce has zero AI training; 61% have received fewer than 5 hours.2
12%
of SMBs are 'very likely' to reduce staff due to AI — but fear doesn't know that.1
The market is screaming for someone who addresses fear FIRST.
Everyone else addresses productivity first. Amplified addresses fear first — and productivity
follows. This is not a philosophical distinction. It is a market positioning that no competitor
occupies.

critically, no no-redundancy guarantee. Their nudges optimise within existing employer-employee power

Managed IT Services (MSP Sector)
The trusted SME relationship — pointed at the wrong problem
94% of SMBs rely on an MSP for technology support,3 making this sector the closest structural analogue to
Amplified’s model: an ongoing, trusted, outsourced technology partner. The MSP sector proves that SMEs
want managed technology relationships. But MSPs manage infrastructure — servers, networks, security.
Nobody in the MSP space manages behavioural AI adoption as a service. This is Amplified’s clearest
adjacency and expansion vector: the familiar model (managed service), applied to the unfamiliar problem
(staff-AI trust).

Market Position Statement
"We are creating a new category:
Trust-First AI Partnership"
Amplified Partners does not fit neatly into any existing market category. That is the point. We are defining a
new one.
We are NOT an AI tools vendor
We don't sell software licences. Staff don't log into our platform. Our AI is
embedded in their existing workflows, invisible until it's valuable.
We are NOT AI consulting
We don't leave after the strategy deck. Consultants diagnose and
recommend. We diagnose, implement, stay, and earn trust over months.
We are NOT managed IT
We don't fix servers or manage networks. The MSP model is our closest
structural analogue, but our focus is human behaviour, not infrastructure.
We are NOT HR tech
We don't replace HR departments or HR software. We sit alongside HR,
working directly with staff in a way HR structurally cannot — because we
are independent of the employer hierarchy.
What we are: the ongoing, embedded AI partner that staff trust — because we earned it. We are
retained. We are present. We are accountable. And we are contractually committed to staff welfare in
ways no employer can credibly promise from within.
The Category Definition
Trust-First AI Partnership is characterised by four pillars:
• Contractual Trust — Formal guarantees (no-redundancy, training privacy, three-way SLA) that cannot be
replicated by internal HR or external software vendors.
• Behavioural Science — Disclosed, transparent use of nudge techniques and communication frameworks
designed to reduce fear and build genuine engagement.
• Forensic Understanding — Deep, observational knowledge of how individual staff actually work — not how
management thinks they work.
• Ongoing Partnership — A managed service model with long-term retention, not project-based consulting or
self-service software.

Conclusion
The competitive scan confirms a clear and actionable finding:
The combination of contractual protections, behavioural science, forensic onboarding,
and managed AI partnership does not exist in the market.
Across fifteen-plus competitors in five adjacent categories, we found:
• Employee advocacy platforms treat staff as marketing channels, not people.
• Employee experience platforms serve enterprise HR departments with top-down tools.
• Nudge technology providers — our closest adjacency — operate exclusively at enterprise scale with no SME
offering, no contractual commitments, and no human onboarding.
• AI consultancies deliver strategies and leave. They build no ongoing relationship.
• Managed IT services have the right model (ongoing, outsourced, trusted) pointed at the wrong problem
(infrastructure, not behaviour).
The fear data is equally clear. With 4 in 10 employees fearing for their job security,1 80% of workplaces
experiencing hidden resistance,2 and 55% of employers regretting AI-driven layoffs,3 the market need is not
theoretical. It is urgent, measurable, and growing.
This is not a market-entry opportunity. We are not competing with existing players for existing budget. This
is a category-creation opportunity. Amplified Partners is building a market that does not yet exist — and the
competitive scan confirms that nobody else is building it either.
The market is wide open.
Trust-First AI Partnership is ours to define, ours to build, and ours to lead.
