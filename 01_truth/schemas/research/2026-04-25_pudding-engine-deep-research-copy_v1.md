### 1.3 The Two-Layer Architecture

**Critical insight from Ewan (vault session 2026-01-19):**

> "The labels are generic and complete. The rubric is specific to the use case."

| Layer | What it is | Who designs it | How often it changes |
|-------|------------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Labels** | Universal pattern vocabulary | Once, carefully | Rarely |
| **Rubric** | "What counts as a good mashup for THIS purpose" | Per use case | Often, as you learn |

This separation is the key innovation. The same labels work for SMB consulting, personal learning, creative writing, anything. You swap rubrics depending on what you're trying to achieve.

### 1.4 The ABC Discovery Process (from Canonical Reference)

1. **Research Phase** — Triple search (Brave, Tavily, Exa), success + failure patterns, 4–7 techniques per domain
2. **Neutral Labelling** — Strip expert names and domain, assign semantic dimensions, flatten everything
3. **Pattern Matching** — Find shared dimensions across domains — these are B-terms (bridges)
4. **ABC Connection** — Name A (domain 1), B (bridge mechanism), C (domain 2), state hypothesis
5. **Score the Recipe** — `Score = (Shared Dimensions × 2) + Unique_A + Unique_B` — minimum viable ≥ 5
6. **State the 1+1=3** — Name the emergent insight explicitly. If you can't name it, it's a list, not pudding
7. **Validate or Label** — hypothesis → tested_internal → tested_client → proven → canonical

### 1.5 What Makes Good Pudding (from Canonical Reference)

1. **Cross-domain is essential** — within-domain connections are just good reading
2. **The bridge must be neutral** — if named after one domain, it's a one-way street
3. **Non-obvious is the test** — if both domain experts say "obviously", it's not pudding
4. **State the 1+1=3 explicitly** — the new understanding must be named
5. **Every pudding is hypothesis until tested**

### 1.6 What Kills Pudding (Failure Patterns)

1. Labelling by source, not mechanism
2. Summarising instead of connecting
3. Stopping at two inputs (ABC is minimum; best pudding = A+B+C+D)
4. Collecting without flattening (keep in separate buckets = surface connections only)
5. Skipping the failure search

## PART 2: ACADEMIC VALIDATION — DEEP RESEARCH

### 2.1 Swanson's LBD: State of the Art (2025-2026)

The most comprehensive recent survey is Kastrin, Cestnik & Lavrač (2025), "Recent Advances and Future Directions in Literature-Based Discovery" ([arXiv:2506.12385](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.12385)), which reviews LBD from 2000 to present across three areas:

1. **Knowledge graph construction** — moving beyond simple co-occurrence
2. **Deep learning approaches** — embedding-based methods (DeepWalk, node2vec)
3. **LLM integration** — the transformative frontier

Key finding: **LLMs turn LBD from a pattern-matching task into a reasoning task**. Pre-LLM methods detected pairwise links (drug → disease). LLMs can follow chains of thought in natural language, formulate hypotheses as a scientist would, and integrate problem context and constraints.

### 2.2 Beyond Biomedical: LBD in Non-Medical Domains

A critical survey by Lau & Kwong (2019), "Application of Literature-Based Discovery in Nonmedical Disciplines" ([ACM Digital Library](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3366650.3366660)), documents LBD applied to:

- **Counter-terrorism** — finding hidden connections in intelligence datasets
- **Water purification** — cross-domain technology transfer
- **Technology-social linkage** — connecting technology capabilities to societal needs
- **Materials science** — recovering functional materials discoveries retroactively

This directly validates Ewan's adaptation. The Pudding is not a misuse of Swanson — it's the first serious application of LBD to **business knowledge synthesis**, which is a genuine gap in the literature.

### 2.3 The Knowledge Representation Problem

Pu, Beck & Verspoor (2025), "Enriched knowledge representation in biological fields" ([J. Biomedical Semantics](https://jbiomedsem.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s13326-025-00328-3)), found that:

- Only **20%** of discoveries are perfectly represented with pairwise relationships alone
- **73%** require pairwise + nested relationships
- **7%** require pairwise + nested + hypergraphs

This validates PUDDING 2026's richer 4-position coding system over simple keyword matching. The compact label (`WHAT.HOW.SCALE.TIME`) captures structural relationships that pairwise co-occurrence misses.

### 2.4 Multi-Agent Cross-Domain Discovery

Aryal et al. (2024), "Leveraging Multi-AI Agents for Cross-Domain Knowledge Discovery" ([arXiv:2404.08511](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.08511)), demonstrates:

- **Domain-specific AI agents** collaborating in a unified framework
- **MetaGPT orchestration** — sequential flow with context passing
- **Key finding:** Integration of domain-specific knowledge significantly improves output quality
- The sequential flow with full context passing (Flow 1) produced the highest-rated answers

This validates the Amplified Partners agent council architecture — multiple domain-specialist agents (CEO, COO, CFO, CTO, Nemesis) collaborating with believability weighting is structurally identical to what this research paper proposes.

### 2.5 The Elicit/LBD Frontier (2025)

Elicit's research blog ([elicit.com](https://elicit.com/blog/literature-based-discovery)) provides the most accessible synthesis of where LBD stands today:

- **LLMs enable "daydreaming loops"** — billions of Swanson-style pairings in hours
- **Exploratory creativity** — searching new regions within an existing conceptual space
- **Transformative creativity** — changing the rules of the space itself
- **Key limitation:** "As a stand-alone approach, LBD still remains mostly a theoretical undertaking... Real-world discoveries that make it to clinical trials remain few and far between"

**What this means for PUDDING:** The Pudding has an advantage over academic LBD because it operates on a **closed, curated vault** (4,787 files, all authored or collected by one person with consistent intent) rather than the open ocean of all published literature. The signal-to-noise ratio is dramatically higher.

### 2.6 Categorical Semiotics and Knowledge Integration

Leandro (2024), "Categorical semiotics: Foundations for Knowledge Integration" ([arXiv:2404.01526](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.01526)), proposes using category theory and institutional sketches to integrate knowledge across disparate models and domains. This is mathematically analogous to what PUDDING 2026 does intuitively — creating a neutral vocabulary (the compact codes) that allows structural comparison across any domain.
