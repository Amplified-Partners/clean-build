# covered.AI Website Architecture

## Homepage Structure

### Hero Section

**Headline (H1):**
"We tighten your processes. You keep running your business."

*Rationale:* This 10-word headline directly states our value proposition. It differentiates from "transformation" messaging (competitors) while emphasizing low-friction operation. The parallel structure ("We tighten... You keep running...") creates memorable rhythm.

**Subheadline:**
"We automate the busywork, chase late payments, and document your processes—so everything runs better without changing how you work. Typical results: 10-20 hours back per week, £8K-15K more collected monthly."

*Rationale:* The subheadline expands on the headline with three specific capabilities (automate, chase, document) followed immediately by concrete results. The "without changing how you work" reinforces low-friction positioning. Specific numbers (£8K-15K, 10-20 hours) build credibility.

**Primary CTA:**
Button: "Get Free Process Audit"
Style: Teal background (#00D9C0), Navy text (#0A2540)
*Rationale:* Teal stands out against navy page elements while maintaining brand consistency. The button text is action-oriented and clearly states what's being offered (free, process audit).

**Secondary CTA:**
Button: "See How This Works"
Style: Navy outline, transparent background
*Rationale:* For visitors not ready to commit, this lower-commitment option keeps them in the funnel. The outline style differentiates it visually from the primary CTA.

**Social Proof:**
"Helping 150+ UK SMBs collect £8K+ more per month"

*Rationale:* The specific number (150+) and result (£8K+) builds credibility. "UK SMBs" reinforces local positioning. Placed immediately after CTAs to validate the decision to engage.

### Problem Section

**Section Headline:**
"Most Consultants Want to 'Transform' Your Business. You Just Need It to Work Better."

*Rationale:* This headline directly addresses the competition (consultants who "transform") and positions covered.AI as the practical alternative. The quote marks around "Transform" subtly mock the jargon. "Work better" is achievable and specific.

**Opening Paragraph:**
"You're not broken. Your processes are fine—they just need tightening. Most business owners have systems that mostly work. The problem is the gaps: invoices that slip through, leads that go cold, processes that only exist in your head, and cash that disappears into late payments.

Sound familiar?"

*Rationale:* This paragraph validates the reader (you're not broken) which reduces defensiveness. It names four specific gaps (invoices, leads, processes, cash) making the problem tangible. The rhetorical question creates engagement.

**Three Pain Point Callouts:**

**Pain Point 1:**
"60+ hours/week doing everything yourself"
*Subtext:* The owner bottleneck. You're the bottleneck. You can't scale because everything flows through you.

**Pain Point 2:**
"£8K-15K stuck in late payments every month"
*Subtext:* Your money, sitting in other people's accounts. Chasing feels awkward. You're too busy anyway.

**Pain Point 3:**
"Marketing feels like throwing money at a wall"
*Subtext:* Leads come in, then disappear. No follow-up system. You're not a marketer—and you shouldn't need to be.

**Differentiation Statement:**
"You don't need transformation. You need optimization. We tighten what's already working—without disrupting your operations, retraining your staff, or forcing new software on you."

*Rationale:* This statement crystallizes the positioning difference. "Transformation vs. optimization" is the key distinction. The three negatives (without disrupting, retraining, forcing) address common fears. The section ends with a clear value statement.

## Mobile Optimization

### Responsive Breakpoints

| Breakpoint | Width | Layout Changes |
|------------|-------|----------------|
| Desktop | 1200px+ | Full 12-col grid |
| Tablet | 768-1199px | 8-col grid, reduced margins |
| Mobile | <768px | Single column, stacked |

### Mobile-Specific Considerations

1. **Touch Targets:** Minimum 44×44px for all buttons and links
2. **Font Sizes:** Minimum 16px for body text (prevents zoom on iOS)
3. **Spacing:** 16px minimum margins on mobile
4. **Navigation:** Hamburger menu for mobile
5. **Images:** Lazy load, appropriate sizes
6. **Forms:** Large inputs, appropriate keyboard types
7. **Performance:** Minimize scripts, optimize images
