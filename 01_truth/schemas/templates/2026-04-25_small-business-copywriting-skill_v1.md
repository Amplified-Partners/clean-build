# SMALL BUSINESS COPYWRITING SKILL
## For Service Business Owners Who Are Too Busy Keeping Things Afloat

---

## THE FUNDAMENTAL TRUTH

**Small business owners (5-25 people, trades/services) are not stupid.**

They know EXACTLY what needs doing:
- Answer enquiries faster
- Follow up with quotes
- Get more Google reviews
- Fix their shitty website
- Stay in touch with past customers
- Stop losing track of jobs
- Automate the repetitive bollocks

**The problem:** They're drowning in actual work. Fixing the business requires time they don't have.

**Bad copy:** "Discover the hidden revenue leaks in your business!"
**Good copy:** "Your phone's ringing but you're on a job. I'll sort that."

---

## CORE COPYWRITING PRINCIPLES (2024-2026)

### 1. Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

People don't buy products. They "hire" solutions to make progress in their life.

**The small business owner is "hiring" you to:**
- Free up time currently spent on admin bollocks
- Stop missing enquiries (losing money)
- Make their life less stressful
- Let them focus on actual work
- Look more professional without being fake

**Not "hiring" you to:**
- Transform their business
- Revolutionize their industry
- Implement cutting-edge technology
- Build complex systems

### 2. Specificity Beats Vagueness Always

**Vague:** "Improve your customer acquisition"
**Specific:** "Your website takes 6 seconds to load on mobile. Half your visitors leave. I'll fix that in 2 days."

**Vague:** "Marketing automation"
**Specific:** "When someone fills your contact form, they get a reply in 2 minutes. Even at midnight. Even when you're on a job."

### 3. Their Language, Not Yours

**They say:** "I'm too busy"
**Not:** "I need to optimize my operational efficiency"

**They say:** "Phone keeps ringing when I'm up a ladder"
**Not:** "I need omnichannel customer engagement"

**They say:** "Website's shit"
**Not:** "My digital presence needs enhancement"

### 4. Problem → Agitation → Solution (But Smart)

**Traditional PAS:** 
Problem: Your website is slow
Agitation: You're losing thousands!
Solution: Buy my service!

**Modern PAS (Jobs-focused):**
Problem: Phone rings when you're on a job
Agitation: Miss call = lose job. Happens 5 times a week = £2K/month gone
Solution: I'll set it up so calls get answered properly. Takes 2 days.

**Key difference:** Agitation is math, not fear. Solution is timeboxed and specific.

### 5. Proof Over Promises

**Weak:** "We'll transform your business"
**Strong:** "Last plumber I worked with went from missing 8 calls/week to missing 0. His revenue went up £2,400/month."

**Weak:** "Industry-leading results"
**Strong:** "I did this for my own business - £2M revenue, 30 staff, sold it. Now I help others do the same."

### 6. Friction Reduction Language

Every word that makes them think = friction
Every requirement = friction
Every "contact us for pricing" = friction

**High friction:** "Schedule a discovery call to explore how our solutions might align with your business objectives"
**Low friction:** "Call me: [number]. I'll tell you if I can help. 15 minutes."

## NEWCASTLE/UK SMALL BUSINESS CONTEXT

### Cultural Factors

**1. Northern Directness**
- No corporate speak
- No "reaching out" or "circling back"
- Say what you mean
- Don't dress up bad news

**2. Skepticism of Southern/London Agencies**
- They've been burned before
- "Marketing experts" who've never run a business
- Expensive, jargon-filled, poor results
- Disappeared after taking money

**3. Relationship-Based**
- Trust matters more than credentials
- Local > London
- Referrals > advertising
- Proven > promises

**4. Value-Conscious (Not Cheap)**
- Will pay for quality
- But want to understand what they're getting
- Hate being ripped off
- Respect straight pricing

### Language Patterns

**Use:**
- Sorted
- Properly
- Bollocks
- Shit (when appropriate)
- Actually
- Right (as in "right, here's what we do")

**Avoid:**
- Leverage
- Synergy
- Solutions (as a standalone noun)
- Ecosystem
- Journey (unless literally traveling)
- Transform/revolutionary
- Game-changing
- Cutting-edge

### Business Types & Their Specific Language

**Trades (Plumbers, Electricians, Builders):**
- "On a job" not "with a client"
- "Up a ladder" not "in the field"
- "Booked solid" not "high utilization"
- "Gets hammered" (busy period) not "demand spike"

**Healthcare/Aesthetics:**
- "Patients" not "clients"
- "Clinic" not "facility"
- "Bookings" not "appointments"
- "Waiting room" not "reception area"

**Professional Services:**
- "Fee" not "price"
- "Engagement" not "project"
- "Practice" not "business"
- "Client" is fine here

## HEADLINE FORMULAS THAT WORK

### Formula 1: The Time/Money Equation

**Pattern:** "[Specific problem] = [Specific loss]. Fix it in [Timeframe]."

**Examples:**
- "Website loads slow on mobile = lose half your visitors. Fixed in 2 days."
- "Missing 5 calls per week = £2K/month gone. Sorted in 48 hours."
- "No follow-up on quotes = 60% never convert. Set up in 3 days."

### Formula 2: The Busy Person's Headline

**Pattern:** "You're too busy [doing work]. I'll sort [the business problem]."

**Examples:**
- "You're too busy on jobs to answer the phone. I'll sort that."
- "You're too busy doing the work to fix your website. I'll handle it."
- "You're too busy keeping things afloat to chase quotes. I'll automate it."

### Formula 3: The What vs. How

**Pattern:** "You know [what needs doing]. I know [how to do it without disrupting your business]."

**Examples:**
- "You know you need more Google reviews. I know how to get them without begging."
- "You know your website's shit. I know how to fix it without shutting your business down."
- "You know you should follow up more. I know how to automate it properly."

### Formula 4: The Specific Problem

**Pattern:** "[Specific situation they recognize instantly]"

**Examples:**
- "Phone rings when you're on a job. Customer hangs up. Job lost."
- "Email enquiry comes in at 7pm. You reply at 9am. They've called someone else."
- "You send a quote. Never hear back. Too busy to chase. Happens 10 times a month."

## BODY COPY PRINCIPLES

### Opening Paragraph Rules

**Purpose:** Make them nod their head in recognition

**Structure:**
1. State their specific situation (that they recognize)
2. Quantify the impact (money or time)
3. Explain why it happens (they're busy, not lazy)
4. Offer the fix (specific, timeboxed, understandable)

**Example:**
"Your phone rings 20 times a day. You're on jobs, so you miss 5-8 of them. Each missed call is a potential £300-500 job. That's £1,500-4,000/week you're leaving on the table. Not because you're bad at business - because you're good at the actual work and can't be in two places at once. I can fix this in 48 hours with a system that answers for you, qualifies the caller, and books them straight into your calendar."

### Feature Translation Table

Translate technical features into their actual outcome:

| Feature | What It Actually Means |
|---------|----------------------|
| AI-powered voice assistant | Your phone gets answered even when you're busy |
| Automated email sequences | Quotes get followed up without you remembering |
| CRM integration | You stop copying customer info by hand |
| Website speed optimization | More people actually see your site instead of leaving |
| Review generation system | You get more 5-star reviews without begging |
| Analytics dashboard | You know which marketing actually works |
| Booking automation | Customers book themselves 24/7 |

### Objection Handling (Embedded in Copy)

**Objection 1:** "I've tried marketing companies before, wasted money"

**Counter (embedded):**
"I'm not a marketing company. I ran businesses for 28 years. I know what works because I've done it, not because I read about it."

**Objection 2:** "I don't have time to implement complicated systems"

**Counter:**
"I do the implementation. You just tell me what needs sorting and point me at the right login. Takes you 20 minutes total, takes me 2-3 days."

**Objection 3:** "How do I know it'll actually work?"

**Counter:**
"You don't pay unless it works. I'll show you what I found, tell you what I'll fix, do the work, and you only pay if you're happy with it."

**Objection 4:** "Can't afford it right now"

**Counter:**
"The diagnostic is free. If I find problems costing you £2K/month, and fixing them costs £1K, that pays for itself in 2 weeks. If I don't find anything worth fixing, you pay nothing."

**Objection 5:** "Not sure I trust giving someone access to my systems"

**Counter:**
"Fair. That's why we start with public stuff only - your website, Google reviews, what's visible to everyone. If that's useful and you want me to look deeper, we'll sign proper agreements, everything's documented, and you can pull the plug anytime."

## CALL-TO-ACTION HIERARCHY

### Primary CTA (Hero)
**Purpose:** Lowest friction entry point

**Good:**
- "Get Your Free Diagnostic" 
- "Show Me What's Costing Me Money"
- "Find My Business Leaks"

**Bad:**
- "Schedule a Discovery Call"
- "Book Your Consultation"
- "Learn More About Our Services"

### Secondary CTA (Middle of page)
**Purpose:** After they understand the problem

**Good:**
- "See If I Can Help - Free Check"
- "Find Out What I'd Find"
- "Get the Report"

### Tertiary CTA (Bottom)
**Purpose:** Multiple contact methods

**Good:**
- Call/text: [number] (fastest)
- Email: [email] (if you prefer)
- Book 15 mins: [cal.com]

## PROOF ELEMENTS (What Actually Works)

### Tier 1: Personal Credibility
"28 years running businesses. Built and sold a practice doing £2M/year."

Not credentials - proof you've actually done it.

### Tier 2: Specific Results
"Last plumber I worked with: £8,400 in the first month from enquiries he was previously missing."

Specific number, specific person type, specific outcome.

### Tier 3: Process Transparency
"Here's exactly what happens: [step-by-step with timeframes]"

Shows you've done this before.

### Tier 4: Risk Reversal
"You only pay if I find problems worth fixing. If I don't help, you don't pay."

Makes it safe to try.

### Tier 5: Local Proof
"Newcastle-based. Working with businesses across the North East."

You're one of them, not a London agency.

## THE PRICING CONVERSATION (In Copy)

### Transparency Framework

**Level 1: Acknowledge the question**
"You're wondering what this costs. Fair question."

**Level 2: Explain the model**
"The diagnostic is free. I find what's wrong, show you the impact, tell you what fixing it would cost."

**Level 3: Give ranges**
"Simple fixes: £500-800. System integration: £1,000-2,000. Full overhaul: £3,000-5,000."

**Level 4: Justify the ranges**
"Depends on what needs doing and how complex your setup is. I'll tell you the exact price before starting anything."

**Level 5: Show ROI**
"If you're losing £2K/month and the fix costs £1K, you're in profit in 2 weeks. If I don't find £2K/month in problems, you probably don't need me."

## COMMON COPYWRITING MISTAKES TO AVOID

### ❌ Mistake 1: Selling the Dream
"Transform your business into a 7-figure empire!"

**Why it fails:** They don't want an empire. They want to go home at 6pm.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Feature Listing
"Our platform includes: CRM, automation, analytics, reporting, integrations..."

**Why it fails:** They don't care about features. They care about going home at 6pm.

### ❌ Mistake 3: The Generic Hero
"Struggling with your business? We can help!"

**Why it fails:** Too vague. They don't know if you're talking about them.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Corporate Voice
"We partner with forward-thinking organizations to deliver innovative solutions..."

**Why it fails:** They stopped reading after "partner."

### ❌ Mistake 5: Hiding the Price
"Contact us for a custom quote"

**Why it fails:** Creates friction. Makes them assume it's expensive.

## COPY TESTING FRAMEWORK

### The "Reading Level" Test
Run through Hemingway App. Aim for Grade 6-8 reading level.

Small business owners are smart, but they're reading on their phone while on a job site.

### The "Mate in a Pub" Test
Read it out loud. Would you actually say this to someone in a pub?

If not, rewrite it.

### The "So What?" Test
After every sentence, ask "So what?"

"Our AI-powered system uses advanced algorithms"
→ So what?
"It answers your phone when you're busy"
→ Better.

### The "Skim Test"
Bold only the most important parts. Someone skimming should understand your offer in 15 seconds.

## COMPLETE HEADLINE GENERATOR PROMPT

```
You're writing a headline for a [service] helping [specific business type] solve [specific problem].

Context:
- Business type: [trades/healthcare/professional services]
- Primary problem: [specific situation they face daily]
- Impact: [money lost OR time wasted]
- Location: Newcastle/North East UK
- Business size: 5-25 people

Requirements:
1. Use their language (see language patterns)
2. Be specific about the problem
3. Quantify the impact if possible
4. Keep it under 10 words
5. Make them nod in recognition

Generate 5 options using different formulas:
1. Time/Money equation
2. Busy person's headline
3. Specific situation
4. What vs. How
5. Direct problem statement

Test each with:
- Reading level (Grade 6-8)
- Mate in pub test
- Does it make them nod?
```

## FINAL CHECKLIST FOR ANY COPY

Before publishing, check:

✓ Uses their language, not yours
✓ Specific situations they recognize
✓ Quantified impact (money or time)
✓ Clear what you'll do
✓ Clear how long it takes
✓ Clear what it costs (or that diagnostic is free)
✓ Proof you've done this before
✓ Easy to take next step
✓ No corporate jargon
✓ No vague promises
✓ Nothing that requires them to guess
✓ Would pass the "mate in pub" test
