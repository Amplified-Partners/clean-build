## Phase 1: Brand Identity System

### Task 1.1: Logo Concepts (3 Options)

Create detailed visual descriptions for AI image generation:

**Concept 1: Background Layer**
- Visual: Overlapping transparent layers in navy and teal, suggesting invisible work happening behind the scenes
- Icon style: Abstract geometric shapes with gradient opacity
- Typography pairing: Satoshi Bold (headings) + Inter Regular (body)
- Color application: Navy as base layer, teal as accent layer
- Use case: Primary logo, website header, social media
- Why it works: Conveys "background operation" without being literal

**Concept 2: Safety Net**
- Visual: Protective element (arc or shield shape) containing a stylized "check" or "tightening" symbol
- Icon style: Minimalist line art with rounded corners
- Typography pairing: Same as above
- Color application: Teal shield outline on navy background
- Use case: Favicon, app icon, social proof badges
- Why it works: Conveys trust, safety, and optimization

**Concept 3: Tightening Mechanism**
- Visual: Stylized gear or tightening screw that looks modern and friendly, not mechanical
- Icon style: Solid shapes with subtle gradient
- Typography pairing: Same as above
- Color application: Navy gear with teal center point
- Use case: Secondary logo, footer branding, merchandise
- Why it works: Literally conveys "tightening processes"

**File Specifications:**
- SVG: Primary logo (vector, scalable)
- PNG: 512x512 (app icon), 1024x1024 (social), 2048x2048 (print)
- PDF: Print-ready version
- Favicon: 32x32 PNG + ICO format
- Brand lock-up: Horizontal (logo left, text right) + stacked (logo top, text bottom)

### Task 1.2: Brand Guidelines Document

Create comprehensive brand guidelines including:

**Brand Overview (2 paragraphs)**
- What covered.AI does (low-friction optimization)
- Who it serves (UK service SMBs £500K-£3M)
- How it works (background automation, no disruption)
- Founder credibility (Ewan, 30 years dental, Newcastle)

**Logo Usage**
- Primary logo specifications
- Icon-only version (when text isn't needed)
- Wordmark-only version (when icon isn't needed)
- Reversed version (white on dark backgrounds)
- Clear space rules (minimum padding around logo)
- Minimum sizes (digital: 32px, print: 20mm)
- Never-do list (stretch, rotate, add shadows, etc.)

**Color Palette**
- Primary Navy: #0A2540 (RGB: 10, 37, 64)
- Secondary Teal: #00D9C0 (RGB: 0, 217, 192)
- Body Charcoal: #2D3748 (RGB: 45, 55, 72)
- Background Light: #F7FAFC (RGB: 247, 250, 252)
- White: #FFFFFF
- Accent colors for charts/data

**Accessibility:**
- WCAG AA contrast ratios (4.5:1 for normal text)
- Test all color combinations

**Typography**
- Headings: Satoshi Bold
- Body: Inter Regular
- Monospace: JetBrains Mono (data/code)
- Font sizes: H1 48px, H2 32px, H3 24px, Body 16px, Small 14px
- Line heights: 1.2 for headings, 1.6 for body

**Voice & Tone**
- Do's: Be specific (use numbers), conversational, honest about limitations, benefit-focused
- Don't: Corporate jargon, salesy hype, vague adjectives, transformational language
- Vocabulary: Use "automate" not "synergize," use "save time" not "optimize efficiency"

**Visual Elements**
- Imagery style: Real business owners, not stock photos, UK context (Newcastle references)
- Icon library: Simple, outline-style icons
- Photography treatment: Natural lighting, candid moments, process documentation
- Grid system: 12-column grid for web, consistent margins

**Application Templates**
- Business card (front: logo + name, back: contact + tagline)
- Email signature (logo + tagline + contact + social links)
- LinkedIn banner (1748x424px, brand colors + photo opportunity)
- Website header (consistent across all pages)

### Task 6.3: Objection Handling & Closing Scripts

**Objection: "Sounds too good to be true"**
Response: "I get that. The reason it works is simple—we don't change anything about how you work. We just automate the stuff you're already doing manually. Mark (dental practice in Durham) said the same thing. Four weeks later, he was working 4 days a week. Happy to connect you."

**Objection: "No budget"**
Response: "Totally understand. Let's look at ROI. Even conservatively, you're losing £8K-15K/month to late payments. Our fee is £2,997-4,997. That's paid back in 2-4 months. After that, it's pure profit. Can I show you the numbers?"

**Objection: "Worried about disruption"**
Response: "That's our entire point. We specifically built covered.AI to work without disruption. No new software. No retraining. No workflow changes. We automate around you. Sarah (salon owner Newcastle) said her team didn't even notice anything changed—except the invoices got paid faster."

**Objection: "Think about it"**
Response: "Of course. It's a big decision. What specifically are you thinking about? Is it the timeline, the investment, or whether it's right for your business? I can help with any of those."

**Objection: "Talk to my partner"**
Response: "Makes sense—it's a team decision. Can I send the proposal over so you can both review it? And if you have any questions after, I'm happy to jump on a quick call. What email should I send it to?"

**Objection: "What if it doesn't work?"**
Response: "Fair question. Here's what we do: we don't take the final 50% until you're seeing results. And if after 30 days you're not seeing progress, I'll refund the first 50%. We're that confident. The case studies are real—talk to Mark or Sarah if you want references."

**Closing Technique: Assumptive**
"So looks like this makes sense for you—shall I go ahead and send the invoice? We can get started Monday if you're free."

**Closing Technique: Alternative**
"Do you want to start with the Standard package (£3,997) or the Premium (£4,997)?"

**Closing Technique: Takeaway**
"If timing's not right, I understand. Can I reconnect in 6 months? The offer will be the same, and we might have more capacity then."

**Follow-Up Sequence:**
- Day 5: Email with any questions answered
- Day 10: "Quick check—did you have a chance to review?"
- Day 15: Phone call check-in
- Day 21: "Two spots left this month for onboarding"
- Day 30: "Proposal expired—happy to refresh if still interested"
