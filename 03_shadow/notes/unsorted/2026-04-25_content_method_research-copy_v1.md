## 1. The Original GaryVee Content Model

### 1.1 Document, Don't Create (2016)

The foundation of GaryVee's content philosophy was articulated in a 2016 article: ["Document, Don't Create"](https://garyvaynerchuk.com/creating-content-that-builds-your-personal-brand/).

**Core idea:** Rather than fabricating polished, scripted content, capture and share the authentic real-time journey of building. Documenting (reality-style) is lower friction, more genuine, and more relatable than "creating" (scripted, idealized).

**Key principles:**
- **Authenticity over production value** — the process, mistakes, and intuitions are the content
- **Volume over perfection** — consistent output at high cadence builds audiences; "keep swinging"
- **Your journey IS the story** — beginners, founders, and operators documenting the climb create connection
- **No excuse not to start** — a smartphone is sufficient; overthinking kills output

This was the philosophical underpinning. The *mechanical* system came three years later.

---

### 1.2 The Content Model / Reverse Pyramid (2019)

Detailed in the ["GaryVee Content Strategy" article (2019)](https://garyvaynerchuk.com/the-garyvee-content-strategy-how-to-grow-and-distribute-your-brands-social-media-content/) and the 270-page ["64 Pieces of Content in a Day" deck](https://garyvaynerchuk.com/how-to-create-64-pieces-of-content-in-a-day/):

**Structure:** A top-down "reverse pyramid" — one piece of pillar (long-form) content at the top feeds an increasingly large volume of micro-content at the bottom.

```
┌──────────────────────────────┐
│     PILLAR CONTENT (1)       │  ← Long-form: keynote, vlog, podcast, Q&A
└──────────────────────────────┘
            ↓
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         MICRO-CONTENT (dozens)               │  ← Clips, quotes, blogs, audiograms, carousels
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
            ↓
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   DISTRIBUTION (everywhere)                      │  ← All social/email/search channels
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**The six-step cycle** ([Pepper Content breakdown](https://www.pepper.inc/blog/garyvee-content-strategy/)):

| Step | Action | Detail |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **Create pillar content** | One long-form piece (keynote, vlog, podcast, deep Q&A). Comprehensive, SEO-valuable, detailed |
| 2 | **Repurpose into micro-content** | Break into snackable bits: quotes, clips, carousels, blogs, audiograms, infographics |
| 3 | **Distribute across platforms** | Each piece tailored to platform context (Reels ≠ LinkedIn ≠ Twitter) |
| 4 | **Listen to the community** | Track what resonates, what gets ignored, what the audience asks for |
| 5 | **Second round of micro-content** | Amplify what worked with new copy, angles, titles; drive more engagement |
| 6 | **Repeat** | Fully "juice" one pillar before creating the next |

**GaryVee's three core tactics:**
1. Create top-down, not bottom-up (pillar first, micro second)
2. Document instead of create (authenticity wins)
3. Employ the "sawdust model" of repurposing (nothing wasted — all scraps become content)

**Real-world scale:** One keynote → 30+ pieces → 35M+ views (GaryVee's own team results)

---

### 1.3 What Pillar Content Looks Like in Practice

GaryVee used:
- Daily vlogs (video, 10–45 min)
- Keynote speeches
- Podcast episodes / Q&A shows ("The #AskGaryVee Show")
- Long-form interviews

From each, his team extracted:
- Short video clips for YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, TikTok
- Quote graphics for Instagram/Twitter
- Blog posts from transcripts
- Audiograms (audio + waveform graphic) for Twitter/Instagram
- Twitter/X threads summarizing key points
- Podcast episodes (audio-only extraction)
- LinkedIn articles (expanded takes on single clips)
- Instagram/LinkedIn carousels (multi-slide breakdowns)
- Email newsletters
- Stories with poll/question CTAs

## 2. Modern Improvements & Variations (2025–2026)

### 2.1 Limitations of the Original Model

The original GaryVee model has several friction points when applied in 2025–2026:

| Limitation | Problem |
|------------|---------|
| **Volume emphasis** | Quantity bias can dilute quality; algorithm fatigue on some platforms |
| **Team-dependent** | Requires a production team of editors and writers to realise at scale |
| **Video-first bias** | Built around GaryVee's video-heavy workflow; less useful for text-first builders |
| **No AI layer** | Manual extraction and repurposing is slow and expensive |
| **Platform context missing** | 2019 guidance predates TikTok dominance, LinkedIn algorithm shifts, X/Twitter changes |
| **No feedback loop system** | "Listen" step is vague; no systematic approach to testing and iteration |

---

### 2.2 The AI-Enhanced Content Engine (2025–2026 Standard)

Modern practitioners have layered AI into every step of the pyramid. The standard 2025 version looks like:

**Step 1: Create or record pillar content**
(Same as GaryVee — one long-form document, video, or podcast)

**Step 2: AI extraction & atomization**
Feed the pillar into an AI system with platform-specific prompts. The AI:
- Identifies key insights, statistics, quotes, and arguments
- Tags each insight with platform-fit scores
- Generates platform-native drafts (not generic copy, but LinkedIn-native vs. Twitter-native vs. email-native)

**Step 3: Human refinement**
AI gets you 80% of the way. A human editor applies voice, brand tone, and platform-specific judgment for the remaining 20%. ([AI Marketing Labs, 2025](https://ai-marketinglabs.com/lab-experiments/how-do-i-use-ai-to-repurpose-long-form-b2b-content-into-micro-content))

**Step 4: Automated distribution**
Tools like Buffer, Hypefury, or Taplio schedule and cross-post with platform-optimal timing.

**Step 5: Performance loop**
Analytics tools identify top performers; AI re-atomizes winning content for a second round.

**The 4-step AI framework** (from [AI Marketing Labs](https://ai-marketinglabs.com/lab-experiments/how-do-i-use-ai-to-repurpose-long-form-b2b-content-into-micro-content)):
1. **Establish source of truth** — one high-quality long-form asset stored centrally (e.g., Airtable)
2. **Define outputs** — map target platforms and desired formats
3. **Engineer prompts per platform** — distinct system prompts for tone, structure, length per channel
4. **Execute generation** — route to optimal AI model (Claude 3.5 Sonnet for summarization, GPT-4o for social)

**From one podcast/article, a modern AI pipeline produces in minutes** ([LinkedIn, 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/akibhayat_5-ai-tools-content-marketers-cant-ignore-activity-7435360984833130496-_5e_)):
- 5–7 LinkedIn posts
- 10–15 tweet-sized snippets
- 3–4 email newsletter sections
- 1–2 blog posts
- 1 short video script

### 2.3 The "Content Atomization" Improvement

The modern term for GaryVee's approach is **content atomization** — breaking complex ideas into their smallest possible units, then reassembling them in different combinations for different purposes.

Key improvement over the original model: rather than just slicing the content, practitioners now:
1. **Extract atomic insights** (individual facts, statistics, arguments, quotes)
2. **Tag by platform fit** (which insights work for which audience)
3. **Recombine** — mix atoms from multiple pillar pieces to create "new" content without new research
4. **Audience-segment** — take the same core content and adapt it for different buyer personas by changing examples and terminology

This is sometimes called the **"Content Alchemy"** approach. ([LinkedIn Pulse, 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/content-alchemy-ultimate-guide-ai-powered-repurposing-patterson--ybqwe))

## 3. Alternative Strategist Frameworks

### 3.1 Alex Hormozi: Content Splintering (validated-first approach)

**Source:** [Copyblogger on Hormozi's strategy](https://copyblogger.com/content-repurposing/) | [ThinkDMG breakdown](https://thinkdmg.com/the-alex-hormozi-content-strategy-how-to-repurpose-one-video-into-30-pieces/)

**Key difference from GaryVee:** Hormozi *validates ideas before creating long-form content*. He uses Twitter/X as a testing ground first.

**His system:**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Post 5 tweets/day from daily thoughts — observe what gets engagement |
| 2 | Turn validated (high-engagement) tweets/threads into long-form video |
| 3 | Team extracts 80+ pieces of content from each video |
| 4 | 5 dedicated platform editors (Twitter, YouTube, LinkedIn, Podcast, IG/TikTok) |
| 5 | Distribute 80 posts/week via Buffer |

**Scale:** 2 days/month recording → 80+ pieces/week. Team of 5 editors, ~$40k/year total.
**Metric:** 7 pieces/week pre-system → 80+ pieces/week post-system ([Copyblogger, 2024](https://copyblogger.com/content-repurposing/))

**Output formats per long-form video:**

| Platform | Formats |
|----------|---------|
| Twitter/X | Text quotes, threads, static images |
| YouTube | Long-form video, Shorts |
| LinkedIn | Text posts, image posts, reposts |
| Instagram | Reels, images, Stories |
| TikTok | Short clips |
| Podcast | Full audio episodes |

**Hormozi's "content splintering" principle:** Pre-record multiple CTA variations (subscribe, share, lead magnet, low-ticket offer, grand slam offer) and insert them at beginning/middle/end of long-form content during editing. This monetizes every format without extra effort.

### 3.2 Justin Welsh: Pillar and Spokes (quality over volume)

**Source:** [ThinkDMG breakdown of Justin Welsh system](https://thinkdmg.com/high-impact-low-time-investment-the-justin-welsh-content-system/)

**Key difference from GaryVee:** Welsh optimizes for *depth and trust* over volume. 4 hours/week total content creation. $5M+ revenue, 750K+ followers.

**His system — the 1-3-5 Method:**
- **1 pillar** = one newsletter issue per week (75 minutes to write)
- **3 themes** = three topic areas he consistently writes about
- **5+ posts** = six to twelve social media posts (LinkedIn + X) derived from the newsletter

**Weekly time breakdown:**
| Activity | Time |
|----------|------|
| Newsletter (pillar) | 75 min |
| Social posts (spokes) | 2 hours |
| Community engagement | 45 min/day |
| **Total** | ~4 hours |

**Tools:** Notion (ideation), Typeshare (newsletter drafting), Hypefury (scheduling)

**Welsh's philosophy:** Fewer, deeper posts beat high-volume shallow content for high-trust B2B contexts (consulting, SaaS, professional services). His "attention formula" for 2025 is: pattern disruption + relevance snap + emotional accuracy ([Justin Welsh newsletter, 2025](https://www.justinwelsh.me/newsletter/reverse-engineering-attention-in-2025)).

### 3.3 Nicolas Cole: Ship 30 for 30 / Atomic Essay Method

**Source:** [Nicolas Cole's writing framework](https://www.nicolascole.com) | [YouTube masterclass](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qZgiNgrD1u8)

**Key contribution:** The concept of **"atomic essays"** — 250–300 word idea units published daily. Each atomic essay is a self-contained insight that:
- Tests audience resonance at low cost
- Can be expanded into a pillar piece (bottom-up inverse of GaryVee)
- Can be combined with other atoms into a newsletter, long article, or book

**Cole's improvement to GaryVee:** The pyramid can run in reverse — start with atoms (micro), identify what resonates, then invest in creating long-form on validated ideas. This is the "Ship 30" hypothesis: write 30 atomic essays in 30 days to accelerate your learning of what your audience actually wants.

**Framework integration:** Cole's atomic essays map perfectly onto a GaryVee content model where the atoms become pillar inputs (validated-first), or where a long-form piece is broken into atoms for distribution (top-down repurposing).

### 3.4 Sahil Bloom: Structured Weekly Content Cadence

**Source:** [Sahil Bloom newsletter strategy breakdown](https://enterprisezone.cc/sahil-bloom-details-his-newsletter-strategy-for-delivering-pure-value/) | [Curiosity Chronicle](https://www.sahilbloom.com/newsletter)

**Key contribution:** A *structured multi-format weekly cadence* built from one primary publication. 800,000+ subscribers with near-100% publish consistency.

**Bloom's weekly format:**
| Day | Content Type | Purpose |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| Monday | Reflection question | Sparks engagement, shows depth |
| Wednesday | Deep-dive on one idea | Builds authority on a topic |
| Friday | Practical framework | Immediate reader value |
| Saturday | Week-in-review | Synthesis + habit formation |

**Key insight:** Each week's content is thematically unified but format-diverse. This creates a "content personality" — readers know what to expect from each touchpoint. The newsletter is the pillar; social posts are the spokes. His approach: write every issue "like I'm sending it to my closest friend."

### 3.5 The "Lenny Rachitsky" B2B Content Flywheel

**Source:** [StartupGTM Substack Lenny playbook breakdown](https://startupgtm.substack.com/p/the-complete-lenny-rachitsky-playbook)

Lenny (Lenny's Newsletter) demonstrates the most effective B2B content model for a technical/founder audience:

**Structure:**
- One deep-research newsletter issue (pillar)
- Podcast episodes on same topics (second pillar, different format)
- Derivatives: LinkedIn posts, X threads, carousels, email segments
- Community (Slack) as content distribution amplifier

**"Lennification" playbook for companies:**
1. Share real internal artifacts (decision memos, onboarding docs, pricing frameworks)
2. Publish as source post → slice into threads, carousels, email drops, video explainers
3. Use backstory in podcasts and founder storytelling
4. Turn internal wins into "How we improved [X] by [Y%]" posts

This maps directly onto a build-in-public strategy: *company artifacts become content*.

### 4.3 Prompt Engineering for Content Atomization

Key principle: **system prompts define brand voice; user prompts define structure**.

**LinkedIn post prompt pattern:**
```
System: You are a [company] content writer. Our brand voice is [direct/technical/conversational]. 
We write for [ICP description]. We do not use buzzwords. We use short sentences.
User: Extract the single most compelling insight from this article and write a LinkedIn post. 
Hook in line 1 (10 words max). Body: 3 short paragraphs. End with a question. 150–200 words total.
[paste content]
```

**X thread prompt pattern:**
```
User: Deconstruct the 5 main arguments in this piece into a Twitter thread. 
Tweet 1: hook/claim. Tweets 2–6: one argument each with a concrete example. 
Tweet 7: synthesis + CTA. Max 280 characters per tweet.
[paste content]
```

**Email snippet prompt pattern:**
```
User: Write a 75-word teaser for this article to feature in a B2B newsletter. 
Focus on the problem it solves for [ICP]. End with "Read more →" CTA. 
Do not reveal the solution — create curiosity.
```

## 6. B2B/AI Company "Build in Public" Best Practices

### 6.1 Why "Build in Public" Is a Content Model, Not Just a Philosophy

Build in public (BIP) is GaryVee's "Document, Don't Create" applied to company building. It naturally generates pillar content because the work being documented is inherently substantive: architectural decisions, product launches, metric milestones, hiring, mistakes, pivots.

The content pyramid becomes:
```
Pillar: internal artifact or documented decision
         ↓
Micro-content: extracted insights for different audiences and platforms
```

### 6.2 Content Types Specific to B2B/AI Build-in-Public

Based on analysis of PostHog, Linear, Vercel, and other technical companies doing this well:

**PostHog's approach** ([PostHog content strategy](https://posthog.com/blog/running-content-at-posthog)):
- Every department contributes content in their domain (engineering writes builds, HR writes hiring, CEO writes vision)
- Interview format: 40–60 min video → transcribe → blog post + podcast + social snippets + email
- Publishes to GitHub for feedback before finalising — community becomes editorial input
- Minimum 2 pieces/week for algorithm consistency

**PostHog's 40 developer marketing lessons** ([PostHog newsletter](https://posthog.com/newsletter/marketing-for-devs)):
- Be opinionated: challenge status quo, assume developers are smart
- Feature-first, not benefit-first (developers distrust vague claims)
- Humor outperforms authority-signalling on social
- AI platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity) now drive 41x more traffic than 12 months prior — optimize for citation by LLMs
- "How did you hear about us?" box outperforms attribution tools for real signal
- Transparency posts (funding, decisions, failures) consistently outperform promotional posts early-stage

**10 proven build-in-public content types** ([YouTube BIP guide for B2B SaaS](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AKcAn8-s-cM)):

| Content Type | Why It Works |
|--------------|-------------|
| F**k-ups / failures | Shows authenticity, high engagement, builds trust |
| Wins (with numbers) | Social proof, motivation for followers |
| Product updates | Surfaces new features, keeps audience informed |
| Behind-the-scenes process | Educational, highly shareable in technical communities |
| Data/metrics milestones | Concrete and credible ("Day 50: $X ARR") |
| Polls on roadmap decisions | Involves audience in product direction |
| Personal learnings | Shows founder growth, relatable |
| Sales/growth experiments | "Here's what we tested and the result" |
| Industry commentary | Positions company as authority |
| Customer stories | Social proof with narrative |

### 6.3 The "Lenny Playbook" for B2B Founders — Practical Templates

From [StartupGTM's Lenny analysis](https://startupgtm.substack.com/p/the-complete-lenny-rachitsky-playbook), the cross-team content flywheel:

```
1. Product posts a feature breakdown ("Why we built X")
2. Marketing repackages it into a newsletter
3. Sales uses it in outreach ("Here's how a customer solved [problem]")
4. CS turns it into a how-to for onboarding
5. Enablement trains the team on it
```

Each internal artifact = potential pillar content. Every week, ask: **"What story from this week deserves to be more than a Slack message?"**

**Proven post templates for B2B/AI BIP:**

*Feature launch template:*
```
Why We Built [Feature Name]
- The problem it solves
- Why now?
- Internal debates + tradeoffs
- How early customers are using it
- What's next
```

*Internal win template:*
```
How we improved [KPI] by [X%]
- What we were trying to solve
- What we did (3–5 bullets with specifics)
- What surprised us / what failed
- What you can take from this
```

*Failure/lesson template:*
```
We tried [X]. It didn't work. Here's why.
- What we expected
- What actually happened
- The root cause
- What we're doing instead
```

### 6.4 B2B Content Distribution Channels (Ranked by ROI for AI/Technical Companies)

| Channel | Audience | Best Format | Effort |
|---------|----------|-------------|--------|
| **LinkedIn** | Founders, operators, buyers | Long-form posts, carousels, video clips | Medium |
| **X (Twitter)** | Technical community, early adopters | Threads, atomic posts, replies | Low |
| **Newsletter (Beehiiv/Substack)** | Owned audience, high-intent | Deep-dive articles, weekly roundup | High (one-time) |
| **YouTube** | Developers, researchers | Tutorials, demos, build vlogs | High |
| **GitHub** | Developers | Open-source releases, README content | Medium |
| **Reddit / HN** | Technical community | Long-form, opinionated posts | Variable |
| **Podcast** | Professionals on the go | Interviews, solo episodes | Medium |
| **SEO Blog** | Search traffic | Technical guides, comparisons | High |

**PostHog's finding:** AI platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity) now deliver 41x more referral traffic YoY — structuring content to be LLM-citable is a new growth lever ([PostHog newsletter](https://posthog.com/newsletter/marketing-for-devs)).

### 6.5 What Makes Technical/AI Company Content Stand Out

1. **Specificity over generality** — "We reduced latency by 40ms by switching to streaming" beats "We improved performance"
2. **Transparent numbers** — ARR milestones, user counts, error rates, before/after metrics
3. **Technical depth** — don't dumb down; assume competent readers
4. **Authentic failures** — technical audiences can smell over-polish; show real debugging sessions and wrong turns
5. **LLM-optimized structure** — answer-first blocks, semantic chunking, FAQs, TL;DRs ([Lureon AI content optimization guide](https://lureon.ai/blog/llm-content-optimization-in-2025/))
6. **Ship notes as content** — treat every deployment, refactor, and architectural decision as a content candidate

## 8. Recommended Stack for a B2B AI Company

### 8.1 The Adapted Model for bykerbusinesshelp.ai

Given the context — a B2B/AI company doing "build in public" — here is the recommended adaptation of the GaryVee model:

**Pillar content type:** Written long-form article or documented decision
- Reason: Text-first creation is lower friction than video for a technical/B2B company, easier to atomize, and better for LLM citation

**Cadence:** 1 pillar piece per week minimum

**Extraction rule:** Target 10–15 pieces per pillar (not 64). Quality > quantity.

**Platform focus:** LinkedIn (primary), X/Twitter (secondary), Newsletter (owned), Blog/website (SEO + LLM citation)

---

### 8.2 Weekly Content Production System (4-Hour Budget)

```
Monday (90 min): Write pillar — one long-form article or documented insight
Tuesday (60 min): AI atomization — feed into Claude/GPT with platform prompts
Wednesday (45 min): Human refinement — edit AI outputs, add voice
Thursday (30 min): Schedule — queue posts via Buffer/Hypefury
Friday (15 min): Engage — respond to comments, note what landed
```

**Output per week:** 1 long-form post + 6–10 derivative pieces + 1 newsletter section

## 9. Key Takeaways & Synthesis

### What GaryVee Got Right (Still Valid in 2026)
1. **The pillar-first, micro-content-second hierarchy** is correct and timeless
2. **Document, Don't Create** is the best antidote to creator's block for builders
3. **Volume matters** — consistent presence beats occasional perfection
4. **Platform context** — the same insight needs different containers for different audiences
5. **The "sawdust" principle** — nothing from the creative process needs to be wasted

### What Has Been Improved Since 2019
1. **AI removes the team requirement** — solo founders can now replicate a 5-person content team with the right AI workflow
2. **Validation-first (Hormozi)** — testing ideas on short-form before investing in long-form reduces wasted effort
3. **Quality floor raised (Justin Welsh)** — high-trust B2B contexts require fewer, deeper pieces, not 64 pieces/day
4. **LLM optimisation is a new layer** — structuring content for AI citation is now a distribution strategy
5. **Build-in-public is a full content system** — internal artifacts are the most authentic and credible pillar content a technical company can produce
6. **Cross-platform automation** — distribution no longer requires manual effort; tools handle scheduling, format adaptation, and posting

### The One-Sentence Summary of the Best Modern Approach
> Document your real work as long-form writing, feed it through AI to generate platform-native micro-content, publish consistently on 2–3 owned channels, and let the signals from your audience tell you what to build next.
