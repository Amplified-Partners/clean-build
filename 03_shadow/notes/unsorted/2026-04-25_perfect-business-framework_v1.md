## PART 2: THE KAIZEN APPROACH (Small, Consistent Changes)

**Kaizen = "Change for the better"**

The Japanese philosophy: **Significant results from continuous small improvements.**

Instead of trying to fix everything at once (which fails), you implement 2-3 small changes per week, building momentum over time.

### **Why Kaizen Works**

1. **Less resistance** - Small change = easier for team to accept
2. **Lower risk** - If it fails, impact is minimal
3. **Momentum builds** - 2-3 per week Ã— 52 weeks = 100+ improvements per year
4. **Learning culture** - Team sees improvement works, gets excited
5. **Sustainable** - Doesn't burn people out
6. **Compound effect** - Small changes stack (1% per week = 52% improvement per year)

### **The PDCA Cycle (Plan-Do-Check-Act)**

Every improvement follows this pattern:

1. **Plan** - Identify problem, design small fix
2. **Do** - Implement (small test)
3. **Check** - Did it work? Measure results
4. **Act** - If works, make permanent. If not, try different approach

**Typical Kaizen improvement cycle: 1 week**
