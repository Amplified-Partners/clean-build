# Covered AI - Three Apps, Designed to be Loved

## Expert Panel Consultation

### The Team

**Don Norman** (Cognitive Scientist, "Design of Everyday Things")
> "Good design is actually a lot harder to notice than poor design, in part because good designs fit our needs so well that the design is invisible."

**Steve Krug** (Usability Expert, "Don't Make Me Think")
> "Get rid of half the words on each page, then get rid of half of what's left."

**Aarron Walter** (Emotional Design, "Designing for Emotion")
> "Personality is the platform for emotion. If we want users to fall in love with our software, we need to make them feel something."

**Nir Eyal** (Behavioral Design, "Hooked")
> "The ultimate goal of a habit-forming product is to solve the user's pain by creating an association so that the user identifies the company's product or service as the source of relief."

**BJ Fogg** (Stanford Behavior Lab)
> "Simplicity changes behavior. Make the target behavior tiny, then celebrate immediately."

**Jakob Nielsen** (Usability Heuristics)
> "Users spend most of their time on other sites. This means that users prefer your site to work the same way as all the other sites they already know."

**Dieter Rams** (Industrial Design - 10 Principles)
> "Good design is as little design as possible. Less, but better."

---

## Three Apps Overview

| App | Users | Core Job | Emotion Target |
|-----|-------|----------|----------------|
| **1. Customer App** | End customers of our clients | Book, pay, communicate | "This is easy" |
| **2. Client App** | Business owners & staff | Run the business | "I'm in control" |
| **3. Ewan App** | You (admin/founder) | Manage everything | "I see everything" |

# APP 1: Customer App
## "The Service App"

### Who Uses It
- Mrs. Thompson who called about a boiler
- Dave who needs an emergency plumber
- Sarah booking her next hair appointment

### Their Mindset
- Stressed (something's broken) OR
- Busy (just want to book quickly)
- Low patience for complexity
- May not be tech-savvy

### Expert Guidance Applied

**Don Norman:** "Make the right thing to do the obvious thing to do"
→ ONE primary action per screen. No hunting.

**Steve Krug:** "Don't make me think"
→ Zero learning required. Grandma-proof.

**Aarron Walter:** "Reduce anxiety through personality"
→ Warm, reassuring tone. "We've got you."

### Customer App - Design Principles

1. **No login required** - Magic links only
2. **No app download** - Web app, add to home screen optional
3. **Business branded** - Feels like THEIR plumber's app
4. **Single purpose per page** - One job, done
5. **Large tap targets** - Minimum 48px
6. **High contrast** - Readable in bright sun
7. **Works offline** - Shows last booking even without signal

# APP 2: Client App
## "The Business App"

### Who Uses It
- Ralph (Titan Plumbing owner) checking calls between jobs
- His office manager Sarah logging jobs
- The apprentice checking the schedule

### Their Mindset
- Busy, often on the move
- Checking phone in 30-second bursts
- Want to feel in control
- Worried about missing something important

### Expert Guidance Applied

**BJ Fogg:** "Tiny habits, big results"
→ Morning check-in takes 10 seconds. Shows everything that matters.

**Nir Eyal:** "Variable rewards create engagement"
→ Celebrating wins (reviews, payments) creates positive associations.

**Don Norman:** "Visibility of system status"
→ Always know: Is Gemma working? Are there problems?

**Dieter Rams:** "As little design as possible"
→ No decorative elements. Everything serves a purpose.

### Client App - Key Features

1. **10-second morning check** - See everything that matters instantly
2. **One-tap actions** - Call, SMS, book directly from anywhere
3. **Gemma status always visible** - Green dot = working
4. **Proactive alerts** - Pushed to phone, not hidden in app
5. **Celebration moments** - Payments, reviews get visual reward
6. **Progressive disclosure** - Simple by default, details on tap
7. **Offline capable** - See recent calls even without signal

## Psychological Principles in Action

### 1. Progress & Completion
- Show "All clear" prominently when nothing needs doing
- Use checkmarks liberally for completed items
- Progress bars for invoices, onboarding

### 2. Reducing Anxiety
- Always show Gemma status (green dot)
- "We've got your details" reassurance for customers
- Clear next steps, never dead ends

### 3. Celebration
- Payment received → Brief animation
- 5-star review → Prominent display
- Milestone → Acknowledgment

### 4. Cognitive Load Reduction
- Max 3-4 items visible at once
- Progressive disclosure (tap for more)
- Consistent patterns throughout

### 5. Trust Building
- Show real data, not vanity metrics
- Acknowledge when things aren't perfect
- Human-friendly language, not corporate

## Technical Requirements

### Shared
- React Native (or Expo) for mobile
- Next.js for web versions
- Tailwind for consistent styling
- Real-time updates via WebSocket

### Customer App
- No auth required (magic links)
- PWA for add-to-home-screen
- Works offline
- Dynamic business branding

### Client App
- Auth required (magic link or password)
- Push notifications
- Offline-first with sync
- Add-to-home-screen prompt

### Ewan App
- Strong auth (password + optional 2FA)
- Desktop-first (also mobile)
- Real-time system monitoring
- Full data access
