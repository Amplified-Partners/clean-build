## Brand Identity System

### Logo Concepts

**File:** `brand/logo-concepts.md`

Three logo concepts were developed:

| Concept | Visual Description | Best For |
|---------|-------------------|----------|
| **Background Layer** | Overlapping semi-transparent layers suggesting invisible optimization | Digital-first, tech-forward positioning |
| **Safety Net** | Protective element (shield/umbrella) with subtle AI styling | Trust-focused, risk-averse SMBs |
| **Tightening Mechanism** | Gear/connection nodes optimizing flow | Process-focused, efficiency-minded clients |

**Recommendation:** Background Layer concept—aligns with "low-friction, no disruption" positioning.

### Brand Guidelines

**File:** `brand/brand-guidelines.md`

**Core Identity:**
- **Name:** covered.AI (with period, lowercase "AI")
- **Tagline:** "We tighten your processes. You keep running your business."
- **Positioning:** The background optimizer—no disruption, just results
- **Founder:** Ewan Bramley (30 years dental practice experience)
- **Location:** Byker, Newcastle upon Tyne, UK

**Color Palette:**
| Color | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|-------|-----|-----|-------|
| Navy Blue (Primary) | #0A2540 | 10, 37, 64 | Headers, CTAs, trust elements |
| Bright Teal (Secondary) | #00D9C0 | 0, 217, 192 | Innovation, accents, CTAs |
| Charcoal Gray (Text) | #2D3748 | 45, 55, 72 | Body text |
| Light Gray (Background) | #F7FAFC | 247, 250, 252 | Section backgrounds |

**Typography:**
- **Headings:** Satoshi Bold
- **Body:** Inter Regular
- **Monospace:** JetBrains Mono (data/code elements)

### Voice & Tone Guidelines

**Voice Principles:**
1. Straightforward, human, non-corporate
2. Specific numbers, not vague adjectives
3. Honest about limitations
4. Conversational but compelling
5. NO corporate jargon, NO salesy hype

**Forbidden Phrases:**
- ❌ "Leverage," "synergize," "transform"
- ❌ "Cutting-edge," "revolutionary," "game-changing"
- ❌ "Best-in-class," "world-class," "enterprise-grade"
- ❌ "Seamless," "effortless," "magical"

**Approved Alternatives:**
- ✅ "Automate," "fix," "tighten"
- ✅ Specific numbers (£8K, 10-20 hours, 30%)
- ✅ "We work around your systems"
- ✅ "Just works," "gets results"

**File:** `blog/ai-prompts.md`

```
You are an expert copywriter for UK SMBs. Write a 2,500-word blog post about [TOPIC].

- Company: covered.AI (low-friction optimization for UK SMBs)

- Use specific numbers (£8K, 10-20 hours, 30%)
- Avoid corporate jargon (no "transform," "leverage," "synergize")

- Position low-friction (work around their systems)
- No vague adjectives (no "best-in-class," "cutting-edge")

Structure:
1. Introduction (150-200 words): Hook + what you'll learn + why it matters
2. The Problem (300-400 words): Specific pain, why SMBs face it, consequences
3. Why Most Approaches Fail (250-350 words): Common mistakes, why they fail
4. The Low-Friction Solution (400-500 words): Approach, step-by-step, example
5. How to Implement (350-450 words): Actionable steps, tools, quick wins
6. Results Expected (250-350 words): Metrics, 30/90-day timelines, examples
7. Conclusion (150-200 words): Recap + CTA

CTA: "Book Free Audit" or "Download [Lead Magnet]"

Include:
- H1 with primary keyword
- H2s with keyword variations
- Meta description (160 chars)
- URL slug (SEO-friendly)
- Internal links to related posts
- Image descriptions for hero and inline images
```

## Sales & Discovery Process

### Discovery Call Framework

**File:** `sales/discovery-call-script.md`

**Call Structure (30 minutes):**

| Phase | Time | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| Intro | 2 min | Establish credibility, set expectations |
| Rapport | 2 min | Learn about their business |
| Pain Discovery | 8-10 min | Identify biggest challenges |
| Situation Audit | 6-8 min | Quantify the leaks |
| Present Solution | 4-6 min | 4-step process + case study |
| Handle Objections | 2-3 min | Address concerns |
| Next Steps | 2 min | Proposal or close |

**Essential Questions:**

1. "What's the biggest challenge keeping you up at night?"
2. "How many hours per week on non-billable work?"
3. "Do you chase late payments?"
4. "What would 20 hours back mean to you?"
5. "Have you tried fixing this before?"

**Pain Discovery Flow:**

```
"Admin/Time" → 30+ hours? → Family/time off? → MOTIVATED
"Cash Flow" → £5K+ overdue? → Chase manually? → MOTIVATED
"Leads" → Inconsistent? → 20%+ lost? → MOTIVATED
"Can't Step Away" → No holidays? → MOTIVATED
```

**Red Flags (Not a Fit):**
- Not the decision maker
- Happy with status quo
- Wants "transformation" not "optimization"
- Expects overnight results
- Wrong size (revenue, staff, geography)

### Objection Handling

**File:** `sales/objection-handling.md`

**Common Objections & Responses:**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Sounds too good to be true" | "I get that. I'm not doing anything magical—just fixing the gaps most businesses ignore. Want a case study with specific numbers?" |
| "No budget" | "On average, clients recover investment in 6-8 weeks. Could we revisit in a few months?" |
| "Worried about disruption" | "I don't change your systems, don't retrain staff, don't force new software. Just automate gaps. Your team won't notice." |
| "Need to think about it" | "Absolutely. What specific aspects? I'll address in the proposal." |
| "Talk to partner" | "Happy to join a call with both of you, or send proposal to you both." |
| "Not the right time" | "Understood. When would be better? I'll follow up in 2-3 months." |

**Closing Techniques:**
1. **Assumptive:** "Let's start Monday—I'll send the invoice."
2. **Alternative:** "Next Monday or the following?"
3. **Takeaway:** "If this isn't the right fit now, let's reconnect in 6 months."
4. **Question:** "What would need to be true for you to move forward?"
5. **Payment Plan:** "We can do 50/50 if that helps."
