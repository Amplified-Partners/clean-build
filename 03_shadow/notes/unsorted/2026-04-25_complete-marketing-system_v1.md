# covered.AI Complete Marketing & Brand System

**Version:** 1.0

**Author:** Ewan Bramley, Founder  
**Location:** Byker, Newcastle upon Tyne, UK  
**Target:** UK SMBs (£500K-£3M revenue, 3-15 staff)

## Executive Summary

This document contains the complete marketing and brand system for covered.AI, a low-friction optimization service for UK service businesses. The system is designed to be executed immediately with no gaps, ambiguations, or missing pieces. Every element has been designed to work together cohesively, reinforcing the core positioning: "We tighten your processes. You keep running your business."

The philosophy behind every element is simple: UK SMB owners are overwhelmed, suspicious of consultants, and have no time for disruption. Every touchpoint must validate their current work, offer specific value, and require zero behavioral change from them. The goal is not to "transform" their business but to optimize what already exists, running invisibly in the background while delivering measurable results.

All content is UK-specific, uses concrete numbers, avoids corporate jargon, and emphasizes the founder's 30 years of dental practice experience. The system is designed to work with MiniMax 2.1 for content generation, HubSpot for automation, and LinkedIn for outreach.

## Phase 1: Brand Identity System

### 1.1 Logo Concepts

The logo must communicate three things simultaneously: the AI/technology element, the protective/optimizing nature of the service, and the low-friction positioning. Below are three distinct concepts, each with detailed specifications for AI image generation.

#### Concept A: Background Layer

**Visual Description:** A stacked composition of three translucent horizontal layers in navy, teal, and charcoal. Each layer overlaps the next slightly, creating depth without clutter. The layers represent different operational areas (cash flow, leads, processes) being optimized simultaneously. The covered.AI wordmark sits to the right in Satoshi Bold, with a subtle dot after "AI" that pulses softly in teal.

**Icon Style:** Geometric, layered, modern. Flat design with subtle transparency effects. No gradients, shadows, or 3D elements. The icon works at 32px favicon size and scales cleanly to 512px.

**Typography Pairing:** Satoshi Bold for primary wordmark, Inter Regular for supporting text. The dot after "AI" serves as the visual anchor and can be used independently as a favicon.

**Color Application:** Primary navy (#0A2540) forms the bottom layer, representing trust and stability. Bright teal (#00D9C0) forms the middle layer, representing the AI/automation component. Charcoal gray (#2D3748) forms the top layer with 50% opacity, representing the invisible, background nature of the work.

**Use Case:** Primary logo for website header, email signatures, business cards, and all formal communications. The icon-only version works for social media profile pictures and favicon.

**Why It Works:** This concept visually represents "layers of optimization working together invisibly." The transparency suggests background operation. The navy base communicates trust and professionalism without being corporate. The teal accent signals innovation without being flashy.

**AI Image Generation Prompt:** "A modern, minimalist logo for a business optimization company called covered.AI. Three horizontal translucent rectangles stacked and overlapping slightly, creating depth. Bottom rectangle in deep navy blue, middle in bright teal, top in semi-transparent charcoal gray. Clean, geometric style, flat design, no shadows or gradients. Professional and trustworthy. White background. To the right, the text 'covered.AI' in a modern sans-serif bold font. Subtle dot after 'AI'. Logo design, vector style, clean lines, minimalist, tech company aesthetic."

**File Specifications:**
- Primary logo (SVG, PNG 512px, PNG 1024px, PNG 2048px, PDF)
- Icon-only version (32px favicon, 128px, 256px, 512px)
- Wordmark-only version (horizontal, stacked)
- Reversed version (white logo on navy, teal, gray backgrounds)
- Clear space: 20px minimum on all sides
- Minimum size: 32px width for icon, 100px for full logo

#### Concept B: Safety Net

**Visual Description:** A stylized shield shape formed by two interlocking curves, resembling both a safety net and a magnifying glass simultaneously. The shield protects what's inside while the magnifying glass element suggests examination and optimization. The interior of the shield contains a simple network node pattern in teal, representing connected systems and automation.

**Icon Style:** Minimalist line art with thick, confident strokes. The shield has rounded corners and feels approachable rather than corporate. The network nodes inside are simple dots connected by thin lines, suggesting invisible connections and automation.

**Typography Pairing:** Satoshi Bold for the wordmark, positioned below the icon. The "covered" portion is slightly larger than ".AI" to emphasize the protective nature.

**Color Application:** Navy outline for the shield (trust, protection), teal interior nodes (innovation, automation). White negative space inside the shield creates contrast.

**Use Case:** Best for LinkedIn company page, social media profiles, and any context where the protective/optimizing duality needs emphasis. Works well in dark mode interfaces.

**Why It Works:** The shield immediately communicates "protection" and "safety," which resonates with owners worried about disruption. The magnifying glass element subtly suggests examination and optimization. The network nodes inside represent the invisible automation running in the background.

**AI Image Generation Prompt:** "A minimalist logo icon for a business optimization company. A stylized shield shape with rounded corners, formed by two interlocking curved lines. Inside the shield, a simple network of 3 dots connected by thin lines in bright teal. The shield outline is in deep navy blue. Clean line art style, thick confident strokes, white background. Icon design, vector style, minimalist, professional, approachable. Suggesting both protection and examination."

**File Specifications:** Same as Concept A. The shield icon works well at small sizes due to its simple geometry.

#### Concept C: Tightening Mechanism

**Visual Description:** A stylized gear or tightening mechanism formed by overlapping circular arcs, suggesting optimization and incremental improvement rather than transformation. The arcs are arranged in a circular pattern around a central point, with the covered.AI wordmark radiating outward. The teal accent appears in the central hub, suggesting the AI engine driving the optimization.

**Icon Style:** Abstract geometric, suggesting mechanical precision without being industrial. The arcs represent different business processes being synchronized and tightened. Clean, modern, tech-forward.

**Typography Pairing:** Satoshi Bold for wordmark, positioned below the icon in a circular arrangement that mirrors the arc pattern.

**Color Application:** Charcoal gray for the arcs (processes, systems), bright teal for the central hub (AI, automation), navy accents on key arc endpoints.

**Use Case:** Best for technical audiences, process-focused content, and any context where "tightening" and "optimization" need visual reinforcement.

**Why It Works:** The mechanical imagery communicates precision and optimization. The circular pattern suggests continuous improvement rather than one-time change. The central teal hub makes the AI component visible without being overwhelming.

**AI Image Generation Prompt:** "A modern abstract logo icon for a business optimization company. ### 1.2 Brand Guidelines

#### Brand Overview

covered.AI is a low-friction optimization service for UK service businesses. We don't transform, revolutionize, or disrupt—we tighten existing processes, automate busywork, and run invisibly in the background while delivering measurable results. Founded by Ewan Bramley after 30 years of running a dental practice in Byker, Newcastle upon Tyne, we understand firsthand the challenges of being an owner-operator: 60+ hour weeks, late payments, inconsistent leads, and the constant feeling that you're doing everything yourself.

Our positioning is clear: we are the background optimizer. We identify leaks in cash flow, automate invoicing and follow-ups, tighten lead capture systems, and document processes so your team can execute them consistently. We work with businesses generating £500K-£3M annually, with 3-15 staff, where the owner is bottleneck and operations need tightening, not transformation.

The brand voice is straightforward, human, and specific. We use numbers not adjectives. We validate what businesses are already doing right. We're honest about limitations. We're compelling without being salesy. We never use corporate jargon, buzzwords, or empty promises.

#### Voice and Tone

**Do's:**
- Use specific numbers (£8K, 10-20 hours, 30%)
- Validate what prospects are already doing right
- Be honest about limitations and realistic timelines
- Use conversational, direct language ("you," "we," "I")
- Reference UK context (pounds, NHS references where relevant)
- Acknowledge pain points with empathy
- Use active verbs and concrete nouns
- Write like a human, not a corporation

**Don'ts:**
- Use corporate jargon (synergize, leverage, optimize, transform)
- Use vague adjectives (amazing, incredible, revolutionary)
- Make exaggerated promises (double your revenue overnight)
- Be salesy or pushy
- Use passive voice excessively
- Reference American contexts or currencies
- Assume the prospect is broken or doing things wrong
- Use buzzwords (AI, cutting-edge, game-changing)

**Vocabulary Guidelines:**

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| Transform | Tighten, improve, optimize |
| Revolutionize | Streamline, automate, simplify |
| Leverage | Use, apply, employ |
| Synergy | Combined effect, working together |
| Best-in-class | Proven, reliable, effective |
| Cutting-edge | Modern, current, automated |
| Disrupt | Change without disruption |
| Paradigm shift | Significant improvement |
| World-class | High-quality, professional |
| Holistic | Complete, comprehensive |

**Example Transformations:**

Wrong: "We leverage cutting-edge AI to synergize operational efficiencies and transform your business processes."

Right: "We automate the boring stuff so you can focus on what actually makes money."

Wrong: "Our revolutionary platform delivers game-changing results through seamless integration."

Right: "We'll set up automated invoice reminders and run them in the background. No changes to how you work."

Wrong: "We help you scale to the next level with our holistic approach."

Right: "You'll get 10-20 hours back each week. We handle the admin while you handle clients."

The goal of this call is simple: show you where your leaks are. If we can help, I'll outline how. If not, I'll tell you honestly. Sound good?"

**2. Build Rapport (2 minutes)**
"Tell me a bit about your business. How long have you been running it? What's the team size? What does a typical week look like for you?"

**3. Pain Discovery (8-10 minutes)**
Questions:
- "What's the biggest challenge you've faced in the last 12 months?"
- "How many hours are you working right now?"
- "Where does your time go? Admin, billing, marketing?"
- "What's your current situation with late payments?"
- "If you could get 20 hours back, what would you do with it?"
- "Have you tried fixing this before? What happened?"

**4. Situation Audit (6-8 minutes)**
"Walk me through your process for [billing/lead follow-up/admin]. Where do things tend to get stuck?"

Identify 2-3 leaks. For each:
- "So you typically see [X] happen. How much would you say that costs you monthly?"
- "If we could fix that, what would that be worth to you?"

**5. Present Solution (4-6 minutes)**
"Based on what you've told me, here are the gaps I'm seeing: [1, 2, 3]. Here's how we'd fix each:

For [gap 1]: We'd set up automated reminders that run invisibly. No changes to your process.

For [gap 2]: We'd create a follow-up sequence that catches leads you'd otherwise lose.

The typical timeline is 4-6 weeks. Zero disruption to how you work. Results: £8K-15K more collected, 10-20 hours back.

[Relevant case study] had a similar situation. Here's what happened..."

**6. Handle Objections (2-3 minutes)**
Common objections:
- "Sounds too good to be true" → "I understand. Let me show you exactly how it works..."
- "Worried about disruption" → "The key is: nothing changes for your team. The automation just runs..."
- "Think about it" → "Of course. What specifically do you want to think about?"

**7. Next Steps (2 minutes)**
"I'll send you a proposal within 24 hours. Three options at different price points. You can review, ask questions, and decide. No pressure.

Does email or PDF work better for you? What's the best email?"

**Close:**
"Any questions before I let you go?"

**Post-Call (5 minutes):**
- Send proposal immediately
- Add to HubSpot CRM
- Set follow-up reminder (5 days)
- Log call outcome and key points

1. "Tell me about your business and how long you've been running it."
2. "What's the biggest challenge you've faced in the last 12 months?"

4. "Where does most of your time go—admin, billing, marketing?"

### 6.3 Objection Handling & Closing Scripts

**Objection: "Sounds too good to be true"**

Response: "I get that. When I first heard about automation, I was skeptical too. The thing is, it's not magic—we're just systematizing things that should have been systems all along. Invoice reminders, lead follow-ups, admin tasks. These are predictable processes. The question isn't whether they can be automated—it's whether the automation will work with your flow. For [similar client], we did [specific thing]. Here are their results: [numbers]. Want to speak with them directly?"

**Objection: "No budget"**

Response: "I understand. Let me reframe this. You're losing £8K-15K/month to late payments alone. If we recover even half of that, we've paid for ourselves 5-10 times over. This isn't an expense—it's an investment with a clear payback period. Most clients see ROI within 1-2 months. The real question is: can you afford not to fix this?"

**Objection: "Worried about disruption"**

Response: "That's our key differentiator. We don't change how you work. Here's what we don't do: We don't force new software. We don't retrain your team. We don't require you to change your daily routine. We work with your existing tools and flow. The automation runs in the background. Your team doesn't notice anything different—they just see things getting done. [Client example] said: 'My team didn't even notice anything changed.'"

**Objection: "Need to think about it"**

Response: "Of course. This is a decision worth thinking through. What specifically do you want to think about? [Listen and address concern] Is there anything I can clarify? I'll send the proposal tonight. Take the week. If you have questions, reply to this email or book a 15-minute follow-up."

**Objection: "Need to talk to partner"**

Response: "Absolutely. If [partner] will be involved in the decision, it makes sense to loop them in. I'm happy to do a quick 15-minute call with both of you, or send the proposal to share. What would be more useful? [Follow their preference] Just so you know, the pricing is valid for 7 days, but if you need more time, just let me know."

**Objection: "What if it doesn't work?"**

Response: "Fair question. Here's what I can offer: We measure everything. You'll see exactly what's changing—revenue collected, hours saved, leads captured. If after 60 days you're not seeing results, I'll refund 50% of what you paid. That said, I don't think that will happen. We've done this 150+ times. The approach works. The question is whether your situation fits our process."
