# Discovery Call Script & Framework

## Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes)

### Before Every Call
Review prospect's:
- LinkedIn profile (company, role, recent posts)
- Website (if available)
- Any form submission data
- Referral source

Prepare:
- 2-3 personalized questions based on their context
- Relevant case study (match their industry if possible)
- Audit template ready
- Timer set for 30 minutes

### Materials Needed
- Laptop with Calendly open
- Notes app with discovery questions
- Case study PDF ready to share
- Proposal template loaded
- Timer visible on screen

---

## Call Structure (30 minutes)

### 1. Opening (2 minutes)

**Script:**
> "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm Ewan from covered.AI—I've spent 30 years running a dental practice in Newcastle, and now I help UK SMBs get their time back.
>
> The goal of this call is simple: I'll ask some questions about your business, and I'll show you where you might be losing money or time. No hard sell—if we're a fit, I'll send a proposal. If not, I'll tell you honestly.
>
> Sound good?
>
> Quick question before we dive in: How much time do you have today? I want to make sure we don't waste it."

**Purpose:** Set low-pressure tone, establish trust, manage time expectations

**Key Points:**
- Personal connection (30 years, Newcastle)
- No pitch promised
- Time-bound

---

### 2. Build Rapport (3 minutes)

**Script:**
> "Tell me a bit about your business. How did you end up doing what you do? What made you start [company name]?"

**Listen For:**
- Their story and passion
- How long they've been doing it
- Size of team
- Their role day-to-day

**Example Follow-Up:**
> "So you've been running this for [X] years. What's the biggest change you've seen in that time?"

**Purpose:** Understand their journey, build connection before diving into problems

---

### 3. Pain Discovery (10-12 minutes)

**Core Questions (ask in natural flow):**

**Question 1: Time**
> "On a typical week, how many hours are you working? And what are you actually doing in those hours?"

**Listen For:**
- 50+ hours/week
- Doing admin, not business work
- Bottleneck at owner level
- No time for strategy or family

**Probe Deeper:**
> "Out of those hours, how many would you say are actually on the business—growth, strategy, client work—versus admin, invoicing, chasing things?"

---

**Question 2: Money**
> "Let's talk about cash flow. How are you doing with getting paid? Do you have issues with late payments or invoices getting stuck?"

**Listen For:**
- £8K-£15K stuck in late payments
- Awkward about chasing
- No systematic follow-up
- Cash flow stress

**Probe Deeper:**
> "On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your payment collection? What gets in the way of it being a 10?"

---

**Question 3: Growth**
> "Where do most of your new clients come from? And what happens after someone first contacts you?"

**Listen For:**
- Inconsistent lead follow-up
- Leads going cold
- Marketing feels like guesswork
- No systematic nurture

**Probe Deeper:**
> "Of all the people who reach out to you, what percentage would you say actually become paying clients? And where do you think you're losing the rest?"

---

**Question 4: The Big One**
> "If I could wave a magic wand and fix one thing in your business—what would it be?"

**Listen For:**
- Their actual priority
- Emotional language used
- What keeps them up at night

**Purpose:** Identify their #1 pain to focus the rest of the conversation

---

### 4. Situation Audit (8-10 minutes)

**Script:**
> "You've given me a really clear picture. Let me ask a few more specific questions so I can understand exactly where the leaks are."

**Process Walk-Through Questions:**

**Invoicing:**
> "Walk me through what happens after you complete work for a client. How do you send the invoice? When do you follow up? What's your current follow-up sequence?"

**Listen For:**
- Manual sending
- No automated reminders
- Awkward about chasing
- Inconsistent timing

---

**Lead Follow-Up:**
> "When someone first gets in touch—say, through your website or a referral—what's the process? How quickly do you respond? What happens if they don't reply?"

**Listen For:**
- Delayed response
- No systematic follow-up
- Leads going cold manually
- Inconsistent approach

---

**Documentation:**
> "If you had a new team member start tomorrow, how would they know how to do your job? What processes are written down versus just in your head?"

**Listen For:**
- Nothing documented
- Owner bottleneck
- Fear of being indispensable
- Inconsistent training

---

**Quick Calculation (while on call):**
> "Based on what you've told me, it sounds like you're losing about [X hours/week] to admin and [£X/month] to late payments. Does that feel right?"

**Purpose:** Show them the specific numbers during the call—this is the "money moment"

---

### 5. Present Solution (5-7 minutes)

**Script:**
> "Here's what I've found, and here's how I'd help. [Recap their top 2-3 issues in 2-3 sentences].
>
> The way I work is different from most consultants. I don't want to 'transform' your business or change how you work. I want to tighten what's already there.
>
> Here's the process:
>
> **Week 1:** I audit everything you're doing. No changes, no disruption. I just watch and identify where the money and time are leaking.
>
> **Weeks 2-4:** I automate the busywork—invoice reminders, lead follow-ups, data entry. You keep working exactly the same way.
>
> **Weeks 5-8:** The system runs in the background. You don't retrain anyone or change your software.
>
> **Week 9+:** You start getting 10-20 hours back per month, and £8K-£15K more in collected payments.
>
> The key point: I work in the background. Your team doesn't notice much except that things get easier.
>
> Does that approach make sense for where you are?"

**Share Relevant Case Study:**
> "Let me show you what's possible. [Share case study PDF]. This is a [salon/dental/vet] owner in [Newcastle/Durham/Sunderland] who had very similar challenges. Here's what we did and what happened."

**Purpose:** Show low-friction approach works, provide social proof, address disruption concern proactively

---

### 6. Handle Objections (3-5 minutes)

**Common Objections & Responses:**

**"Sounds too good to be true"**
> "I get that. I was skeptical too when I started doing this. The reason it works is simple: we're not changing how you work, we're automating the stuff that shouldn't take your time in the first place. The case study I just shared—[specific results]—that's real work from real clients. I can put you in touch with them if you'd like to verify."

---

**"I don't have time for this"**
> "That's exactly the problem we're solving. The audit takes 30 minutes of your time, and then I do all the work. You keep running your business. The only time you need to commit is our initial call and maybe an hour later to review the results."

---

**"How much does it cost?"**
> "I'll send you a proposal with clear pricing—usually £2,997 to £4,997 depending on complexity. The key question is: would a return of £8K-£15K/month justify that investment? Most clients see ROI within the first month. I'll make sure the numbers work before you commit."

---

**"I need to think about it"**
> "Absolutely. This is a business decision. What specifically would you like to think about? I want to make sure you have everything you need to decide."

---

**Purpose:** Address concerns honestly, build trust through transparency

---

### 7. Next Steps (2-3 minutes)

**If Interested:**
> "Based on our conversation, I think we can help. I'll send you a proposal within 24 hours with three options—Basic, Standard, and Premium—so you can choose what fits your budget and goals.
>
> I'll also include a simple ROI calculation so you can see exactly what return you'd get.
>
> Does email at [email] work best, or should I send it somewhere else?
>
> Any questions before I go?"

---

**If Not a Fit (be honest):**
> "I appreciate your time. I don't think we're the right fit for where you are right now, and I'd rather be honest about that than push something that won't work for you.
>
> If your situation changes or you want to revisit this in 6 months, feel free to reach out. Good luck with [their business goal]."

---

**Always Send:**
- Thank you email within 1 hour
- Proposal (if interested)
- Relevant case study
- Calendly link for follow-up

## Post-Call Template (5 minutes)

### Immediately After Call
1. Log in HubSpot:
   - Call outcome (Interested / Not Interested / Follow-up)
   - Key pain points identified
   - Next steps agreed
   - Follow-up date set

2. Send follow-up email within 1 hour:
   ```
   Subject: Thanks for speaking today, [Name] - Next Steps

   Hi [Name],

   Thanks for taking the time to speak with me today. I really enjoyed learning about [company name] and the challenges you're facing.

   As promised, here's [the proposal / the case study we discussed / our Calendly link to book a follow-up].

   [Insert relevant link]

   If you have any questions after reviewing, just reply to this email. I'm happy to jump on a quick call to walk through anything.

   Talk soon,
   Ewan
   covered.AI
   ```

3. Add to CRM workflow:
   - If interested: Proposal sequence (days 1, 3, 5, 10)
   - If not: Stay in general nurture sequence

## Discovery Questions Quick Reference

| Category | Question | Listen For |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **Time** | "How many hours do you work weekly? What are you actually doing?" | Owner bottleneck, admin overwhelm |
| **Money** | "How's your payment collection? Late payments an issue?" | £8K-£15K stuck, no system |
| **Growth** | "Where do leads come from? What happens after first contact?" | Inconsistent follow-up, lost leads |
| **Magic Wand** | "If I could fix one thing—what would it be?" | #1 priority, emotional language |
| **Process** | "Walk me through your invoicing/follow-up/documentation" | Manual work, no systems |
| **History** | "What have you tried before? What worked/didn't work?" | Previous failures, skepticism |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Talking too much** — Aim for 40% your time, 60% theirs
2. **Skipping rapport** — Personal connection builds trust
3. **Rushing the audit** — Numbers during the call = higher conversion
4. **Not addressing skepticism** — Proactively mention case studies
5. **No clear next step** — Always end with agreement on next action
6. **Forgetting to ask "magic wand"** — This is often their real priority
7. **Not sending follow-up within 1 hour** — Speed matters
