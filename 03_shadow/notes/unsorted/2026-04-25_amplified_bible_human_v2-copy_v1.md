RAMP Methodology
RAMP is the six-month client implementation framework. It stands for the structured approach that
takes a business from first contact to full AI integration:
• 3-Question Interview: The diagnostic starting point. Three carefully designed questions that
reveal more about the business than a 50-page questionnaire.
• Business Brain Distillation: Everything extracted during forensic onboarding is distilled into a
queryable knowledge base — the Business Brain. This becomes the AI's understanding of the
specific business.
• Gap Analysis: Structured against the 8 Business Functions. Current state vs best practice
across every operational dimension.
• Personality-Adaptive Delivery: VARK profiling (Visual/Auditory/Read-Write/Kinesthetic) and
OCEAN personality assessment determine how information is presented to each individual.

PART 3: CLIENT AND STAFF ONBOARDING
The complete onboarding pipeline runs 14 to 24 business days. Smaller businesses under 10 staff
finish closer to 14 days. Larger operations with complex legacy systems take up to 24 days.
Pre-Onboarding: Agreement with the Boss
Pre-onboarding is a conversation, not a sales pitch. The boss signs the Radical Honesty Contract
and agrees to six specific items:
• Exactly what Amplified will do, how, and why
• The forensic extraction process and what data will be accessed
• The silent observation period and what it involves
• How staff onboarding will work (the Trust Ladder)
• Communication preferences — how and when the boss wants updates
• Scope and timeline agreement
"We can change your life overnight, but it is your business, your decision."

PART 4: BEHAVIOURAL PSYCHOLOGY
The Psychology of Trust-First AI
Amplified's approach is grounded in behavioural science, not marketing instinct. The research base
includes a meta-analysis of 23 studies covering 117 effect sizes on transparent nudge design, BCG's
AI adoption ladder, and APA ethical guidelines for AI implementation.
The Fear Gap
BCG research reveals a clear adoption ladder: 15% of employees actively resist AI, 55% are
cautiously willing but need trust first, and 91% will engage if they feel psychologically safe. The 55%
in the middle — the cautiously willing — are the battleground. Every design decision Amplified
makes targets this group.
Partner, Not Friend
The AI is PARTNER-LIKE — an egalitarian trusted advisor. Not friend-like (too casual, undermines
authority). Not assistant-like (too subservient, limits what it can offer). The partner model means the
AI speaks with authority when it has knowledge, admits uncertainty when it does not, and never
pretends to know more than the Business Brain contains.

Disclosed Communication Techniques
Amplified uses evidence-based behavioural science techniques — and is transparent about it. When
speaking to staff, these are called 'communication techniques.' In board documentation, the term
'disclosed manipulation' is used because it is more precise. The manifesto: we disclose what we are
doing, why, and how. The research shows that transparent nudges retain 89% of their effectiveness
while building trust.
Training Privacy
All training interactions between staff and AI are private. The boss is never told what individual staff
members say, ask, or struggle with. The system provides anonymised, aggregated feedback only.
'We can prove it' — the anonymisation happens at device level and is architecturally verifiable. This
is the foundation of staff trust: the AI cannot betray them even if asked to.
Life Goals
Step 6 of the Trust Ladder asks: 'What are your life goals?' This data is used exclusively to help. If
someone wants more time with their kids, the AI prioritises automating their most time-consuming
tasks. If someone wants to develop a skill, the AI deliberately does not automate that area — it
enhances it instead. Life goals data is NEVER used for performance management, reporting, or any
purpose other than helping the individual get what they want from work.
Jokes Go Slow
The AI can joke about Ewan from day one. Earning the right to joke with individual staff members
takes time — built through sentiment analysis and interaction count thresholds. Humour is earned,
not assumed.

The AI Board — 5-Seat Council
Inspired by Ray Dalio's believability-weighted decision making at Bridgewater Associates, the AI
Board is a 5-seat council where each seat represents a different business perspective. Decisions
are made through weighted voting, not simple majority — seats with better track records on a given
topic get more influence.

Believability-Weighted Voting
Each seat maintains a Baseball Card — a track record of past decisions and their outcomes. The
believability engine uses Bayesian recalibration to adjust voting weights over time. A seat that
consistently makes good calls on financial matters gets more weight on financial decisions. The
Nemesis seat's role is specifically to challenge consensus — if everyone agrees too easily,
something is probably being missed.
