# covered.AI Logo Concepts

## Executive Summary

This document presents three distinct logo concepts for covered.AI, each designed to communicate the brand's core promise: low-friction optimization that works quietly in the background. All concepts prioritize accessibility (WCAG AA contrast), scalability across digital and print applications, and instant recognition at small sizes (favicon, mobile).

---

## Brand Foundation Notes

Before reviewing concepts, these foundational elements inform all visual decisions:

**Core Brand Attributes**
- **Positioning**: Background optimizer—no disruption, just results
- **Tone**: Straightforward, human, non-corporate
- **Audience**: UK SMB owners (£500K-£3M revenue, 3-15 staff)
- **Key Differentiator**: Works invisibly, no retraining, no software changes

**Color Psychology**
- Navy Blue (#0A2540): Trust, professionalism, stability
- Bright Teal (#00D9C0): Innovation, energy, fresh approach
- Charcoal Gray (#2D3748): Authority, readability
- Light Gray (#F7FAFC): Clean backgrounds, breathing room

**Typography**
- Headings: Satoshi Bold (modern, confident)
- Body: Inter Regular (readable, clean)
- Data: JetBrains Mono (when needed)

## Concept 1: Background Layer

### Visual Description

A stylized representation of overlapping translucent layers, suggesting systems working in parallel without interference. The design features three horizontal bands in varying opacity, creating depth while maintaining visual lightness. The top layer (brightest) carries the word "covered," the middle layer shows ".AI" in a lighter weight, and the bottom layer (most transparent) provides a subtle foundation. A single accent line in teal cuts through all layers diagonally, symbolizing automation and flow without disrupting the underlying structure.

The icon-only version isolates just the layered element as a compact mark, suitable for favicon and app icon contexts.

### Typography Pairing

- **Primary Wordmark**: Satoshi Bold, tracking at +20 for confidence and modern feel
- **Secondary Wordmark**: Inter Regular at 80% weight for ".AI" to distinguish the technology layer
- **Icon Integration**: Layers can stack horizontally or vertically depending on context

### Color Application

**Full Lockup (Horizontal)**
- "covered" in Navy (#0A2540)
- ".AI" in Charcoal (#2D3748) at 80% opacity
- Accent line in Teal (#00D9C0)
- White or transparent background

**Icon-Only Version**
- Navy base layer at 100%
- Teal accent line
- All elements scale to 512px minimum

**Reversed Version (Dark Backgrounds)**
- "covered" in White
- ".AI" in Light Gray (#E2E8F0)
- Teal accent line

### Use Cases

- **Website Header**: Full horizontal lockup
- **Favicon**: Icon-only version (64px)
- **Social Media Profile**: Icon-only version (circular crop)
- **Email Signature**: Horizontal lockup at 200px width
- **Business Cards**: Icon-left, text-right layout
- **Letterhead**: Full lockup in header area

### Why This Concept Works

1. **Visual Metaphor Alignment**: The layered approach literally shows "background" work happening simultaneously with primary operations—no disruption implied.

2. **Scalability**: Three horizontal bands translate cleanly to favicon (stacked) and full-width applications.

3. **Trust Signals**: The transparency and overlap suggest transparency in operations—everything is visible and accountable.

4. **Technology Hint**: The ".AI" distinction subtly signals automation capability without making it the dominant element.

5. **UK SMB Relatability**: Clean, professional, not flashy or startup-esque—appeals to established business owners.

## Concept 2: Safety Net

### Visual Description

A stylized protective element that suggests coverage and security without feeling corporate or insurance-like. The design features a continuous line forming a protective arc above a solid anchor point, resembling both a safety net and a tightening mechanism. The line creates negative space that forms a subtle checkmark or upward arrow, suggesting positive movement and results.

The anchor point (bottom) represents the business's core operations—solid, unchanged. The protective arc hovers above, suggesting oversight and optimization without physical connection. A teal accent color highlights the "covered" portion of the arc, while the anchor remains in navy.

### Typography Pairing

- **Primary Wordmark**: Satoshi Bold, set in a single line for clarity
- **Subtitle Option**: Inter Regular in Charcoal for tagline or positioning
- **Weight Contrast**: "covered" in bold, ".AI" in medium weight to distinguish

### Color Application

**Primary Application**
- Protective arc in Navy (#0A2540) fading to Teal (#00D9C0) at the rightmost point
- Anchor point in solid Navy
- Text in Navy

**Icon-Only Version**
- Teal arc with white fill
- Navy anchor point
- All white on dark backgrounds

**Reversed Application**
- White arc with teal accent
- Light gray anchor
- White text

### Use Cases

- **Website Header**: Full lockup with protective arc above text
- **LinkedIn Banner**: Icon-centered with "covered.AI" below
- **Business Cards**: Arc icon top-left, text bottom-right
- **Invoice Headers**: Small icon with company details
- **Proposal Covers**: Large arc icon as hero element

### Why This Concept Works

1. **Protection Without Insurance Feel**: The arc suggests "coverage" without corporate insurance imagery—feels like optimization, not risk management.

2. **Stability + Motion**: The anchor represents business stability; the upward-pointing negative space suggests positive movement and improvement.

3. **Low Friction Visual**: No complex elements, no dense patterns—simple line work that communicates at a glance.

4. **Scalable Recognition**: Simple line icon works at 16px (favicon) and scales to billboard size.

5. **Ewan's Dental Background Connection**: The "checkmark" negative space subtly references clinical precision and thoroughness—important for E-E-A-T signaling.

## Concept 3: Tightening Mechanism

### Visual Description

A geometric representation of optimization and efficiency. The design features a hexagonal core (representing the business system) with internal elements that appear to be "tightening" toward the center. Four connecting lines converge inward, each ending in a small circle that represents a process being optimized. The circles are positioned at 12, 3, 6, and 9 o'clock, suggesting four key areas: invoicing, follow-ups, documentation, and cash flow.

The center of the hexagon remains open, symbolizing that the business continues operating normally while optimization happens around it. A subtle teal gradient moves from the outer circles toward the center, suggesting energy and focus.

### Typography Pairing

- **Primary Wordmark**: Satoshi Bold, letters slightly condensed (-5% width) for efficiency feel
- **Secondary Elements**: Inter Regular for ".AI" and supporting text
- **Number Integration**: Can integrate "4" or "4x" subtly if needed for messaging

### Color Application

**Full Color Version**
- Hexagon outline in Navy (#0A2540) at 70% opacity
- Four connecting lines in Charcoal (#2D3748)
- Four optimization circles in Teal (#00D9C0)
- Text in Navy

**Monochrome Version**
- Single color (Navy) for all elements
- Line weights adjusted for clarity at small sizes

**Reversed Version**
- White hexagon outline
- Light gray connecting lines
- Teal circles for accent
- White text

### Use Cases

- **Website Hero Section**: Large icon as visual anchor
- **Slide Decks**: Consistent icon for "optimization" slides
- **Process Diagrams**: Expandable to show 4-step process visually
- **Case Study Graphics**: Icon as section header
- **Social Media Graphics**: Icon with overlay text

### Why This Concept Works

1. **Process Optimization Visual**: The "tightening" metaphor directly communicates the brand promise—tightening processes, not transforming them.

2. **Four Pillars Reference**: The four circles visually represent the four key services (invoicing, follow-ups, documentation, automation) without text.

3. **Expandable Visual**: Can grow from simple icon to full process diagram depending on context.

4. **Technical Credibility**: Geometric precision suggests methodical, proven approach—important for E-E-A-T with Ewan's 30 years experience.

5. **Differentiation**: Unlike generic "growth" or "rocket" startup imagery, this feels grounded and process-focused.

## Brand Lock-Up Rules

### Minimum Clear Space

All logos require minimum clear space equal to the height of the "c" in "covered" (or equivalent visual unit) on all sides.

**Clear Space Diagram**
```
[   x   ][ LOGO ][   x   ]
         x = 1x height of lowercase letter
```

### Minimum Sizes

- **Full lockup (web)**: 120px width minimum
- **Full lockup (print)**: 25mm width minimum
- **Icon-only (web)**: 32px minimum
- **Icon-only (print)**: 8mm minimum

### Never-Do List

1. **NEVER** stretch or distort the logo
2. **NEVER** rotate the logo
3. **NEVER** add drop shadows or effects
4. **NEVER** use gradient on the logo (except specified versions)
5. **NEVER** place logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
6. **NEVER** change the colors except for approved versions
7. **NEVER** separate the icon from the wordmark except for approved layouts
8. **NEVER** add outlines or strokes not in original files
9. **NEVER** use font substitutions for the wordmark
10. **NEVER** rotate or reposition elements within the logo

### Approved Backgrounds

**Good Backgrounds**
- White (#FFFFFF)
- Light Gray (#F7FAFC)
- Navy (#0A2540) - for reversed version
- Photographs with sufficient contrast (test with overlay)

**Avoid**
- Low-contrast grays
- Patterned backgrounds
- Photographs without testing
- Bright teal backgrounds (competes with accent)

### Horizontal Lockup Variations

**Standard (Preferred)**
```
[Icon] [covered.AI]
```

**Compact (Emails, Narrow Spaces)**
```
[Icon] covered.AI
```

**Stacked (Square Formats)**
```
[  covered  ]
[   .AI     ]
```

## Recommended Selection

### Recommended Concept: Background Layer

**Rationale**

For a service positioning itself as "background optimization with zero disruption," the Background Layer concept most directly communicates this promise through visual metaphor. The overlapping, translucent layers show work happening simultaneously without interference—exactly what the brand promises.

**Why Not Others**

- **Safety Net**: While excellent for protection messaging, it slightly偏离 from the core "optimization" positioning and risks insurance-adjacent associations.
- **Tightening Mechanism**: Strong technical appeal but may feel too engineered for a human, relationship-based service. Better as secondary visual for process content.

**Hybrid Approach (Optional)**

Consider using Background Layer as primary mark, with Safety Net-inspired elements in specific contexts (e.g., guarantee pages, trust-building content).
