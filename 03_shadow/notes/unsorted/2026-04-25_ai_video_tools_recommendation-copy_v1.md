## The Hollywood Prompt Formula

Digital agencies use this structure for cinematic AI video prompts:

```
[SUBJECT/ACTION] in [ENVIRONMENT/SETTING], 
[CAMERA ANGLE/MOVEMENT], 
[LIGHTING/MOOD], 
[STYLE/GENRE], 
[ADDITIONAL DETAILS]
```

### Camera Angles & Movements to Use

| Term | Effect | When to Use |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Slow dolly-in** | Intimate, building tension | Revealing a problem |
| **Tracking shot** | Following action, momentum | Customer journey |
| **Crane shot** | Epic scale, overview | Business transformation |
| **Handheld subtle shake** | Documentary, authentic | Testimonials, real moments |
| **Macro close-up** | Detail focus, importance | Key statistics |
| **360° orbit** | Full perspective, wow factor | Product reveal |
| **Static wide shot** | Establishing scene | Opening, context |
| **Over-the-shoulder** | Perspective, immersion | Using the product |

### Lighting Terms to Use

| Term | Mood | When to Use |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Soft diffused lighting** | Professional, calm | Presenter segments |
| **Golden hour glow** | Warm, optimistic | Success stories |
| **High-contrast studio lighting** | Dramatic, serious | Problem statements |
| **Neon cyberpunk lighting** | Modern, tech-focused | Product features |
| **Natural window light** | Authentic, trustworthy | Testimonials |
| **Backlit silhouette** | Mystery, anticipation | Before transformation |

### Style/Genre Terms

- **Cinematic** — Film-like quality
- **Documentary** — Real, authentic
- **Editorial** — Magazine-quality
- **Corporate professional** — Clean, business
- **Minimalist** — Simple, focused
- **Hyper-detailed** — Sharp, crisp
- **UGC-style** — User-generated, authentic

## The Complete Production Workflow

### Step 1: Script & Storyboard (30 min)
1. Write 90-second script (use psychology brief template)
2. Break into scenes (5-7 scenes)
3. Sketch rough storyboard (even stick figures work)
4. Note which scenes need presenter vs. B-roll

### Step 2: Generate B-Roll in Runway (60 min)
1. Write detailed prompts for each B-roll scene
2. Generate 3-5 variations per scene
3. Pick the best ones
4. Download in 1080p or 4K

**Pro tip:** Generate at 2-3x the length you need. You can trim in editing.

### Step 3: Create Presenter in HeyGen (30 min)
1. Upload script
2. Select or create avatar
3. Choose voice (clone yours or select)
4. Adjust pacing (pauses between sentences)
5. Generate video

### Step 4: Edit in Descript (60 min)
1. Import all assets (HeyGen video + Runway clips)
2. Arrange on timeline
3. Cut and trim to match script
4. Add transitions (simple cuts work best)
5. Add captions (auto-generated)
6. Add background music (subtle, royalty-free)
7. Export final video

### Step 5: Review & Iterate (30 min)
1. Watch full video
2. Check pacing (too fast? too slow?)
3. Verify all stats are correct
4. Show to one trusted person for feedback
5. Make adjustments

## Pro Tips from Digital Agencies

### 1. The "3-Second Rule"
Every scene must communicate its message in 3 seconds. If it takes longer to understand, cut it.

### 2. The "One Idea Per Scene" Rule
Never show multiple concepts in one shot. One scene = one idea maximum.

### 3. Prompt Iteration
Generate 3-5 variations of each prompt. Pick the best. AI is probabilistic — not every generation is perfect.

### 4. Consistent Style
Use the same lighting style, color palette, and camera movement language throughout. This creates cohesion.

### 5. Sound Design Matters
Good sound makes video feel 50% more professional. Don't skip music and subtle sound effects.

### 6. Test with Real People
Before finalizing, show to 2-3 people who match your target audience. Watch their reactions. Ask: "What did you understand? What confused you?"
