# covered.AI Objection Handling & Closing Scripts

## Document Overview

**Purpose:** Provide proven scripts for handling common objections and closing sales conversations

**When to Use:** During discovery calls, proposal follow-ups, and any sales conversation

**Philosophy:** Address concerns honestly, provide evidence, and guide toward decision

---

## Objection Framework

### How to Handle Objections (General Approach)

**Listen Fully**
- Let them finish their thought completely
- Don't interrupt, even if you know the response
- Take a brief pause before responding

**Acknowledge & Validate**
- Show you understand their concern
- "I completely understand why that's a worry."
- "That's a fair question. Let me address that."

**Reframe with Evidence**
- Provide specific evidence or case study
- Use numbers, not vague promises
- Connect to their situation

**Close or Continue**
- "Does that address your concern?"
- "Would you feel comfortable moving forward?"

## Top 6 Objections & Responses

### Objection 1: "Sounds Too Good to Be True"

**Why They Say This:**
- They've been burned before
- Skeptical of marketing claims
- Worried about hidden costs or failures

**Response Script:**
```
"I get that. Honestly, I was skeptical too when I first heard about what automation could do. Here's the thing—I spent 30 years running my own practice. If someone had told me I'd get 20 hours back and collect £12K more per month without changing anything, I'd have laughed them out of the room.

But that's exactly what happened when I implemented this in my own business. And I've since replicated it with 150+ other UK SMBs.

Here's what makes it work: we're not promising miracles. We're automating the stuff that shouldn't take your time—invoice reminders, lead follow-ups, basic data entry. It's not complicated. It just works.

Want me to walk you through exactly how it works in practice? Or would it help to talk to one of my clients directly?"
```

**Evidence to Provide:**
- Specific case study from similar business
- Timeline: "Week 1 audit, weeks 2-4 build, week 5 live"
- Guarantee: "30-day guarantee. If it doesn't work, you don't pay."

**If They Push Further:**
```
"The reason it works is simple: every business has the same leaks—unpaid invoices, leads that go cold, admin that piles up. We've built systems that plug those leaks. It's not magic. It's process.

Would you be open to a 30-minute audit where I show you your specific leaks? If I can't identify £5K+ in potential improvements, I'll tell you honestly and we part ways as friends."
```

### Objection 2: "No Budget / Too Expensive"

**Why They Say This:**
- Genuinely cash-constrained
- Testing your commitment to price
- Want to see if they'll get a discount
- Don't see the ROI clearly

**Response Script:**
```
"I appreciate you being direct about budget. Let me ask—is it that the investment feels too high, or that you're not seeing how it pays for itself?

Because here's what I've found with clients in similar situations: the average late payment is £8-15K per month. That's money sitting in other people's bank accounts. Our fee is £3-4K. Most clients recoup that investment within 4-6 weeks just from improved cash flow.

Let me put it another way. What's the cost of another year of status quo? £8K/month × 12 months = £96K in late payments. £15K+/year in your time. That's a lot more than £4K.

If budget is genuinely an issue, we do have a Basic package at £2,997 that focuses on one process area. Or we could start with invoice automation—which typically pays for itself in 2-3 weeks. Would either of those work better for your situation?"
```

**ROI Calculation Approach:**
```
"Here's the math for your situation:
• Hours you're spending: [X] hours/week on admin
• Your hourly rate: £[Y] (or market rate for your role)
• That's £[Z]K/month in time cost
• Plus late payments: £[A]K/month
• Total leak: £[B]K/month

Our fee: £[C]K
Payback period: [C ÷ B] weeks
First year return: £[D]K

You're not spending £4K. You're investing £4K to get £[D]K back."
```

**If Still Hesitant:**
```
"I understand. What if we structured it differently—50% upfront, 50% after you see results in week 4? That way you're not risking anything.

Or we could do a pilot: focus on just invoice automation for £1,997. If you see £3-4K improvement in the first month, we know the full approach will work. Fair?"
```

### Objection 3: "Worried About Disruption"

**Why They Say This:**
- Had bad experiences with consultants
- Can't afford downtime
- Team is already stretched thin
- Fear of change

**Response Script:**
```
"That's the #1 concern I hear, and honestly—it's exactly why I built this service the way I did.

I spent 30 years running a practice. I know what happens when someone comes in and says 'you need to change everything.' It never works. People resist. Things fall apart.

Here's what we do differently:

1. We observe FIRST. We watch what you're already doing. No changes required.
2. We build in the BACKGROUND. While you're running your business, we're setting things up.
3. We go LIVE without retraining. Your team keeps working exactly the same way. They don't even notice the change—they just notice that reminders go out on time, leads get followed up, invoices get paid.
4. We're GONE after setup. We don't become a permanent fixture. We set it up, it works, we move on.

I've had clients where the team didn't even know we'd been involved until I checked in 3 months later asking if everything was still working. That's the goal.

Does that address your concern?"
```

**Case Study for Disruption:**
```
"Take Mark's dental practice in Durham. Biggest worry: 'I can't have my team learning new systems right now.' Here's what we did: Week 1 audit, weeks 2-4 build, week 5 go-live. His team noticed exactly nothing except that payments came in faster. Quote from Mark: 'I was skeptical. But they set it up and left. Now it just runs.' Happy to connect you two if you'd like to ask him directly."
```

### Objection 4: "Need to Think About It / Talk to Partner"

**Why They Say This:**
- Genuinely needs time
- Waiting for partner's input
- Overwhelmed by decision
- Polite way to end call

**Response Script:**
```
"Absolutely. This is a decision that deserves thought. Let me make sure you have everything you need.

What specifically do you want to think over? Is it:
- The investment?
- How it would work in your situation?
- Whether now is the right time?
- Something else?

[Wait for answer]

That's helpful. Let me address that directly.

[Address specific concern]

Also, I want to be transparent: I only take on a few clients each month to ensure quality. If we do move forward, I'd want to start within the next [X] weeks to catch the next billing cycle. But there's no pressure from me—take the time you need.

When would be a good time to check back in? [Suggest specific date]"
```

**If They Need to Talk to Partner:**
```
"That makes total sense. Would it help if I joined a quick call with you and your partner? I can walk through the numbers and answer their questions directly. Sometimes it's faster than trying to explain it all.

Or if they prefer, I can send a summary they can review. What's their biggest concern typically—time commitment, cost, or results?"
```

### Objection 5: "What If It Doesn't Work?"

**Why They Say This:**
- Risk-averse
- Previous bad experiences
- Need assurance
- Testing your confidence

**Response Script:**
```
"I love that question. Because honestly—if it doesn't work, you shouldn't pay.

Here's our guarantee: 30 days after go-live, if you're not seeing measurable improvement in either time saved or money collected, you don't pay the second 50%. No questions, no arguing.

We've never had someone invoke this guarantee. But it's there because I understand the risk you're taking.

More importantly, here's why it works: we're not guessing. Our process is:
1. We audit first—so we know exactly what we're fixing
2. We build proven systems—not experiments
3. We test before going live—so you only see it when it works
4. We're available for 60 days after—so any issues get fixed fast

I have 30 years of business experience and 150+ clients who can tell you it works. But the guarantee protects you either way.

Does that help?"
```

**Evidence to Provide:**
- Testimonials from similar businesses
- Specific timeline of when results appear
- Case study with measurements

### Objection 6: "I'm Happy With the Status Quo"

**Why They Say This:**
- Actually content (rare)
- Polite rejection
- Not feeling the pain enough yet
- Don't see the opportunity

**Response Script:**
```
"Honestly, that's totally fair. If things are working for you and you're happy, I'm not here to convince you otherwise.

Let me ask though—when you say 'happy,' what does that look like? 
- Are you working the hours you want?
- Is cash flow consistent?
- Can you take time away without the business suffering?

[Wait for answer]

Here's the thing I tell everyone: the difference between businesses that grow and those that stagnate isn't usually talent or product. It's whether the owner can step back from the day-to-day.

If you're happy running at full capacity forever, that's a valid choice. But if there's part of you that wants more time or more growth—and just doesn't see how—that's where we can help.

No pressure. But if you ever want a free audit just to see what's possible, I'm here. Some of my best clients came in 'happy' and left surprised at what was possible.

Would a no-commitment audit make sense? It takes 30 minutes and you'll at least know what leaks you have—even if you don't plug them."
```

## Closing Techniques

### Technique 1: Assumptive Close

**When to Use:** When they've shown clear interest and all objections are addressed

**Script:**
```
"Great. It sounds like this makes sense for your situation. Let's get you on the calendar for next week. I have [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time]—which works better for the kickoff call?"
```

**If They Hesitate:**
```
"We can also push to the following week if you need, but I'd recommend starting sooner. The longer we wait, the longer you wait for results. Let's get you those 15 hours back."
```

---

### Technique 2: Alternative Close

**When to Use:** When they seem ready but need a nudge between options

**Script:**
"Would you prefer the Standard package at £3,997, or shall we start with the Basic package at £2,997 to test the approach? Both work, but the Standard gives you the full result faster."

---

### Technique 3: Takeaway Close

**When to Use:** When they seem uncertain but interested

**Script:**
```
"I appreciate that you're thinking it through. Let me be honest with you—I only take on [X] clients per month to ensure quality. We're actually at [Y/2] for next month.

If you want to move forward, I'd recommend booking this week. If you need more time, I completely understand—but I want to be transparent that I may not have availability if you come back in a few weeks.

No pressure. What's your instinct?"
```

---

### Technique 4: Question Close

**When to Use:** When there's still a hidden concern

**Script:**
```
"I can tell you're interested, but something's still holding you back. Can you tell me what it is? If it's something I can address, I will. If it's not the right fit, I'd rather you make that decision with all the information.

What needs to be true for you to move forward?"
```

---

### Technique 5: Payment Plan Close

**When to Use:** When budget is the concern

**Script:**
```
"I hear you on the investment. What if we structured it differently?
- 50% upfront (£1,998.50)
- 50% after week 4 when you see results (£1,998.50)

That way you're not risking anything. If it doesn't work, you don't pay the second half. And if it does work, you've paid the same total amount—just spread out.

Fair?"
```
