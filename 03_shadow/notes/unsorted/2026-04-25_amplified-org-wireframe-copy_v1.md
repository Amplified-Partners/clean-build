# Organisational Wireframe: What the Best Companies Actually Build

A structural skeleton derived from 30 companies — 20 AI-powered SMB platforms and 10 traditional business advisory firms — showing the universal departments, their sub-functions, typical headcount allocations, and how lean operators collapse them.

This is a starting point, not a prescription. The purpose is to give Amplified Partners a map of "what goes where" so that when agents and individuals are assigned, nothing falls through the cracks.

---

## The Seven Core Divisions

Every company in the dataset, from a 3-person startup doing $1M ARR to a 4,500-person public company doing $839M, covers the same seven functional areas. The difference is headcount and how they're organised — not whether they exist.

| Division | What It Covers | Typical % of Headcount | Source Companies |
|----------|---------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Product & Engineering | Software, R&D, AI/ML, QA, infrastructure | 28–50% | [Freshworks](https://www.freshworks.com) 45-50%, [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 31%, [Keap](https://keap.com) 28%, [Scorpion](https://www.scorpion.co) 17% |
| Revenue & Sales | BizDev, account management, sales ops, partnerships | 13–30% | [Freshworks](https://www.freshworks.com) 30%, [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 27%, [Scorpion](https://www.scorpion.co) 19%, [Keap](https://keap.com) 13% |
| Marketing | Brand, content, digital, enterprise marketing | 5–12% | [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 12%, [Freshworks](https://www.freshworks.com) 10%, [Keap](https://keap.com) 5% |
| Customer Success & Support | Onboarding, support, experience, customer ops | 10–20% | [Podium](https://www.podium.com) largest unit, [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) separate division, [Keap](https://keap.com) "significant" |
| Operations & IT | Platform ops, billing, infrastructure, IT | 5–11% | [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 8% + 11% IT, [Scorpion](https://www.scorpion.co) 6% + 3% IT |
| Finance & Legal | Accounting, FP&A, legal, compliance | 3–5% | [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 4%, [Scorpion](https://www.scorpion.co) 4%, [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) merged division |
| People & Culture | HR, talent acquisition, workplace, impact | 2–4% | [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 4%, [Scorpion](https://www.scorpion.co) 2%, [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) "People & Impact" |

## Division Breakdown: Sub-Functions

### 1. Product & Engineering

The engine room. Every SaaS company allocates the largest share here. The best companies split it into clear sub-teams rather than a flat engineering org.

| Sub-Function | What It Does | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Back-end Engineering | APIs, services, data pipelines, core logic | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) has 11 distinct tech teams; [GoHighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com) handles 4B+ API calls/day |
| Front-end Engineering | UI, UX implementation, client-side applications | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) separates front-end as distinct team |
| Platform / Infrastructure | Cloud, DevOps, CI/CD, reliability | [nexos.ai](https://nexos.ai) R&D 7 roles; [ServiceTitan](https://www.servicetitan.com) engineering across US/Armenia/Canada |
| AI / ML / Data Science | Models, training, data pipelines, AI product features | [GoHighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com) growing AI/data science; [Collective[i]](https://collectivei.com) proprietary EFM model; [Podium](https://www.podium.com) Jerry AI, Voice AI |
| QA / Testing | Quality assurance, automated testing, release validation | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) separate QA team |
| Product Management | Roadmap, prioritisation, specs, user research | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Product division includes Management + Tribes; [Tidio](https://www.tidio.com) Product/Design team |
| Product Design | UX research, UI design, prototyping | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Design within Product division |

### 2. Revenue & Sales

Second largest function. The best companies don't just have "sales" — they split it into specialised sub-functions.

| Sub-Function | What It Does | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Business Development | New market entry, strategic partnerships, outbound | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) BizDev within Revenue division; [Scorpion](https://www.scorpion.co) 251 sales staff |
| Account Management | Existing client relationships, upsells, renewals | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) separates Account Mgmt from BizDev |
| Sales Operations | CRM, pipeline, analytics, enablement, tools | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Sales Ops team; [Thryv](https://www.thryv.com) Sales Ops division |
| Customer Success | Onboarding, adoption, retention, health scoring | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) CS within Revenue; some companies put it under Customer division |
| Professional Services | Implementation, consulting, custom work | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Professional Services within Revenue division |
| Partnerships / Channel | Affiliate programmes, integrations, ecosystem | [GoHighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com) 7.7k new affiliates 2025; [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) inTandem partner programme |

### 3. Marketing

Ranges from 5% to 46% of headcount depending on whether the company's core offering IS marketing (like Scorpion) or marketing supports another product.

| Sub-Function | What It Does | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Content & Thought Leadership | Blogs, reports, books, podcasts, speaking | [EOS Worldwide](https://www.eosworldwide.com) Traction Library; [ActionCOACH](https://www.actioncoach.com) 17 books; [Sandler](https://sandler.com) thought leadership |
| Digital / Performance Marketing | SEO, paid ads, analytics, conversion | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Digital Marketing team; [Scorpion](https://www.scorpion.co) RevenueMAX |
| Brand & Communications | PR, brand voice, awards submissions, media | [Tidio](https://www.tidio.com) content/SEO studies; [Podium](https://www.podium.com) CEO thought leadership |
| Community & Events | User groups, conferences, meetups | [GoHighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com) 115k LinkedIn community; [EOS Worldwide](https://www.eosworldwide.com) annual conference |

### 4. Customer Success & Support

Some companies fold this into Revenue (Brevo model). Others make it standalone. The best companies separate reactive support from proactive success.

| Sub-Function | What It Does | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Customer Experience | Proactive engagement, NPS, journey mapping | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Customer Experience team; [Thryv](https://www.thryv.com) Client Experience |
| Customer Operations | Ticketing, SLAs, escalation, tooling | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Customer Operations team |
| Technical Support | Break-fix, troubleshooting, L1/L2/L3 | [Podium](https://www.podium.com) Sales/Support as largest unit |
| Onboarding & Training | New client activation, education, adoption | [BetterUp](https://www.betterup.com) 4M+ coaching sessions; [The AI Consulting Lab](https://theaiconsultinglab.com) Education as step 2 |

### 5. Operations & IT

The connective tissue. Keeps everything running.

| Sub-Function | What It Does | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Platform Operations | Uptime, monitoring, incident response | [GoHighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com) 470TB data, 4B API hits/day |
| IT & Security | Internal tools, access, compliance, infosec | [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 21 IT staff (11%); [nexos.ai](https://nexos.ai) SOC 2 + ISO 27001 |
| Billing & Payments | Invoicing, payment processing, collections | [Thryv](https://www.thryv.com) Client Care/Billing; [Podium](https://www.podium.com) payments 2.7-3.5% |
| Process & Quality | Internal process improvement, documentation | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) B Corp + ISO 27001 certified |

### 6. Finance & Legal

Small but critical. Every company at scale separates these.

| Sub-Function | What It Does | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Finance / FP&A | Budgeting, forecasting, reporting, treasury | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Finance team; [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 7 finance staff |
| Accounting | Bookkeeping, reconciliation, audit | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Accounting team; [Bench Accounting](https://bench.co) bookkeepers as majority of staff |
| Legal & Compliance | Contracts, IP, regulatory, privacy | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Legal team; [Keap](https://keap.com) General Counsel role |

### 7. People & Culture

Smallest division by headcount, but present everywhere once a company passes ~50 employees.

| Sub-Function | What It Does | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| HR / People Operations | Policies, benefits, employee relations | [vcita](https://www.vcita.com) 8 HR staff; [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) People team |
| Talent Acquisition | Recruiting, employer brand, hiring pipeline | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) separate Talent Acquisition team |
| Culture & Workplace | Office management, remote work, values | [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) Workplace team; [ActionCOACH](https://www.actioncoach.com) 14 Points of Culture |

## Two Models That Matter for Amplified

### Model A: The Hub-and-Spoke (Traditional Advisory)

This is how EOS, Vistage, ActionCOACH, Sandler, and TAB all work. Small headquarters, large delivery network.

```
                    ┌─────────────┐
                    │   HQ CORE   │
                    │  (10-100)   │
                    └──────┬──────┘
                           │
          ┌────────────────┼────────────────┐
          │                │                │
    ┌─────┴─────┐    ┌────┴─────┐    ┌─────┴─────┐
    │ Methodology│    │  Brand   │    │ Platform  │
    │ & Product  │    │ & Mktg   │    │ & Tools   │
    └───────────┘    └──────────┘    └───────────┘
                           │
              ┌────────────┼────────────┐
              │            │            │
        ┌─────┴────┐ ┌────┴────┐ ┌────┴─────┐
        │Franchise │ │Franchise│ │Franchise │
        │Partner A │ │Partner B│ │Partner C │
        │(Delivery)│ │(Delivery│ │(Delivery)│
        └──────────┘ └─────────┘ └──────────┘
```

HQ owns: Methodology, certification, brand, platform/tools, marketing  
Network delivers: Client work, local sales, coaching, implementation

Evidence:
- [EOS Worldwide](https://www.eosworldwide.com): ~100 HQ staff, 850+ EOS Implementers
- [Vistage](https://www.vistage.com): 500-1000 HQ, 1,300 Chairs
- [ActionCOACH](https://www.actioncoach.com): ~20 HQ, 1,100 franchise partners
- [Sandler](https://sandler.com): ~87 HQ, 400+ trainers across 230 franchises
- [TAB](https://www.thealternativeboard.com): 11-50 HQ, 340 franchise offices

### Model B: The AI-Augmented Lean (Swan AI / Mega)

This is where Amplified sits. Tiny human team, AI handles execution at scale.

```
                    ┌─────────────┐
                    │  FOUNDERS   │
                    │   (1-5)     │
                    └──────┬──────┘
                           │
          ┌────────────────┼────────────────┐
          │                │                │
    ┌─────┴─────┐    ┌────┴─────┐    ┌─────┴─────┐
    │   GTM &   │    │  Eng &   │    │ Product & │
    │  Content  │    │  Infra   │    │   CX      │
    └─────┬─────┘    └────┬─────┘    └─────┬─────┘
          │               │                │
    ┌─────┴─────┐   ┌────┴─────┐    ┌─────┴─────┐
    │ AI Agents │   │ AI Agents│    │ AI Agents │
    │ Marketing │   │ Platform │    │ Delivery  │
    │ Content   │   │ Build    │    │ Support   │
    │ Outreach  │   │ Deploy   │    │ Onboard   │
    └───────────┘   └──────────┘    └───────────┘
```

Humans own: Strategy, judgment, accountability, relationships  
AI handles: Execution, maintenance, orchestration, scaling

Evidence:
- [Swan AI](https://www.getswan.com): 3 founders, $1M ARR in 9 weeks, explicitly "no employees beyond founders"
- [Mega](https://www.gomega.ai): 34 people, $10M ARR in 10 months, 55% fully automated

## The Universal Wireframe

Combining both models, here is the complete functional map. Every function listed below exists in the best companies. The question for Amplified is: which slot gets a human, which gets an agent, and which gets both.

```
AMPLIFIED PARTNERS — ORGANISATIONAL WIREFRAME
══════════════════════════════════════════════

LAYER 1: GOVERNANCE & STRATEGY
├── CEO / Visionary — Direction, vision, external relationships
├── Integrator / COO — Execution, accountability, internal operations
└── Advisory Board — External perspective, domain expertise

LAYER 2: VALUE CREATION (Client-Facing)
├── METHODOLOGY & PRODUCT
│   ├── Framework Development — Rubrics, diagnostic tools, playbooks
│   ├── Platform / Software — Client-facing tools, dashboards, portals
│   ├── AI Agent Development — Building and training delivery agents
│   └── Knowledge Management — Vault, research, IP curation
│
├── CLIENT DELIVERY
│   ├── Diagnostic / Intake — Health checks, audits, assessments
│   ├── Implementation — Executing the methodology with clients
│   ├── Coaching / Advisory — Ongoing 1:1 and group sessions
│   └── Monitoring / Optimisation — Tracking outcomes, adjusting
│
└── CUSTOMER SUCCESS
    ├── Onboarding — New client activation, setup, education
    ├── Support — Reactive help, troubleshooting
    ├── Retention — Health scoring, renewal, expansion
    └── Community — Peer networks, events, shared learning

LAYER 3: GROWTH ENGINE
├── MARKETING
│   ├── Content & Thought Leadership — Blog, podcast, reports, speaking
│   ├── Digital / Performance — SEO, ads, conversion, analytics
│   ├── Brand & PR — Voice, positioning, media, awards
│   └── Community & Events — Conferences, meetups, partnerships
│
└── REVENUE / SALES
    ├── Business Development — Outbound, strategic partnerships
    ├── Sales Operations — Pipeline, CRM, analytics, enablement
    ├── Account Management — Existing client growth, upsell
    └── Channel / Partnerships — Affiliates, referrals, ecosystem

LAYER 4: PLATFORM & OPERATIONS
├── ENGINEERING
│   ├── Core Platform — Backend, APIs, services
│   ├── Frontend / UX — Client interfaces, dashboards
│   ├── AI / ML — Model training, agent orchestration, data science
│   ├── Infrastructure / DevOps — Cloud, CI/CD, monitoring
│   └── QA / Testing — Quality assurance, release validation
│
└── OPERATIONS
    ├── IT & Security — Tools, access, compliance, infosec
    ├── Billing & Payments — Invoicing, collections, processing
    └── Process & Quality — Internal workflows, documentation

LAYER 5: FOUNDATION
├── FINANCE & LEGAL
│   ├── Finance / FP&A — Budgeting, forecasting, reporting
│   ├── Accounting — Books, reconciliation, tax
│   └── Legal & Compliance — Contracts, IP, regulatory, GDPR
│
└── PEOPLE & CULTURE
    ├── Talent — Recruiting, employer brand (humans + agent capability)
    ├── Culture — Values, ways of working, Amplified Laws
    └── Learning & Development — Upskilling, certification
```

## What This Means for Amplified

The wireframe above has 33 distinct sub-functions across 5 layers. A traditional company would need 50-200+ people to cover them all. Amplified's thesis is that AI agents can cover many of these — particularly in Layer 3 (Growth Engine) and Layer 4 (Platform & Operations).

The next step: map the agents and individuals you have against these 33 slots. That will show:

1. **What's covered** — a slot has an agent or person assigned
2. **What's doubled up** — multiple agents/people doing the same thing (efficiency opportunity)
3. **What's empty** — no one owns it (risk or deliberate choice)
4. **What needs a human** — judgment-heavy slots that can't be fully automated

Waiting for the agent/individual capability data to do that mapping.
