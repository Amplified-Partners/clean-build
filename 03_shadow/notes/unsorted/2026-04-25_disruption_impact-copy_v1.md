Case Study 2
HubSpot (2006–2025)
"Nobody was doing inbound marketing for SMEs. Big vendors ignored small businesses."
The Early Challenge: Selling to SMBs
• 70% Net Revenue Retention (NRR) — price-sensitive SMBs churned at high rates1
• Average contract $3K–$18K/year — far below enterprise SaaS norms1
• IPO 2014 with 88.6% NRR — below 100% threshold Wall Street considered viable1
• Conventional wisdom: you cannot build premium SaaS selling to small businesses.
The Breakthrough: Proving the Model
• Pivoted to usage-based pricing — NRR jumped to ~100% immediately1
• Land-and-expand — start small, grow with the customer
• 2021: Peak 115% NRR — customers expanding faster than churning1
• 2025: $3.13B revenue, 19% growth — SMB markets sustain premium SaaS at scale1
The Amplified Parallel
The AI industry is ignoring SMEs. Enterprise gets Perceptyx, Viva, Workday. SMEs get
nothing. SMEs are the £78B opportunity.
• UK SME AI gap: £78B potential over next decade, only 35–39% of SMEs actively using AI2
• 33% of UK SMEs have no plans to adopt AI2 — nobody is serving them properly
• HubSpot proved SMBs pay for software that solves their problem. Amplified targets the same
segment.
• Land-and-expand applies: start with one team, prove trust, grow across the organisation.

What "No Fucker's Doing It" Actually Means
When a founder says nobody is doing what they're doing, investors hear it a hundred times a week.
Usually it's delusion. Sometimes it's reality.
In Amplified's case, it's reality. There is no trust-first AI partnership service for SMEs. Enterprise has
Perceptyx, Viva, Workday. SMEs have nothing.
What First-Mover Category Creation Delivers
• First-mover advantage in a category worth billions. UK AI market reaches $180.8B by 2033. Even a
fraction of the trust-first segment is transformative.
• Defining the standards before competitors arrive. Salesforce wrote the rules for cloud CRM.
Amplified writes the rules for trust-first AI adoption.
• The reference customer base is yours to build. Every early customer becomes a case study, a
proof point that makes the next sale easier.
• Brand ownership. When enterprise players eventually try this — and they will — Amplified is already
the name people associate with trust-first AI.
• Network effects compound. Staff who experience trust-first AI carry that expectation to every
future employer. Each deployment creates demand elsewhere.
Every great company started when nobody believed it was possible.
Salesforce started in a one-bedroom apartment when nobody believed in cloud software.
HubSpot started with a $500 budget when nobody believed SMBs would pay for marketing
tools. Slack started with 8 employees when nobody believed email could be displaced.
The gap between 'nobody's doing it' and 'everybody's doing it' is where fortunes are
made.
Amplified Partners is building in that gap.
