## THE CORE NARRATIVE

Every workplace recording system in history has been designed to catch people doing something wrong. Customer complaint lines. CCTV review after incidents. Call monitoring for "training purposes." The recording is the stick. The surveillance is the threat.

Amplified flips this entirely.

**The Positive Capture Loop is a celebration engine.** When the system detects a genuinely delighted customer — not just polite, genuinely over the moon — it captures that moment. Not to file it away in some HR database. To celebrate the staff member who created it.

In Ewan's words: *"The microphone isn't there to catch you doing something wrong. It's there to catch you doing something right."*

That single sentence reframes the entire relationship between staff and technology.

## CONTENT ANGLE 1: "The Microphone That Celebrates You"

**The hook:** What if the recording device at work wasn't there to catch mistakes — but to catch brilliance?

**The story:**
- Every recording system in history: surveillance, complaint capture, compliance monitoring
- The orange microphone does something different — it listens for joy
- When a customer is genuinely delighted, the system notices
- Staff member hears: "You did something brilliant there. Listen to how happy that customer was."
- Not a performance review. Not a KPI. A genuine moment of recognition.

**Ewan's quote:** *"Look, you did unbelievably well there. That's great service."*

**The emotional arc:**
1. **Fear** — "They're recording me at work" (the universal staff reaction)
2. **Understanding** — "It's recording customers, not me" (the P1 framing)
3. **Experience** — "It caught something amazing I did" (the P7 moment)
4. **Acceptance** — "That microphone isn't so bad" (the trust shift)
5. **Advocacy** — "Did you hear what happened with my customer?" (the cultural shift)

**Visual suggestion:** Split-screen — traditional surveillance camera (grey, ceiling-mounted, impersonal) vs. the orange microphone (warm, visible, human). Caption: "Same technology. Opposite purpose."

## CONTENT ANGLE 2: "Consent as Empowerment, Not Bureaucracy"

**The hook:** Most consent processes feel like liability management. This one feels like being asked to share your best work.

**The story:**
- The dual consent chain: staff asked first, then customer
- Staff can say no — zero consequences, immediate deletion
- Staff choose: named ("Sarah made our day") or anonymous ("A team member went above and beyond")
- Customer can say no too — and the moment disappears
- If everyone says yes: AI writes the story, staff checks it, boss checks it, off it goes

**The key distinction:** This isn't consent-as-box-ticking. It's consent-as-agency. The staff member controls their narrative. They approve the words. They choose whether their name appears. They can change anything.

**Ewan's quote:** *"It's got to be celebratory."*

**The competitive contrast:**
| Traditional approach | Amplified approach |
|---|---|
| Company writes the review | AI drafts, staff approves |
| Employee has no say | Employee has total control |
| Consent is a checkbox | Consent is empowerment |
| Anonymous by default (company choice) | Named or anonymous (staff choice) |
| Company owns the story | Staff owns the story |

**Visual suggestion:** A flowchart showing the consent chain — at every decision point, the power sits with the individual (staff or customer), not the company. Every "No" leads to immediate, clean deletion.

## CONTENT ANGLE 3: "Real Moments, Not Fake Reviews"

**The hook:** In a world drowning in fabricated testimonials, what if your marketing content was actually real?

**The story:**
- 82% of consumers read online reviews before making purchasing decisions (BrightLocal, 2023)
- Consumers increasingly spot (and punish) fake reviews — FTC cracking down, platforms filtering
- The Positive Capture Loop creates authentic content from real customer moments
- Not "We asked customers to leave us a review" — the system caught a genuine interaction
- The content is specific, human, and warm because the moment was specific, human, and warm

**The content pipeline:**
1. Real moment happens (customer genuinely delighted)
2. AI writes the story (from real transcription, real emotion)
3. Staff checks it (adds their perspective, removes anything they're uncomfortable with)
4. Manager approves (quality check, brand alignment)
5. Published across channels — reviews, social, internal recognition, marketing

**The authenticity advantage:**
- "A customer at our practice was over the moon after their visit..." (real)
- vs. "We pride ourselves on delivering exceptional customer service..." (generic)
- Content from real moments is more engaging, more believable, more shareable

**Visual suggestion:** Side-by-side comparison — a typical corporate testimonial (stock photo, vague quote) vs. a Positive Capture Loop story (specific detail, genuine emotion, real context).

## CONTENT ANGLE 4: "The Antidote to Surveillance Anxiety"

**The hook:** Staff fear of workplace recording is rational. The Positive Capture Loop makes it irrational.

**The story:**
- Workplace surveillance is a growing concern — 78% of employees report discomfort with monitoring (Gartner, 2023)
- The fear is legitimate — most surveillance IS punitive
- Amplified doesn't pretend recording doesn't happen — the orange microphone is deliberately visible
- Instead of hiding the recording, the system gives recording a positive personal benefit
- "Why should I accept being recorded?" → "Because occasionally it catches you being brilliant"

**The psychological shift:**
- Traditional: Recording exists → staff are wary → trust is low → compliance-based acceptance
- Amplified: Recording exists → system celebrates wins → staff experience positive outcome → genuine acceptance → trust grows → recording becomes a feature, not a threat

**The self-determination theory alignment:**
- **Autonomy**: Staff choose whether to participate, how to be identified
- **Competence**: System recognises and celebrates expertise
- **Relatedness**: Celebration creates shared positive experiences within teams

**Ewan's quote:** *"When we hear something really positive, we listen more. We keep the data on phone or on device until we ask permission."*

## CONTENT ANGLE 5: "48 Hours or It's Gone"

**The hook:** The most trust-building feature of the Positive Capture Loop is what it deletes.

**The story:**
- When a positive moment is detected, audio stays on-device for maximum 48 hours
- Not uploaded. Not transmitted. Not backed up. Sits on the device, encrypted, waiting.
- If no consent obtained in 48 hours — auto-deleted. Gone. Irrecoverable.
- If consent obtained — content created, audio deleted anyway. The story replaces the recording.
- Either way, the audio is gone within 48 hours. No exceptions. No overrides. No "just in case."

**The trust mechanism:**
- "We could keep it forever, but we don't"
- The deletion IS the proof that this isn't surveillance
- Even the positive moments are temporary — only the consented story persists
- Staff can verify: the audio was there, the audio is gone, only the approved words remain

**The technical enforcement:**
- Hardware-backed deletion timer
- No code path to extend retention
- Maximum retention is a constant in the code, not a configurable variable
- Auto-delete fires regardless of system state

**Visual suggestion:** A timeline graphic — Detection → 48-hour window → auto-delete. Within the window: celebration, consent, content creation. After: only the approved story remains.

Every other system in this space starts with measurement and ends with management. Amplified starts with measurement and ends with celebration.

That's the story. That's the difference. That's why staff will trust the orange microphone.
