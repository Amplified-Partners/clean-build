# Today Mirror - Behavioral Personas

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2024-12-16  
**Purpose:** Define realistic user patterns for simulation testing

---

## Important Note

These personas are **NOT** based on proprietary personality systems (DISC, MBTI, etc.). They are derived from general behavioral patterns observed in research on:
- Task management behavior
- Decision fatigue and choice overload
- Present vs future orientation
- Commitment and follow-through patterns

---

## Persona 1: The Overloaded Operator

### Behavioral Profile
**Core Pattern:** Chronic overcommitment with low completion rates

**Research Basis:**
- Choice overload (Iyengar & Lepper, 2000)
- Present bias (O'Donoghue & Rabin, 1999)
- Planning fallacy (Kahneman & Tversky, 1979)

### Characteristics

**Task Creation:**
- Attempts to add 4-6 tasks per day (violates 3-task limit)
- Creates tasks reactively throughout the day
- Often adds tasks in response to external demands
- Rarely uses Recovery mode (sees it as "giving up")

**Mode Preferences:**
- **Money-First:** 60% of days
- **Balance:** 35% of days
- **Recovery:** 5% of days (only when forced by circumstances)

**Completion Patterns:**
- **Average completion rate:** 40-50%
- **Revenue tasks:** 55% completion
- **Delivery tasks:** 45% completion
- **Life tasks:** 30% completion (often archived)

**Typical Day:**
1. Starts with 3 tasks in Money-First mode
2. Attempts to add 2-3 more tasks mid-day (blocked by system)
3. Archives 1-2 tasks to make room for "urgent" items
4. Completes 1-2 tasks, carries over 1 task
5. Ends day feeling behind

**Failure Patterns:**
- Persistent Life lane neglect (burnout risk)
- High archive rate (30-40% of committed tasks)
- Mode-actual rarely matches mode-set
- Frequent mid-day mode switching
- Tasks often vague or too large

## Persona 2: The Recovery Seeker

### Behavioral Profile
**Core Pattern:** Under-commitment with high completion rates but low ambition

**Research Basis:**
- Avoidance coping (Roth & Cohen, 1986)
- Self-handicapping (Jones & Berglas, 1978)
- Fear of failure (Atkinson, 1957)

### Characteristics

**Task Creation:**
- Often creates only 1-2 tasks per day (doesn't fill all 3 slots)
- Tasks tend to be small, safe, easily achievable
- Avoids revenue-generating tasks
- Prefers familiar, low-risk activities

**Mode Preferences:**
- **Recovery:** 50% of days
- **Balance:** 40% of days
- **Money-First:** 10% of days (rare, usually external pressure)

**Completion Patterns:**
- **Average completion rate:** 85-95%
- **Revenue tasks:** 70% completion (when attempted)
- **Delivery tasks:** 90% completion
- **Life tasks:** 95% completion

**Typical Day:**
1. Starts with 2 tasks in Recovery mode
2. Completes both tasks by midday
3. Doesn't add a 3rd task (leaves slot empty)
4. Generates summary showing high completion rate
5. Feels satisfied but not challenged

**Failure Patterns:**
- Revenue lane avoidance (financial stagnation risk)
- Under-utilization of capacity
- Mode-actual often "mixed" due to incomplete task set
- Rarely pushes boundaries
- May use app as validation for inaction

## Persona 3: The Balanced Drifter

### Behavioral Profile
**Core Pattern:** Starts with good intentions but drifts toward low-value tasks

**Research Basis:**
- Ego depletion (Baumeister et al., 1998)
- Temporal discounting (Ainslie, 1975)
- Implementation intentions vs actual behavior gap

### Characteristics

**Task Creation:**
- Consistently creates 3 tasks per day
- Initially balanced across lanes
- Replaces high-value tasks with easier ones mid-day
- Uses LLM suggestions but modifies them to be easier

**Mode Preferences:**
- **Balance:** 70% of days (stated preference)
- **Money-First:** 15% of days
- **Recovery:** 15% of days

**Completion Patterns:**
- **Average completion rate:** 65-75%
- **Revenue tasks:** 50% completion (often replaced)
- **Delivery tasks:** 70% completion
- **Life tasks:** 80% completion (easiest to complete)

**Typical Day:**
1. Starts with 3 balanced tasks
2. Completes Life task first (easiest)
3. Replaces Revenue task with easier Delivery task
4. Completes 2 tasks, archives 1
5. Mode-actual shows drift from Balance to Recovery

**Failure Patterns:**
- Task substitution (replaces hard with easy)
- Mode drift (Balance → Recovery pattern)
- High completion rate masks low-value work
- Rarely achieves stated mode
- Uses archive function to avoid hard tasks

## Persona 4: The Money-First Striver

### Behavioral Profile
**Core Pattern:** High revenue focus with Life lane neglect and burnout risk

**Research Basis:**
- Goal pursuit and well-being (Sheldon & Elliot, 1999)
- Work-life balance research
- Burnout progression (Maslach & Leiter, 2016)

### Characteristics

**Task Creation:**
- Always fills all 3 slots
- Heavily biased toward Revenue tasks
- Views Life tasks as "wasted slots"
- Rarely switches out of Money-First mode

**Mode Preferences:**
- **Money-First:** 80% of days
- **Balance:** 15% of days (when forced)
- **Recovery:** 5% of days (crisis only)

**Completion Patterns:**
- **Average completion rate:** 70-80%
- **Revenue tasks:** 85% completion
- **Delivery tasks:** 75% completion
- **Life tasks:** 40% completion (often archived)

**Typical Day:**
1. Starts with 3 tasks in Money-First mode (2 revenue, 1 delivery)
2. Completes both revenue tasks
3. Completes delivery task if time permits
4. Never adds Life tasks voluntarily
5. High alignment between mode-set and mode-actual

**Failure Patterns:**
- Persistent Life lane neglect (burnout trajectory)
- Declining completion rates over time (fatigue)
- Resistance to Recovery mode even when needed
- Mode-actual consistently Money-First (no flexibility)
- May experience health/relationship issues (not tracked by app)

## Simulation Testing Goals

### What We're Testing

1. **3-Task Limit Enforcement**
   - Does Overloaded Operator hit the limit?
   - How does system handle attempted overcommitment?

2. **Mode Validation**
   - Do personas respect lane rules?
   - How often does mode-actual match mode-set?

3. **Completion Patterns**
   - Do completion rates match persona profiles?
   - Are there unexpected patterns?

4. **Language Compliance**
   - Do summaries remain factual across all personas?
   - Any guilt/praise language creeping in?

5. **Behavioral Drift Detection**
   - Can we identify Balanced Drifter's pattern?
   - Does Money-First Striver show burnout trajectory?

### Success Criteria

**System Integrity:**
- 3-task limit never violated
- Mode rules always enforced
- No guilt/praise language in any summary
- All summaries factual and neutral

**Persona Fidelity:**
- Simulated behavior matches persona profile
- Completion rates within expected ranges
- Mode distributions match specifications
- Failure patterns emerge as predicted

**Insight Generation:**
- Weekly patterns reveal persona characteristics
- Drift and burnout trajectories detectable
- System provides useful reflection data
- No false positives (guilt where none intended)

## Simulation Duration

**Short Run:** 30 days per persona (120 days total)
- Validates basic patterns
- Tests system stability
- Quick feedback loop

**Long Run:** 90 days per persona (360 days total)
- Reveals long-term patterns
- Tests burnout trajectories
- Validates weekly aggregation

## Principle Violations to Watch For

Based on [`PRINCIPLES.md`](PRINCIPLES.md), watch for:

1. **Choice Overload** - System must block 4th task
2. **Neutral Feedback** - No praise/guilt in summaries
3. **Self-Monitoring** - Patterns visible without judgment
4. **Environment Design** - Constraints work as intended
5. **White-Hat Only** - No manipulation of any persona
