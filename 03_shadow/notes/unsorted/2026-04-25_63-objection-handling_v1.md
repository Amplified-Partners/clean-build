# Phase 6.3: Objection Handling & Closing Scripts

## Objection Scripts

### Objection 1: "Sounds Too Good to Be True"

**Script:**
"I completely understand why you'd feel that way. When I first started optimising my own dental practice, I was skeptical too. Here's what I've learned: we don't promise overnight miracles. The 4-6 week timeline is real because we're working with your existing systems. The results come from plugging leaks that already exist - not from selling you something new. Mark's case study showed £45K in 90 days, but that was built on top of his existing patient base and processes. Would it help if I walked you through exactly how we'd do that in your specific situation?"

**Why It Works:**
- Validates their skepticism as reasonable
- Uses social proof (Mark) with specific numbers
- Offers concrete next step without pressure
- Doesn't over-promise

---

### Objection 2: "No Budget"

**Script:**
"That's a fair concern. Let me reframe it: our typical client sees a return of 5-10x their investment within 90 days. For the Standard package at £3,997, that's roughly £20K-£40K back. If you're currently losing £8K-£15K to late payments alone, this pays for itself in the first month. Also, we offer payment terms - 50% upfront, 50% on completion. Does that change the equation at all?"

**Why It Works:**
- ROI-focused framing shifts from cost to investment
- Specific multiplier (5-10x) builds credibility
- Payment terms reduce immediate cash impact
- Opens dialogue rather than closing it

---

### Objection 3: "Worried About Disruption"

**Script:**
"This is probably our most important differentiator. I built this business specifically because I hate consultants who disrupt everything. Here's what disruption looks like for us: zero changes to how you work, zero retraining of your staff, zero new software to learn. We automate the boring stuff in the background. Sarah's team didn't even notice the invoice system running for 3 weeks - they just saw payments coming in. Does that help address your concern?"

**Why It Works:**
- Directly addresses the core fear
- Provides specific examples of what disruption isn't
- Uses testimonial evidence
- Positions low-friction as competitive advantage

---

### Objection 4: "Think About It"

**Script:**
"Absolutely, this is a decision worth thinking through. To help you decide, I'll send over a brief proposal with no obligation. It will include specific numbers for your situation based on what you've told me. If after reviewing it you have questions, I'm happy to answer. If not, no hard feelings. Does that work?"

**Why It Works:**
- Gives them permission to think
- Commits to sending valuable information
- Sets clear next steps
- Removes pressure while maintaining momentum

---

### Objection 5: "Need to Talk to Partner"

**Script:**
"That makes sense - important decisions should be discussed. Would it help if I joined a call with both of you? Or I can send the proposal to both of you? Either way, I'd suggest we book the audit first - it's free and shows you exactly where your money leaks are. Even if you decide not to proceed, you'll have that information. What would work best for you?"

**Why It Works:**
- Offers flexible options
- Keeps the audit booking as next step
- Adds value even if they don't convert
- Removes barrier to progress

---

### Objection 6: "What If It Doesn't Work?"

**Script:**
"Fair question. Here's our reality: we've never had a client who didn't see results, because we only work with businesses where we can identify clear leaks. During the audit, if I can't find £8K+ in potential improvements, I'll tell you honestly and we won't proceed. We also don't take on clients who aren't a fit - if you're not ready or not suitable, we'll say so. Does that address your concern?"

**Why It Works:**
- Acknowledges legitimate concern
- Provides guarantee framework
- Positions honesty as core value
- Builds trust through transparency

## Closing Techniques

### Technique 1: Assumptive Close

**Use When:** They've shown consistent interest, answered questions positively.

**Script:**
"Great. Let's get you started - I'll send the invoice and we can kick off Monday. Does that work for your schedule?"

**Why It Works:**
- Assumes agreement and moves forward
- Natural flow from conversation
- Low pressure because it's presented as logical next step

---

### Technique 2: Alternative Close

**Use When:** They seem interested but hesitate on timing.

**Script:**
"Understood. Would you prefer to start Monday and have this wrapped up before month-end, or would the following week work better for you?"

**Why It Works:**
- Gives them control over timing, not decision
- Both options lead to agreement
- Creates artificial urgency without manipulation

---

### Technique 3: Takeaway Close

**Use When:** They're pushing back on price or value.

**Script:**
"If you're not 100% confident this will work for you, I should probably be honest - maybe this isn't the right time. I'd rather you wait than spend money you're not sure about. Let's reconnect in 6 months when things have settled?"

**Why It Works:**
- Shows you care about their success, not just sale
- Creates scarcity through limitation
- Often triggers them to commit because they don't want to lose opportunity

---

### Technique 4: Question Close

**Use When:** They're close but have one final hesitation.

**Script:**
"What would need to be true for you to move forward today?"

**Why It Works:**
- Identifies exact barrier
- Gives them chance to voice concern
- Opens dialogue to address specific issue

---

### Technique 5: Payment Plan Close

**Use When:** Budget is the main objection.

**Script:**
"Given the ROI, let me offer this: 50% now (£1,998.50) and 50% when you see results in Week 6. That way you're not out of pocket, and you only pay the second half if you're seeing the value. Fair?"

**Why It Works:**
- Reduces immediate financial barrier
- Links second payment to results
- Shows confidence in outcomes

## Follow-Up Sequence

### Day 5: Email - Questions

**Subject:** "Any questions about the proposal?"

**Body:**
"Hi [Name],

Just checking in on the proposal I sent over. I know you're busy, but I wanted to make sure you had a chance to review it and answer any questions.

If you're not the right fit right now, that's completely fine - just wanted to make sure you had the information you needed.

Best,
Ewan"

---

### Day 10: Email - Scarcity

**Subject:** "Quick note - two spots left this month"

**Body:**
"Hi [Name],

I wanted to let you know that we're currently at capacity for new clients - just two spots available before we pause intake for the month.

Given your situation, I'd hate for you to wait, but I also don't want to pressure you. If you're still interested, let's get you booked in the next few days.

If not, no worries - I'll check back in next month.

Best,
Ewan"

---

### Day 15: Personal Call

**Script:**
"Hi [Name], it's Ewan from covered.AI. I just wanted to check in - did you have a chance to think about the proposal? I know you're busy, so I'll keep this quick. Are you still interested, or should I not bug you any further?"

**Why It Works:**
- Personal call shows commitment
- Gives easy out ("don't bug me")
- Keeps it brief and respectful

---

### Day 21: Final Email

**Subject:** "One last thought"

**Body:**
"Hi [Name],

I've been thinking about our conversation. I believe we could genuinely help you get 10-20 hours back and recover £8K-£15K/month in lost revenue.

But if I'm honest, it sounds like you may have other priorities right now. Rather than keep reaching out, I'll leave this with you.

If you ever want to explore this, my number hasn't changed. Best of luck with everything.

Warmly,
Ewan"

**Why It Works:**
- Respects their time with final communication
- Reminds them of value without pressure
- Leaves door open without being pushy

- IF reply "LATE" → Invoice Automation Series
- IF reply "INVOICE" → Setup Guide Series
- IF clicked link but no booking → Re-engagement sequence
- IF no open after 3 sends → Alternative contact method
