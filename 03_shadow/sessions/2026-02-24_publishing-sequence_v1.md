# Publishing Sequence - Amplified Partners

**The Gary Vee Model: One Story → 20-30 Pieces → Published Everywhere**

---

## Daily Workflow

### Morning (20 mins) - Ewan
- Wake up, send voice notes to Sam via Telegram
- Document what happened yesterday, observations, stories
- Sam transcribes, asks follow-up questions
- Ewan responds (voice)

### Afternoon (10 mins) - Ewan
- Sam sends draft Substack post (2,000 words)
- Ewan reads, approves, or gives feedback
- Sam publishes

### Evening (0 mins) - Sam (Automated)
- Sam creates 20-30 micro pieces
- Sam schedules across all platforms
- Content goes out 24/7

---

## Publishing Order & Timing

### 1. PILLAR CONTENT (Foundation)

**Platform:** Substack
**Timing:** 5:00 PM UK time (end of workday, people reading evening)
**Format:** 1,500-2,000 words, long-form, personal, authentic
**Goal:** Deep engagement, build relationship

### 2. MICRO CONTENT (Distribution)

**Created from pillar, published in this order:**

#### **LinkedIn** (Professional audience)
- **Timing:** Tuesday/Wednesday 8-9 AM UK time (peak engagement)
- **Format:** 300 words, professional tone, business outcomes
- **CTA:** Link to Substack post
- **Goal:** Thought leadership, reach B2B audience

#### **Twitter/X** (Fast, punchy)
- **Timing:** Wednesday 10-11 AM UK time
- **Format:** 8-tweet thread, punchy, quotable
- **Hook:** First tweet grabs attention
- **Goal:** Viral potential, reach tech audience

#### **Facebook** (Story-driven)
- **Timing:** Wednesday 1-2 PM UK time
- **Format:** 500 words, casual, story-driven
- **Tone:** Conversational, community feel
- **Goal:** Reach broader audience, personal connection

#### **Instagram** (Visual + caption)
- **Timing:** 12-1 PM UK time (lunch hour)
- **Format:** 1 sentence or short phrase + image/quote graphic
- **Stories:** 6-7 slides throughout the day
- **Goal:** Visual engagement, younger audience

## Weekly Cadence

### Monday-Friday (Weekdays)
- **1 Substack post/day** (pillar)
- **1 LinkedIn post/day**
- **1 Twitter thread/day**
- **1 Facebook post/day**
- **1-2 Instagram posts/week**

### Saturday-Sunday (Weekends)
- **No Substack** (pillar content weekdays only)
- **Optional:** Instagram stories (behind-the-scenes, personal)
- **Lower engagement** (people less active on weekends)

**Total output:** 5 Substack posts/week + 20-25 micro pieces/week = 25-30 pieces/week

## Platform-Specific Publishing Rules

### Substack (The Pillar)
- **Length:** 1,500-2,000 words (sweet spot for engagement)
- **Structure:** Title → Subtitle → Story → Insights → Conclusion
- **Tone:** Personal, authentic, vulnerable, funny, honest
- **Attribution:** Always cite sources (radical attribution)
- **Publish:** 5 PM UK time (Tuesday-Friday best days)

### LinkedIn
- **Length:** 300 words max
- **Tone:** Professional BUT personal (not corporate)
- **First line:** Hook (3-5 words that grab attention)
- **CTA:** Soft ("Read more on Substack" with link)
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best time:** 8-9 AM UK time

### Twitter/X
- **Format:** 8-tweet thread
- **Tweet 1:** Hook (grab attention immediately)
- **Tweets 2-7:** Story beats, insights, value
- **Tweet 8:** Payoff + link to Substack
- **Tone:** Punchy, quotable, short sentences
- **Best days:** Wednesday, Thursday, Friday
- **Best time:** 10-11 AM UK time

### Facebook
- **Length:** 500 words
- **Tone:** Casual, story-driven, conversational
- **Structure:** Start with story, end with insight
- **Best days:** Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best time:** 1-3 PM UK time

### Instagram
- **Caption:** 1 sentence or short phrase
- **Image:** Quote graphic (pull best line from Substack)
- **Stories:** 6-7 slides/day (key points, swipe-through)
- **Best time:** 11 AM - 1 PM UK time (lunch hour)

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO:
✅ Document, don't create (raw, authentic, real-time)
✅ Post at optimal times (Tuesday-Thursday mornings)
✅ Be platform-native (different tone/format per platform)
✅ Radical attribution (cite sources, stand on giants)
✅ Radical honesty (personal stuff, vulnerable, genuine)
✅ Daily cadence (consistency > perfection)

### DON'T:
❌ Overpost (1 per platform/day max)
❌ Post on weekends (lower engagement)
❌ Use same content everywhere (platform-native required)
❌ Aim for perfection (authenticity > polish)
❌ Skip attribution (always credit sources)
❌ Post late at night (9 PM+ = low engagement)

## Success Metrics (What We Track)

### Substack:
- Subscribers (not followers - we don't care about ego)
- Opens (how many read)
- Engagement (comments, replies)

### LinkedIn:
- Comments (quality > quantity)
- Shares (reach)
- Profile views (discovery)

### Twitter:
- Thread completion rate (did they read all 8 tweets?)
- Retweets (reach)
- Replies (engagement)

### Facebook:
- Reactions (engagement)
- Shares (reach)
- Comments (connection)

**Key metric:** Are people TALKING to us? (Not AT us, WITH us)

## Emergency Protocol

### If Sam Goes Down:
1. Check `openclaw health`
2. Restart: `openclaw gateway --force &`
3. Verify Telegram: `openclaw health` (should show "Telegram: ok")
4. Manual post if needed (copy from staging files)

### If Behind Schedule:
- **Don't panic**
- Skip a day if needed (consistency > burnout)
- Sam catches up next day
- Quality > quantity
