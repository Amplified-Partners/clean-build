## Gemini

You can schedule posts on Threads (by Instagram) using both the native app (in many regions) and third-party social media tools.

Here is the breakdown of what you can schedule, the hard limits on files, and the strategic "optimal" numbers for engagement.

### 1. What Can You Schedule?
You can schedule the following content types on Threads:
* **Text Posts:** Up to 500 characters.
* **Single Images:** Vertical (9:16) or standard aspect ratios.
* **Videos:** Up to 5 minutes long.
* **Carousels:** A mix of photos and videos in a single swipeable post.

**How to Schedule:**
* **Natively (In-App):** Look for a clock/calendar icon or "Schedule" option in the post composer (feature availability varies by region/update).
* **Third-Party Tools:** You can use tools like Buffer, Hootsuite, Later, or Planable, which officially support the Threads API.

---

### 2. How Many Files (Images/Videos) Can You Include?

#### **The Hard Limits**
* **Maximum:** You can add **up to 20 items** (photos or videos) in a single thread/carousel post.
    * *Note:* This limit was recently increased from 10 to 20 to match Instagram's expanded carousel capacity.
* **Video Size:** Max 1GB per file.

#### **The Optimal Number (For Engagement)**
Just because you *can* post 20 files doesn't mean you *should*. The optimal number depends on your goal:

| Goal | Optimal Files | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quick Tips / Memes** | **1–3** | Threads is fast-paced. Users scroll quickly. 1-3 slides ensure your main point is seen immediately without requiring high commitment. |
| **Storytelling / Education** | **5–7** | This is the "sweet spot" for carousels. It allows enough depth to provide value (e.g., a "How-to" guide) but finishes before the user gets bored and scrolls away. |
| **Photo Dumps / Recaps** | **10–15** | Use higher numbers only for visual "vibes" or monthly recaps where specific details matter less than the overall aesthetic volume. |

### **Summary Strategy**
* **For maximum reach:** Stick to **text + 1 single image/video**. Single visual posts take up more vertical screen real estate and are easier to digest than a carousel.
* **For deepest engagement:** Use a **5-7 slide carousel**. The first slide must be a strong "hook" (headline), and the last slide should be a Call to Action (e.g., "What do you think?").
