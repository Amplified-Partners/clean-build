## What It Is

A structured method for finding non-obvious connections between ideas from completely separate domains and testing whether those connections produce something genuinely useful.

It is a prospecting tool, not a prediction engine.

> "You are prospecting, not expecting."

---

## The Core Principle

Rigorous enough to be repeatable. Loose enough to be surprising.

> "You're looking at things through squinted-up eyes so you can see things you wouldn't normally spot. If you concentrate too much, they disappear."

---

## What Destroys It

Making it mathematical.

This was proven. Two weeks of work produced a mathematically correct formula using stochastic mathematics, Taguchi, and four gates theory. The result was a complicated Kaizen loop. It already existed. It eliminated the surprise — which is the whole product.

**Do not mathematise the Pudding Technique.**

---

## The Prospecting Zone

Think of a production tolerance window. Inside the radius: obvious connections, no value. Just outside: non-obvious but plausible, close enough to test — this is where the Pudding Technique operates. Too far outside: a giraffe crossed with a donkey. Technically a mammal. Practically useless.

The rubric estimates how far outside the radius a pairing is. The lens moves the centre of the circle depending on whose situation you are looking through.

---

## The Sequence

1. **Taxonomy** — classify every document by structural shape, not subject matter. Neutral, repeatable, sparse. The same logical structure appears in law, biology, and business. The taxonomy sees that.

2. **Lens** — the client's specific situation, captured through the interview. Without a lens it is noise. The interview is not just the delivery mechanism — it is a core input to discovery.

3. **Score** — run document pairs through the rubric: friction, cost, complexity, improvement potential.

4. **Pattern match** — find the non-obvious bridges between domains.

5. **Test against the rubric** — run the pairing back through the same rubric. This is the bullshit filter, not a proof. It answers: is this worth testing in reality?

6. **Reality test** — conceptual pairings can be validated by reasoning. Process or code pairings must be built and run. The bullshit filter surfaces candidates. Reality is the final judge.

---

## What the Taxonomy Is Not

The WHAT dimension is not topic tags. Topic tags keep documents from the same field together. The taxonomy describes structural shapes that cross domain boundaries — that is what makes the matching possible.

---

## The Role of APDS

APDS automates steps 1–4 at a scale no human team could manage, then surfaces candidates worth human attention. It does not replace the lens. The lens remains the client interview.
